Consumers Are Paying More For Cars
 
 
According to the latest data from the US Bureau of Labor Statistics, car buyers are paying an average of 10% or more over sticker for new cars.
 
Some brands, like Jeep and Porsche, reach as much as 20% over sticker.
 
📉📈As the inventory shortage drags on, the national over-sticker price continues to climb to match demand.
 
🔎Experts suggest car shoppers widen their search for essential vehicle needs, or, if possible, wait for prices to come down.
 
Telling it like it is — Every week somebody in All Things Used Cars reminds us that consumers are going to remember who treated them how when this supply chain issue is over and done. These high water marks are a great time to deliver on the kind of transparency, customer experience, and community support that makes dealers a vital part of the nation.

California Finds The Sweet Spot
 
 
Don't worry about California's 2035 ban on new ICE sales. According to... California... it's actually a sweet spot.
 
👍A recent interview by the chair of the California Air Resources Board, Linda Randolph, shared how the decision was made with the current reality for automakers, infrastructure, and production in mind.
 
They say the 2035 date is actually less aggressive than environmental groups suggest but should still lead to a sharp cut in emissions that is realistic for automakers.
 
Jokes aside —  It does seem the state leadership has heard and cares about the concerns voiced by people who will be affected by this change. That is always a good start to finding middle ground.

AUTOMOTIVE
China’s Tesla Clone
 
 
🚗🚗Chinese automaker Changan Automobile has a new EV that resembles the Tesla Model 3 but runs just $26K.
 
💸Hours after the launch, the Shenlan SL03 had over 15K reservations. The state-owned automaker is working to deliver the reserved cars by the end of the year and ramp up production to around 15K per month.
 
🔋The "Tesla clone" comes with a 320-mile range and an option to upgrade to nearly 450 miles.
 
🇺🇸At $14K less than the Model 3, this car could be China's competitive edge as tax credit rules in the US become more protectionistic.
 
Telling it like it is — Affordability is a big barrier to EV adoption. We aren’t sure how China is able to price these vehicles so competitively, but it will be interesting to see the impact it has on the market.
TECHNOLOGY
Tech Companies Take On Food Waste
 
 
Several emerging tech groups are looking to eliminate food waste by connecting people to restaurants looking to get rid of food. One such app is "Too Good To Go," which allows customers to pay a minimized amount for food instead of it being sent to a landfill. While, "Flashfood" has a similar program for supermarkets.
 
Current estimated say between 35-40% of food produced is wasted. Programs like this could reduce or even eliminate such a tragic reality.
 
Telling it like it is — Less landfill. Less hunger. Less wasted budget. This is a pretty great idea.
