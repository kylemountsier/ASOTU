DEALERS
Forward Thinking Dealers?
 
 
Cox Automotive conducted a study in which 449 successful dealers were examined in an effort to gain insight into their successful practices. Though their definition of “successful” may be subjective, some good points were made. Here are a few of their suggestions from the Cox Automotive Forward-Thinking Dealership Study:
 
Have a plan. Every single car is essential, so be intentional about creating a long-term vision for inventory. Utilize dedicated resources to ensure you are always working towards the vision.
 
Embrace data. Allow data to inform every aspect of operations. Bonus points for optimizing and integrating data.
 
Be adaptable. Things don't have to be done the way they've always been. In fact, most advise against it.
 
Marketing is major. Dedicated marketing staff create quality content and maintain engagement — it's worth it.
 
Always go the extra mile. From personalized messages to streamlining the lending process… customers will remember. (And you'll get your steps in for the day😁)
 
Telling it like it is - We notice they didn’t mention those inflatable tube guys… their loss. What’s working for you?

Honda Accelerates Electrification
 
 
Honda's new EV division will strengthen and speed up the brand's electrification (like a fork in a light socket).
 
On April 1, 2023, the division will restructure the company by consolidating the strategy and development of electric cars, motorcycles, generators, etc., in the hopes of increasing business efficiency (not like a fork in a light socket).
 
The renewed effort to catch up to Tesla, BYD, and others in the EV global market may cause a shake-up, but will likely lead to better processes moving forward (exactly like after you put a fork in a light socket).

Lightyear Moves On
 
 
Lightyear, a Dutch EV startup, announced yesterday that it will halt assembly of its first EV just a few months into production. The flagship Lightyear 0 was introduced in 2019 as one of the first EVs with solar capabilities.
 
To Two (and beyond?) The Lightyear 2 is the startup’s new focus and will inherit all the advancements of the Lightyear 0, but it will be only a fraction of the price at around $40K. The company says they already have about 20K pre-orders from fleet owners, which is ahead of the 2025 production plan. As the abrupt cancellation of the Lightyear 0 makes evident, a lot can happen between now and then.
 
Telling it like it is- the biggest question that we’re all pondering is — what the heck happened to Lightyear 1?
