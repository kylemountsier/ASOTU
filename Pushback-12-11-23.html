EV Tax Credit Changes in 2024

Starting  2024 , electric vehicles with battery materials from China, including popular models like the Ford Mustang Mach-E, Tesla Model 3, Nissan Leaf, and Volkswagen ID.4, will no longer be eligible for the $7,500 clean car tax credit in the U.S. This shift aims to stimulate the American battery industry and reduce dependency on China's supply chain.

  • Ford Mustang Mach-E : Unlikely to qualify for the federal tax credit after 2023.

  • Tesla, Nissan, VW Also Impacted : Certain trims of these vehicles face potential reductions in tax credits.

  • Global Supply Chain Shifts : A strategic move to reroute the EV supply chain away from China.

A lot of people wait until the last minute to care about what DC says it's gonna do, because often they change their mind (or leaders) before it matters. 

Volkswagen and Renault's Affordable EV Venture

In  response  to the growing influence of Chinese EV makers like BYD and SAIC’s MG, Volkswagen and Renault are collaborating on a €20,000 ($21,500) EV. This venture, still in early-stage talks, aims to produce 200,000-250,000 units annually.

  • Renault's New Launch : 'The Legend', expected to start under €20,000 ($21,500).

  • Volkswagen's Entry-Level EVs : The ID 2all and the cheaper ID 1, with prices starting around €25,000 ($27,000).

  • Electrek's Analysis : Challenges in affordability and the timeline for these models, not expected before 2025.

The best product in the world isn't worth anything if nobody can buy it, but also the worst product in the world is worth a lot of people can't live without it. Seems we can't balance the two as a society. 

BMW and Mercedes-Benz's EV Charging Network in China

A joint  venture  between BMW Brilliance Automotive Ltd. and Mercedes-Benz Group China Ltd. aims to establish 1,000 stations with around 7,000 charging piles by 2026. The first stations, starting in 2024, will focus on primary New Energy Vehicle (NEV) regions.

  • Seamless Digital Experience : Featuring plug & charge functionality.

  • Sustainable Approach : Plans to use renewable energy sources where possible.

  • Nationwide Accessibility : Open to the public with exclusive features for BMW and Mercedes-Benz customers.

"Exclusive features for BMW and Mercedes-Benz customers" could be code for free Wi-Fi or "unthrottled charging speeds." Any bets on which one it will be? 

Tesla's Labor Challenges in Scandinavia

Tesla is  facing  significant pressure in Scandinavian countries to sign a collective bargaining agreement with employees. This includes blockades across Finnish ports starting December 20, and a recent Swedish court ruling against Tesla in a license plate delivery dispute.

  • Regional Solidarity : Unions in Denmark and Norway joined the strike action.

  • PensionDanmark's Divestment : Selling Tesla stock over labor issues.

  • Ideological Stalemate : Tesla's policies clash with the Nordic labor model.

It's easy to forget Unions sometimes support one another unless you remember, by definition, Unions are organizations that specialize in Uniting people. Apparently, the Scandinavian people are currently united in their desire to ruin Elon Musk's day.

Data-Driven Decisions

Giphy/CABL

David Long’s All Things Used Cars community was no-nonsense on Friday, getting straight down to business on how to cut the fiction and work the facts.

People often cherry-pick data to support their preconceptions, ignoring the full picture. It’s called confirmation bias, and it's a common trap in decision-making.

Simply Psychology

Battling Bias —

🛡️ Quality Data Collection:  Getting to the truth means diving deep into the data, not just skimming the surface. Be sure to look at the whole picture, not just the parts that fit a preferred narrative.

⚔️ Embrace Diverse Teams: They offer varied perspectives, helping to spot biases and connections that everyone may not easily see.

🛡️ Team Collaboration: Keep your data collection, analyst, and communications teams interconnected. This collaboration fosters questions about data origins and representation, identifying and neutralizing potential biases.

⚔️ Stay Alert to Unconscious Bias: Our position and background can unwittingly influence business decisions made. Recognizing and acknowledging these biases is the first step in mitigating their influence.

🛡️ Avoid Assumptions: Don’t just assume things are working as they should. Regularly inspect resources like your website for accuracy and efficiency.

Google's New AI Challenger: Gemini

Giphy

Google's latest AI breakthrough ,  Gemini , is here to rival OpenAI's GPT-4. Touted to surpass GPT-4 in  30 of 32 academic benchmarks , Gemini is a multimodal AI capable of processing text, images, code, and audio.

Gemini in Google Bard
Currently, Gemini is powering Google Bard, which is available in  170 countries . This AI comes in three sizes:  Ultra  for complex tasks,  Pro  for diverse tasks, and  Nano  for consumer devices like the Pixel 8 Pro. However, only Gemini Pro is available for public use through Google Bard.

Performance and Integration
While Google claims Gemini's superiority in efficiency and coding, especially with its  AlphaCode 2  version, its edge over GPT-4 is subject to debate. The real-world effectiveness of Gemini in practical tasks remains to be seen.

Future Plans
Looking ahead, Google plans to integrate Gemini into its various products, including  Search, Ads, Chrome, and the Pixel 8 Pro . Starting December 13, developers will have access to Gemini Pro via  Google AI Studio  or  Google Cloud Vertex AI . This launch marks a significant milestone in Google's journey in the competitive AI market.
