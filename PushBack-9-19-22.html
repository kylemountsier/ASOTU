Chip Shortage In Spain
 
 
🎵Hello, darkness, my old friend. 🎵
 
🐌Stellantis and Renault are partially halting production in Spanish plants over a shortage of chips.
 
🕰Renault will shut down one factory completely and cancel shifts at another, while Stellantis is canceling its weekend night shifts in one location.
 
🥈These are not the first recent production adjustments for the brands in Spain.
 
Telling it like it is — Not to disregard the challenge, but each week we see fewer mentions of a chip shortage, each with a smaller impact.

China Also Does Not Approve 👎
 
 
Last week we included a story about the EU trade commissioner having a few thoughts about the US's new EV tax credit rules. Today we woke up and found China also has some thoughts about being economically cut out of US automotive production.
 
Shots fired❓ A Chinese ambassador warned that proceeding with the new standards would harm both nations' interests and upset the deeply intertwined global supply chain.
 
Shots fired❗ In a more concrete claim, the ambassador said China would cut the US from the Chinese auto market, meaning American brands would lose access to the world's largest market if the parties do not identify and agree to some compromises.
 
TL:DR — “If you can’t handle me at my sell-batteries-in-your-country, you don’t deserve me at my create-most-of-the-world-automotive-parts.” -China, maybe

Offshore Floating Wind Farms
 
 
The quest for energy independence continues! The US borrows a play from overseas rivals by exploring floating wind turbine power production.
 
Unlike bottom-fixed turbines, the floating turbines can be launched into far deeper waters to collect unobstructed wind power from the miles of open space in the ocean.
 
The White House is targeting 15 gigawatts of floating power production by 2035.
 
Just thinking out loud — There is also a lot of direct sun on the ocean… 🌞
