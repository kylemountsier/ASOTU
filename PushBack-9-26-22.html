Ten Years Of Giving Back In Kansas City
 
 
Each year for the last decade, the Chevrolet dealership ad group in Kansas City has partnered with Chevrolet, the Kansas City Royals, the Military Warriors Support Foundations, and GameDay to bless one veteran's family with a brand new Chevy.
 
This year the Hood family received the gift. We reached out to Karl Kramer, president of the dealership group that supports the event, who explained they would offer the family a choice of vehicles and pay the taxes, ensuring the family receives something right for them.
 
He said the vehicles fall within a set price point which does not include full-size utility or Corvettes, but that the family could apply the gift to those vehicles allowing the recipient to upgrade by paying the difference. Quite a well-thought-out system!
 
Karl shared that the event is always rewarding, and he loves seeing the impact the gift will have on the family as they pay it forward to the veteran community.
 
Telling it like it is -  Cars, community, charity, and care: ingredients found in the recipe for continuing to shift the cultural perceptions of auto 😁

Ford’s New Plant
 
 
Construction of Ford's new $5.6B plant in TN is well underway. Production of electric trucks and batteries, in partnership with Korea's SK, will start at the plant in 2025.
 
Everybody is excited. Director of new footprint constructions, Eric Grubb, calls the factory Ford's blueprint for leading America's shift to electric vehicles.
 
TN Governor Bill Lee calls it the single-largest investment in state history.

NASA’s Poppin’ New Tech
 
 
Ok, here comes a weird one. We know you're ready.
 
Through contracts with tech groups, NASA has been developing low-Earth orbit habitats for commercial and, someday, recreational use.
 
One such contract holder, Sierra Space, has just completed a process called "Ultimate Burst Pressure" on its Large Inflatable Flexible Environment (LIFE) habitat.
 
Ultimate burst pressure may sound dangerous- because it totally is! They literally filled the flexible habitat until it reached its bursting point.
 
The resulting explosion showed researchers the space-house exceeds pressure safety requirements of 182.4 by nearly ten psi.
 
Confidence boost - It’s pretty cool this is exactly how all us non-scientists would have tested the strength of balloon houses too.
