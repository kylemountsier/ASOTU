Investing in the Future: Walser-Urban League Partnership
 
 
Walser Automotive Group teamed up with Urban League to support young talent in the Twin Cities. The partnership focuses primarily on adulting prep, including money and investment management workshops, navigating college admissions, skills training, civic/community service, and more.
 
The group's Walser Foundation will cover the fees for all 20 participants in the program this year, which includes a springtime tour of historically black colleges and universities, museums, and civil rights monuments. Walser says they're most excited about the opportunity to cultivate relationships and develop skills in the next generation of auto leaders.
 
Telling it like it is - The fruit of the future are in the seeds you plant today.

Ford Falls a Little Flat
 
 
Ford's been left feeling a little frustrated. Full-year profits were nearly $1.1B lower than anticipated, and their shares fell more than 6% Thursday. Ford pointed toward the usual suspects, chip shortages, supply chain disruptions, and slow production, to explain the dip.
 
Down, but still up. The company's overall revenue increased 16% to $158.1B in 2022, including a 17% uptick in the fourth quarter to $44B.
 
CEO Jim Farley remains optimistic and has outlined plans to slash prices and increase quality. Farley's plan involves investing heavily in electric/autonomous vehicles and sustainability practices to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050.
 
Telling it like it is - With all the past year's ups and downs, we're expecting Ford to announce a rollercoaster-filled theme park any day now.

Recall Round-Up
 
 
Some OEMs are feeling the heat as thousands of vehicles are being recalled due to various issues:
 
⚠️ Honda asked owners of about 8K vehicles from 2001-2003 to stop driving their cars until they could repair their potentially defective Takata airbags.
 
♨️ Toyota recalled about 16K of their plug-in hybrid vehicles because of a software issue with the high-voltage battery cooling system.
 
🚙 Kia recalls nearly 32K of their 2023 Telluride SUVs due to faulty second-row side airbags.
 
📷 Ford recalls 462K vehicles due to rear camera display failure. Guess we have to use our stupid necks again.
 
Pro move: Dealers can turn lemons into lemonade by using recalls as an opportunity to showcase the reliability and convenience of their service departments... of course if you have REAL lemonade to give the customers, that could really seal the deal. 
TECHNOLOGY
Tech Layoffs Spark New Opportunities
 
 
The move-fast-and-break-things tech scene is going through some growing pains. While layoffs never feel great, tech workers' skills are still highly sought after, making them well-positioned for new job opportunities. With a strong job market and a desire for diverse skill sets, many laid-off tech employees succeed in starting their own new businesses or pivoting to fields beyond the tech world.
 
Someone's loss is another's gain, said Dan Ives, a tech analyst and managing director at Wedbush Securities. "Highly skilled developers and software engineers won't stay unemployed for long, and the companies that snap them up will probably be those at the forefront of exciting new sectors such as artificial intelligence, electric vehicles, cloud storage, and cybersecurity."
 
Like automotive, the tech industry is constantly evolving. These changes ultimately present pathways for companies and employees to reassess their goals and begin fresh. The road to success in the tech industry is never a straight line, but one thing's for sure, skilled, adaptive workers will always find a way to roll with the punches.
 
Telling it like it is - Many of the skilled people entering the hiring pool right now will dramatically benefit the industry that pulls them in. Got your nets?
