Nearly three weeks after a suspected cyber attack shut down domestic factory production for a day, Toyota announced it would shut down most of its plants in Japan for several days next week. More EV Price Hikes
 
 

Over the last few weeks, both Tesla and Rivian have announced price increases on their electric vehicles. Ongoing supply chain issues deepened by the Russian invasion of Ukraine have raw materials such as nickel and cobalt further pinched. Many of these rare earth minerals are essential in creating the powerful batteries used to operate EVs. Still, other affected materials are needed to produce much-needed semiconductor chips. 

Welcome to the party. Chinese EV maker Xpeng Inc announced Friday they, too, will be increasing prices on their EVs. Depending on the model, the increase will be between $1,500 and $3,000. 

Peter Rawlinson, CEO of EV maker Lucid, said they would inevitably review their prices as the "great EV price hike" stretches on.

Bitcoin Mine Gives County a Headache
 
 

Nobody is a stranger to claims of the environmental impact cryptocurrency mining can have. For instance, lawmakers in the European Union recently drafted ecological standards to limit the energy consumption of crypto mining. But in Limestone, TN, a different kind of pollution has folks seeking options: Noise. 

WHAT? Washington County, where Red Dog Technologies' bitcoin plant operates, is suing the company and landowner for "immediate and irreparable injury, loss, and damage." People living near the factory say the noise of the machinery is so loud they cannot have a conversation in their yard. County commissioner Kent Harris says he voted to approve what was billed as a solar farm and data center and regrets his vote now.

Egyptian Banks Set Up Fintech Innovation Fund
 

Egypt's three biggest state-owned banks are setting up an $85M financial technology (fintech) fund. The fund's goal will be to accelerate innovation in the sector. Banque Misr, National Bank of Egypt, and Banque du Claire will be initial and critical investors as the fund seeks to attract additional participation from regional and international investors. 

 First steps. The fund made preliminary investments in four companies.
  • Khazna -  Financial app that provides products to underserved consumers. 
  • Lucky - Offers installments, cashback rewards, and credit to consumers.
  •  Mozare3 - Provides small farmers with input financing, markets, and tech support. 
  • Paumod - A digital payment provider.
