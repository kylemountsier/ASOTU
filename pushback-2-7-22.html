Ford's Preemptive Strive Against Price-Gouging
 
 
With the new all-electric F-150 Lightning heading to market soon, Ford has announced by letter to dealers they will not tolerate any form of side-dealing that could damage the trust consumers have in the brand and Dealer Body.

The Letter: "The Dealer shall avoid in every way any 'bait', deceptive, misleading, confusing, or illegal advertising or business practices", with selling "Premium early delivery" slots, access limiting ADM, additional reservations payments, and side deals being targeted specifically, the letter continues "if it is determined that your dealership is engaging in such practices, Ford Motor Company reserves the right to redirect that dealership's allocation of the F-150 Lightning for the entirety of MY 2022."

In order to offset the risk of potential pick-up scalping, Ford also offered support for a No-Sale Provision to be added to dealer closing forms so customers cannot buy, mark up, and resell the pickups when they release.

TECHNOLOGY
Google AI Wows in Program Writing Competition
 
 
DeepMind, a Google-owned artificial intelligence company, has announced a huge milestone for their AlphaCode project. The news comes as the result of 10 simulated contests in which the code writing AI ranked in the top 54% out of 5000, mostly human, competitors.

What is competitive computer programming? While it has become common for AI to impress onlookers with learning and problem-solving abilities, competitions such as these require more creativity and critical thinking of the simulated mind than the average problem they encounter in training. Typical problems in the contest include city planning puzzles and board game strategies where not only must a process be implemented but often invented by the competitor.

The AI's success has surprised the founder of Codeforces, Mike Mirzayanov, who ran the competitions saying he was skeptical but he can "safely say that the results of AlphaCode exceeded my expectations".

Amazon Prime Price Increase
 
 
Amazon announced on Thursday they will raise the price of their Prime service from $119 to $139 per year, the first price increase for the service since 2018. The nearly 17% increase will affect monthly membership price as well, bumping it from $12.99 to $13.99. The company said the price change will start on Feb. 18th for new customers, while existing customers will see the increase after March 25th.

The price increase comes on the back of ongoing supply chain disruptions and labor constraints, but also as part of the increased price for keeping Prime members renewing their membership with 2 day shipping, entertainment access, and other perks. The membership program is a driving growth factor for Amazon's core business as members spend more than non-members.
