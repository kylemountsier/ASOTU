DEALERS
All Things Used Cars Builds An Unstoppable Team
 
 
Last week David Long and the All Things Used Cars crew were joined by Andy Elliot to talk about building an unstoppable sales team. Good friends and new voices alike asked questions and shared their own experiences with leadership. Of course, our personal leadership hero, Liza Borches, was there and left us with plenty to write down. Check out our notes from Liza below, and listen to the full replay on Clubhouse.
 
The right people, in the right places. Anyone can be inspired, but inspiration only carries somebody as far as their hunger, humility, and smarts can reach.
  1. Hunger. As leaders, we’re here to help people get where they want to go, so we need to know what they want. Do they personally want to live up to the opportunity they have? 
  2. Humility. Loving cars is a bonus, but loving people and genuinely wanting to serve them in meaningful relationships is a must. Do they know serving is the actual path forward? 
  3. Smarts. We must collect dots before we can connect the dots! Early on, we have to personally, along with leadership, help employees connect the dots. Are we making it easy to connect their work to the bigger picture?

Too often, training is focused only on the front of the house. Equipping sales, managers, service, etc., for success starts with the ongoing training of every leader in your company.

Telling it like it is - Often, the skills that get somebody promoted to leadership aren’t actually leadership skills. It is nobody’s fault, but they are responsible for learning the new skill they’ve been trusted with.
AUTOMOTIVE
Used Car Depreciation Slows Again
 
 
Depreciation has slowed for another week in a row. Many segments had small value declines, while some even increased value. Here are some points from Black Book's Market Insights. 
  • Mid and full-size cars had the smallest declines, with -0.12% and -0.14%, respectively. 
  • Sports Car values increased for the third week in a row by +0.12%
  • Overall truck value decreased by -0.23%, which is slower than the previous week's -0.50% depreciation. 
  • Compact Crossovers rose +0.14%, the first increase since June 2022. 

Telling it like it is - The used car market is a lot of things, but boringly predictable doesn’t seem to be one.

AUTOMOTIVE
Scaling Down, Speeding Up, and Skipping Taxes (Not That Kind)
 
 
✄ Ford disclosed in a filing that it cut its stake in Rivian to 1.15%. At the end of 2021, the blue oval held 11.4% of the EV maker. Amid Rivian's falling market value, layoffs, and the expansion of Ford's electric vehicle project, it seems the two brands are heading for an amicable parting.
 
🏋 GM's Hummer EV is apparently checking nobody's boxes. At 9,000 lbs, the vehicle is so massive almost no view is unobscured, according to a review by the Verge's Emme Hall. She says that engaging the WTF mode (Watts to Freedom) is "super fun, but stopping is terrifying" as the 1000hp outruns the road quickly.
 
💸 Lucid will offer buyers a $7,500 credit to compete with US EV tax credits. Following recent Tesla and Ford EV discounts, the brand's credit applied to specific trim levels of its Air EV. At $87,400, the Lucid Air starting prices place it out of reach of tax credits. Until March 31, customers can take a little off the top.
 
TL: DR - Ford says bye to Rivian, GM’s new electric Hummer is big and scary, and Lucid says, “who needs taxes when we have discounts?”
