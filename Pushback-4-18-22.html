Manufacturing Rebound In March
 
 
Data for March shows US manufacturing gaining momentum for a third straight month. Industrial production in March jumped 0.9% over February and 5.5% year over year.
 
Automotive is "driving" the growth. After a 4.8% Drop in February, automotive part production shot up 7.8% in March. Total vehicle production rose to nearly 9.5M, the highest since January 2021.
 
Telling it like it is —  Manufacturing has been like roll-starting a car uphill. Maybe the engine has finally turned over?
AUTOMOTIVE
BMW Cautions Against Abandoning ICE Completely
 
 
BMW CEO Oliver Zipse says going all-in on EVs will lead companies to dependence on a few countries. ICE vehicles still have a market, Zipse noted, and if one company will not sell a customer the engine they want, another will.
 
Improvement over replacement. Current EV prices and gaps in charging infrastructure place EVs out of reach for some consumers. Zipse advocates against all-out ICE bans and insists companies improving fuel efficiency is more critical now for the consumer and the environment than shifting totally to EVs.
 
Translation  —  Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Look around, disruptions are bound to happen.

CRYPTO
Brazilian Parliament Preparing for Crypto
 
 
The Brazilian National Congress will vote on a bill that will regulate the country's crypto market. Two crypto bills previously approved by the senate will merge and receive a vote with hopes of being passed by June.
 
Decrypting. The bills seek to define crypto assets, combat crypto-based money laundering, and set state response mandates regarding fraud using digital assets.
 
Pushback Proverb —  When something gets harder for the few to abuse, it gets safer for the many to use.

Twitter Activates "Poison Pill"
 
 
A poison pill is a method used by a targeted firm to remove the appeal of a hostile takeover. Twitter announced one such plan Friday after Elon Musk's sudden offer to buy the social media platform.
 
Take two and call us in the morning. Twitter announced The Rights Plan to enable all shareholders to realize the total value of their investment. The plan opens the ability for shareholders to buy additional shares of common stock at a discount if a single entity buys more than 15% of the company without board approval.
 
Telling it like it is —  Elon Vs. Twitter is about to be a $90 pay-per-view event.-
