All Things Used Cars: Our Own Economy

Giphy/NBC

David Long and the All Things Used Cars community start every week with an economic breakdown by our friend John Ellis. The snapshot is never all roses or rain, but contains a different combination of the two from week to week.

This week David Long invites the community to consider what it means to "make their own economy" - to not bury their heads when John starts to speak about challenges, but to shorten the distance between learning and applying the best possible strategy for the market, economy, and industry as they shift.

David shared the thought starters: 

  • How can a company become impervious to a down turning economy? David thinks it is not a market thing but a strategy and intention that can be learned and duplicated.
  • What are the essential changes somebody makes when buying a new store? Can those same changes be implemented by current operators to bring about those same 2, 4, 8 fold improvements? 
  • What are we in this business for? David does not think it is to sell cars. Is it to make money? To help people?

Nissan workers in TN voted overwhelmingly against unionization. Of the 88 tap-and-die workers, 97% participated in the vote with 62 voting against and 9 for union representation. Nissan's spokesperson said they respect this decision, and the union rep says they will continue to support workers until the time is right to revive the campaign.

Speaking of "revival,"  a custom Nissan Leaf hearse offers a cozy, carbon-free ride to one's final resting place. The coffin transportation area extends from the dashboard on the passenger side to the back hatch, leaving just enough space for the driver. Since it drives silently, it is just as restful in the driver's seat as it is in the passenger's.

Giphy/Ludo Studio/ASOTU

Oh, thank heaven. 7-Eleven says it will build the largest charging network of any retailer in the US and Canada. The 7Charge project already has working DC fast chargers operating in Florida, Texas, Colorado, and California, but plans to expand across the US and later into Canada.

The company says the charger hardware will be compatible with the most common types and work with Tesla adapters. Always thinking ahead, the 95-year-old service giant launched an app to help travelers find charging stations.

However, with all these companies launching unique charging networks, we wonder: will there ever be an app to tell us which square mile of North America does NOT have a charger on it?

Giphy/Happy Madison

Despite getting past that big wall, GM and other American automakers are having issues claiming a piece of China. GM's earnings in the world's largest auto market fell 67% from their high point in 2014-2015. In the same period, GM's market share in the nation dropped from 15% to 9.8% in 2022.

While Covid has played a role in the decline, it started years earlier and has only become more complex in the last few years as both the US and China navigate economic and political tension. 

TL: DR - Nissan workers in TN voted against unionization, 7-Eleven is getting into EV charging, and GM is getting the worst of the China/US freeze-out.
