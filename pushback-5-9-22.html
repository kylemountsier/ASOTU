AUTOMOTIVE
Used Car Prices Easing
 
 
Cox Automotive data reports a 1% decline in used car prices from March to April. The move marks a 6.4% drop for 2022, but prices are still 14% higher than a year ago.
 
Appreciation for depreciation. Cox Automotive Chief Economist Jonathan Smoke says the market is returning to vehicles depreciating again. Vehicle prices coming down is good news for inflation and consumers looking to buy.
 
Telling it like it is —  It would stand to reason good news for consumers looking to buy is good news for dealers looking to sell.
AUTOMOTIVE
Battery Maker Eyes New Plant In US
 
 
CATL, the world's largest battery maker, is in the final stages of selecting a site in the US to build an EV battery plant. The Chinese company plans to support Ford and BMW EV production by placing their first US factory in South Carolina or Kentucky, where those OEMs already have assembly plants. Production will potentially begin in 2026.
 
Telling it like it is —  It’s a tough choice, SC has the ocean, but KY has *Googles*... 95% of the world's bourbon production…hmm.

Three Dimensional NFTs Are Coming
 
 
A partnership between Sony and South Korean blockchain operator Theta Labs claims they will design and release 3D NFTs using Sony's Spatial Reality Display technology. The project will include ten copies of a token named "The Tiki Guy."
 
Sony. Sony's 3D mixed reality goggles went on sale in 2020 and retail for around $5K. US-based owners of "The Tiki Guy" tokens can redeem them for a Sony Spacial Reality Device.

US Economy Adds More Jobs in April
 
 
Despite less optimistic outlooks in the stock market, the US labor market added over 400K jobs in April. The numbers mark 12 straight months of job growth of at least 400K.
 
Inflation. The hiring boom and steady wage gains will drive consumer spending and likely keep the Fed on track to raise borrowing rates to fight inflation.
 
Telling it like it is —  Yes this is good news, but the unemployment rate stayed the same. So were jobs created or just…wiggled?
