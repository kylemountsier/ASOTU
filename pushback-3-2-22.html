AUTOMOTIVE
Ford to Pause F-150 Production at Missouri Plant
 
 
Due to the global semiconductor-chip shortage, Ford announced they would pause production of F-150 pickups at their Missouri assembly plant for a week. The decision comes after the weeks of trimmed numbers at several plants. 

Is this goodbye? With Ford and GM recently introducing their first EV Pickup, some may see this as making space for a graceful exit. But the ICE F-150 is still produced at the Dearborn, MO plant and is expected to resume March 7th at the paused plant. 

Ford shares increased 4% the day of the announcement.

 
Telling it like it is - Are they announcing taking a break today to avoid announcing being out of chips tomorrow?

Classic Chevrolet Fleet and Commercial Repays Good With Good
 
 
For those of you who listen to the  daily podcast you know, but for everybody else we received some good news on the ASOTU positivity page!


Classic Chevrolet Fleet and Commercial of Grapevine, Texas, made one generous high school senior the proud new owner of a brand-new Chevy Spark. Classic Fleet donated the car to help address a chronic blood shortage amid the pandemic. Carter BloodCare hosted a high school pep rally to announce the winner after choosing one at random from a list of blood donors between November and January. 

While one student won the car, the whole community is rewarded each day by the generosity of the folks who call it home.

We wrote a song for the occasion: slide into the party like butter on a parka / handin' out new cars like my name is Bob Barker.

Dodge Fine Tuning The Sound of EV Muscle
 
 
Dodge said it will release its first battery-electric muscle car in 2024. They are confident consumers in their 30s and beyond are open to the idea of electric muscle. Before showing the EV concept this year, they are capturing the powerful sonic driving experience that people expect from muscle.
 
No samples of the sound have been made public, but Stellantis CEO Carlos Tavares has called it "... a sound you cannot imagine" and "shocking."
 
Telling it like it is - Imagine getting to select your own engine noise? Ours would be “Danger Zone” by Kenny Loggins.

CULTURE
Mardi Gras Returns
 
 
The first full-dress Mardi Gras since 2020 started Tuesday. With fewer Covid-19 hospitalizations, falling case numbers, and 92% of the city's adult population at least partially vaccinated, organizers and the crowds are ready to party. 

Back and better? Fewer police officers in the city have necessitated shorter parade routes. Some local businesses report ten-year high numbers, with hotels down almost 20% from 2020.

Bitcoin Hoards Spike
 
 
The number of bitcoin whales, wallets with over 1,000 Bitcoin, jumped nearly 5%, including the number of wallets with 100 or more also increasing 1.3%. Bitcoin valuation puts 1000 BTC at almost $50M. 

It is not clear what exactly has caused the spike. Still, some speculate that the recent financial sanctions on Russia could cause a sudden spike in cryptocurrency investment as the Russian Ruble falls below 0.01 USD.

TECH
California Permits Autonomous Vehicle Services
 
 
California Public Utilities Commission issued permits Monday to GM's Cruise and Alphabet's Waymo to allow self-driving passenger service vehicles. The vehicles must have safety drivers present, but will primarily operate autonomously between 10 pm and 6 am.

Baby steps. The step to a limited trial of the self-driving ride service comes after a period of test rides with no fare collection permitted. It is a step toward full automation, but a small one only covering San Francisco by Cruise and San Francisco with San Mateo by Waymo.
 
Telling it like it is - If California is cool with a new technology, we should probably get ready to hear more about it.
