DEALER STORIES
Kunes Auto's Trade In Hunger Event
 
 
125k! That is an astounding number for just about anything. It is also the number of meals donated to local food banks across four midwestern states, thanks to the efforts of Kunes Auto and RV Group.
 
Something worth competing over. The Trade In Hunger event was not only a massive collaboration by the dealer group but also a time-based competition to determine who could pack nearly 600 meals the fastest! The winning time was 21 minutes and 36 seconds by Woodstock Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram, while Green Bay RV took a more leisurely pace at an even 110 minutes. It all tastes the same in the long run, we're sure!
 
Telling it like it is —  We can’t think of any wrong ways to go about helping people experiencing food insecurity. Turning it into a contest that invites more people into the giving end of the equation has to be pretty high up on the list of right ways though.
AUTOMOTIVE
EV Batteries Outliving Expectations
 
 
Like really poorly designed boomerangs, Nissan says almost none of their EV batteries have returned for recycling. Many warranties cover 8 years or 100k miles. It seems more likely a battery will outlive its first car and go on to power a 2nd with 60-70% of its capacity remaining. This surprises many who believe recycling efforts would be a significant part of EV adoption.
 
Telling it like it is —  We had to include an Energizer Bunny reference because the batteries “keep going.” We don’t make the rules… our hands are tied here.

Products And Policies
 
 
Two US House reps are working to boost self-driving vehicle research and production. US Reps Robert Latta and Debie Dingell will introduce the Congressional Autonomous Vehicles Caucus to help educate fellow lawmakers on self-driving technology. As reps from automotive states, the pair believe it is vital to keep the US competitive in the self-driving market. 
 
 
Biden will sign a $52b chip industry subsidization plan today. The bill will provide semiconductor manufacturing and research funds, including $24b in tax credits for chip plants. Advocates of the bill see it as a way to protest the US economy, supply chain, and national security. Ahead of the signing, GlobalFoundries, Applied Materials, Ford, and GM leaders met with lawmakers to discuss possible funding applications. 
 
 
The Alliance for Automotive Innovation which represents several major automakers, says the recent extension of the EV tax credit program will exclude many existing EVs. The bill imposes restrictions on using Chinese-made materials by phasing in a required percentage of North American battery components. Right away, the program applies only to vehicles assembled in North America. The restriction jeopardizes the group's goal of reaching 40-50% EV sales by 2030.
 
TL:DR —  The US has big plans for the future of self-driving and chip production, but some OEMs think they forgot the part where they sell the cars they already have.
WHAT THE KIDS ARE INTO
Bluey Season 3
 
 
To say cartoons directed at young children are a dime a dozen would undervalue dimes. However, sometimes something special comes along that feels like more than a thinly veiled toy marketing campaign. Bluey is, at once, more pretty colors and silly jokes, but also family content that dives into some of the challenges of being young. Or, in our case, caring for the young people in our lives.
 
 The first half of the show's third season premieres on Disney+ on August 10th. It will feature 25 new short-form episodes that will entertain and empower children while making adults say, "They're just cartoon dogs. I'm not going to cry!"
 
Asking The Experts —  It's a top-shelf TV show according to Nathan’s nephew, Kyle’s kids, and Chris (who is a grown man).
