New Year’s Resolutions with All Things Used Cars
 
 
The last Friday of the year means the last episode of All Things Used Cars for 2022. On Friday, David Long and the community shared their New Year's Resolutions.
 
Listen to the whole replay on Clubhouse. In the meantime, we pulled our favorite 3 to share with you here.
 
Reduce the space between saying and doing. David Long started the room with a riddle: "If three frogs are on a log and two decide to jump in the water, how many frogs are still on the log?" The answer is three. Decisions are nothing without action.
 
Apply what you learn and act out what you intend.
 
Be realistic. Larry Feldman reminded us, "when you lie to yourself, nobody is there to correct you."
 
Take time to reflect, evaluate, and ask yourself difficult questions.
 
Don't ever give up on yourself. Bill Schomburg shared, "The six inches between your ears is the most dangerous place to isolate yourself."
 
Listening to the encouragement in a community like ATUC will help you remember your value, find the low-hanging fruit of improvement, AND continuously hone your craft.
 
Check out the recording to hear more from other community members, and send us your resolution for 2023 by replying to this email!

EU Welcomes Inflation Reduction Act Update
 
 
The European Commission gave a guarded welcome to the Inflation Reduction Act following the news that European-made commercial vehicles will qualify for Tax Credits without requiring changes to existing business models.
 
Guarded. The Commission remains guarded over concerns that the Act violated competition-protecting international law. A joint task force is looking into the concerns and possible solutions.
 
One final stipulation. While European vehicles/components are eligible for credits, companies must perform final assembly in North America.
 
Telling it like it is - I like to welcome my grass growing. It makes the fact that I cannot stop it easier to accept. -Chris

A Semi Truck Charging Network
 
 
Volvo and Pilot are planning to install EV Semi truck fast-chargers at Pilot and Flying J locations. The partners are working to determine which of the 750 service stations is suitable for chargers.
 
Telling it like it is - Volvo offers two brands of electric trucks, each with around 275-mile ranges, so we expect to see Pilot chargers every 250 miles or so. To help visualize that distance just think of roughly 2,640,000 iPhones.

Investing and Expanding
 
 
Stellantis is getting into the hydrogen game. While critically moving toward electrification, the auto group has started the process of acquiring Symbia to develop an exclusive hydrogen solution. The company plans to release hydrogen vans in Europe by 2024 and in the US the following year.
 
Their press release said they don't plan on sharing the tech. Which we assumed, but now that they have said it out loud, it still seems rude.
 
Faction is yet another driverless electric delivery vehicle company in San Francisco. What is unique about this one is that instead of being a small robot or regular car, the company uses a 3-wheel platform. They started deliveries for one company in July, but have focused on expansion. Currently, they charge about $2 per mile for deliveries.
 
Think of all the costs you'll save on that 4th tire!
 
TL: DR - Stellantis is investing in hydrogen, and Faction is investing in replacing the gig-economy.

Netflix Gets Fit
 
 
Are you tired of the insane amount of fitness content on YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and any other app that could create influencers out of people who are good at stretching?
 
Netflix isn't.
 
In an effort to recapture viewership, the streaming platform is adding content from Nike Training Club. Subscribers will have access to 90 workout videos to start the new year.
 
Of course, if Netflix's recent password-sharing policies have "complicated" your access to the service, you could check out one of Apple's 3000 guided workouts on Fitness+.
 
Telling it like it is - If you really want to get fit while you watch TV, just tape a 75 lb weight to your remote. You can also try this with your toothbrush, car keys, or favorite book - the applications are endless.
