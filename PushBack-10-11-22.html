Big Donations and Big Hearts
 
 
Mending the physical and emotional damage in the wake of Hurricane Ian will require more than money, but, of course, money is undeniably important.
 
Luckily, Qvale Auto Group sees the need and is cleaning up the mess Ian made with their money and hearts. After kicking off a season of giving with a $50K donation, the group announced from Oct. 8 to Nov. 12, they will donate $100 to the American Red Cross for every sale.
 
QAG says they are aiming for $100K to help rebuild through partnering with their customers and additional yet-to-be-announced donations.
 
Big fan - Sunshine or rain, the money spent with community-focused dealers still belongs to the community because that is #WhosDealersReallyAre - members of the communities where they operate.

Suspended in Michigan
 
 
❓ Pop Quiz: What was suspended in Michigan for concerns regarding "Imminent harm to the public?"
 
Nope, we are not talking about Full Self Driving this time. 🤖
 
👮 Michigan Department of State (MDOS) has suspended Carvana LLC in Oakland County.
 
The MDOS found several alleged violations while investigating no-title complaints at the location. 🔍
 
Violations include.
  • 112 failures to apply for titles and registration within 15 days of delivery. 
  • 3 separate occasions of a Carvana employee destroying title applications and all applicable documents and the car in question being taken back.  
  • Failing to maintain odometer records, properly issue temporary registrations, or provide records during reasonable inspection. 
  • Violating previously set probation rules 127 times. 

🔔 Due to the ongoing nature of the violation (multiple fines and probation extensions), the MDOS took emergency action.

Carvana says they are ready to fight back. According to a company spokesperson, they have addressed the state's issues and are being unfairly targeted. The company says the suspension ignored the 98% of its Michigan customers get their registration on time. 👩‍⚖️

Telling it like it is - ⚾️ How many strikes does it take to get kicked out of a whole state?

Parks and Rec and OEMs
 

Whats better than relevant and easy to skim news in your inbox? Relevant and easy to skim news WITH memes. Enjoy!  

 
Honda's Ohio plant let workers know last week that their bonus was too big. The leaders of the plant gave the workers the option of paying the company back or having the overpaid amount deducted from their paychecks until they were squared.
 
One affected worker placed the requested repayment at "hundreds of dollars."
 
 
Rivian's shares took a 10% hit after the EV startup announced a steering defect recall on more than 12K of its vehicles. Twelve thousand vehicles may seem like a drop in the bucket to a brand like Ford or Toyota, but for Rivian, it is nearly every single vehicle they have produced.
 
Only about 1% of recalled vehicles will be affected by the issue but they will all be checked. Investors are shook.
 
 
Nissan and Renault are hanging out without Mitsubishi, but we won't tell. Two members of the three-company alliance are talking about the future of their 23-year partnership. The balancing of control has become more important following the 2018 arrest and eventual flight of Carlos Ghosn, who previously held things togehter as chairmen.
 
Currently, Nissan owns a 15% stake in its French partner, Renault, but has no voting rights. On the other hand, Renault has a 43% stake in its Japan-based ally, Nissan.
 
TL; DR: Honda wants part of that bonus back, Rivian wants all of its EVs back (but only for a moment), and Nissan may want some of itself back from Renault.
