In The Spirit

Giphy/NBC

ASOTU enjoys a front-row seat for the generosity in dealerships across the US all year long. This time of year is special, and those people and teams who make giving a normal part of their culture go the extra mile in the name of the season.

Here are a few stories we wanted to share to get your holiday weekend started right:

Meals on Wheels

Foundation Automotive Group

The  Foundation Automotive Group  recently helped their local Meals on Wheels fill a room with ready meals for veterans and seniors. 

 

Toy Truck

Acura David (And Santa) aren't the only ones filling vehicles with toys. the  Berman Auto Group  filled the bed of a Grontier for the kids at the Haugan Elementary School! 

 

Angels on Wheels

Rydell

Rydell Auto Group  partnered with CVIC, Northdale Oil Inc., and Safe Kids Grand Forks for the Angels on Wheels event, giving away 12 vehicles to families! Like families, vehicles come in many shapes and sizes, so the vehicles were custom gifts that considered the family's size and needs. Good things are rarely "one size fits all!" 

 

The Grinch

Santa is for malls! The  West Herr Automotive Group  invited its community and families to meet the Grinch! His heart may have grown 3 sizes that day, but our appreciation for West Herr and all they do grew 4 sizes, so we win.

CAPTION CONTEST

AI Misadventures

Giphy/MTV

In a wild turn of events at Watsonville Chevy, an AI chatbot showcased its quirky side by offering a brand-new 2024 Chevy Tahoe for just one dollar.

X

Curious about this digital phenomenon, somebody tried it with another Fullpath-powered chatbot at Szott Ford, hoping for a similar deal on a 2024 Ford Bronco. While the AI declined our dollar deal, it showcased its helpful nature by suggesting alternative vehicles, showing off its sophisticated and responsive customer service application.

So, let this be a lesson to keep an eye on all this quickly moving tech. As we navigate this new era of AI-assisted service, one thing's for sure: it should be entertaining.
