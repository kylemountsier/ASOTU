AUTOMOTIVE

AI and Automotive

Giphy/Rusandroid

There are three areas to consider while considering the future of AI in the automotive industry . What is now, what is just over the horizon, and what our imagination can dream up. Remember, the first tablet computer was a Star Trek dream.

What is now:

Companies already use AI in the design, maintenance, and enhancement of owner experience.

  • 🦜 Mercedes-Benz has introduced GPT models in its Voice Assistant across 900K cars to assist drivers with inquiries and recommendations.

  • 🎨   BMW utilizes generative AI in its design process to generate many efficient, innovative, and visually appealing design alternatives.

  • 📸 Toyota Research Institute employs a generative AI technique, using text-to-image AI tools to expedite the harmonization of design and engineering considerations in their design workflow.

  • 🤖 Tesla's AI-powered autopilot system uses generative AI to understand and learn from various driving scenarios, improving its advanced driving assistance capabilities.

  • 🧠 Chinese startup Haomo.ai introduced DriveGPT, a generative language model-based platform that enhances decision-making in autonomous driving systems.

  • 🗺️ Waymo uses generative AI models to create thousands of diverse real-world driving scenarios for training its autonomous driving algorithms, improving safety and resilience.

What is getting close:

For now, we expect all practical applications of AI only to evolve as the technology is refined and real-world experiments are performed. Already new breakthroughs get closer each day.

  • 🎮 Digital autonomous driving testing grounds provide something so close to real-world testing that self-driving tech development will be safer and faster.

  • 🧑‍🍳 In-car software that knows the driver's   preferences . Route suggestions, service recommendations, seat placement, and radio stations, each set by a personal assistant that follows you from car to car.

  • 📻 Marketing campaigns that can build themselves .

  • 🤑 Save costs and time between designing, developing, and producing new models.

  • 👩‍🔧 Precise knowledge of vehicle health from tires to performance with real-time data about upcoming maintenance and even the results of hitting a nasty pothole.

For fans, it is an exciting time for automotive and AI. While others are left wondering if somewhere, buried in all the artificial intelligence, will be space left for authentic intuition.

AUTOMOTIVE

Motor Morsels: Tesla, Rivian, Stellantis

Giphy

📈 🏭🍕 Tesla's Q2 scheduled its Q2 results call for July 19th, but things are already looking great for the EV company.

  • Tesla's 466,140 deliveries put Q2 2023 at nearly double the same quarter last year.

  • At 479,700, the automaker increased production by 83% compared to Q2 2022.

While ramping up production toward the Cybertruck's release, Tesla also plans to open a new plant in Mexico . It seems to be readying Third Party App support, allowing users to customize their experience further. Like an Android, not an Apple.

Giphy

💪🚀🚚 Rivian's Q2 outperformed market estimates with 12,640 EV deliveries, exceeding the expected 11,000, leading to a 14% stock surge. These figures, reflecting a production increase at their Illinois facility, suggest At nearly 14K vehicles produced, Rivian is overcoming past supply chain issues and is on track to reach its annual production goal of 50,000 vehicles.

The US-based EV startup also shipped the first of its delivery vans to Europe as part of a deal with Amazon to electrify part of the fleet in the market. Following a year of dents and dings, the news suggests the company has found a pace that could carry it long-term.

Giphy/HBO

👏🚙🍝 Stellantis' Q2 sales scaled 6.4% on the shoulders of its newest models. While a far cry from the estimated industry forecast of 16-18%, the increase confirms pent-up demand just waiting for inventory levels to rise. The fact that the big sellers were a Chrysler minivan, a Jeep SUV, and a Dodge Durango also hints that, despite gas price woes, people are waiting for specific models and types.

Beyond Q2, the company is working out a $5.45M battery material deal with Australia's Kuniko and a $109M investment to electrify an Italian plant.


TL; DR – Tesla’s Q2 is unofficially great, Rivian’s Q2 is better than anybody expected, and Stellantis’s Q2 is good but not great.
