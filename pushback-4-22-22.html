Carvana Reports Larger Than Expected Q1 Losses
 
 
Online used car retailer Carvana shares have dropped 20% over the last two days and are down 80% from their 2021 summer peak. The purchase of ADESA US over the summer forced the company to sell $2B in common and preferred stock at a low price.
 
The numbers. Carvana's sales declined in Q1 2022 compared to Q4 2021, causing net losses to grow from $36M to $260M in a year.

COX Automotive Take-Aways From Q1 2022
 
 
COX Automotive released ten key insights from their Kelly Blue Book Q1 reports for 2022.
 
Insights: 
  • Toyota is still on top
  • EVs sales continue to increase
  • Only four brands delivered year-over-year sales gains: BMW, Genesis, Mini, and Tesla
  • Ford’s F-Series is still king
  • Fiat sold only 340 vehicles in Q1
  • Luxury brands grabbed more share in Q1
  • Tesla is still the dominant player in the EV market
  • Small vehicles are more popular than mid-sized, but the large are the most popular models
  • In luxury, new is important
  • Three segments account for 49% of US auto sales: Compact SUVs, Full-Size Pickups, Mid-Size SUVs

Telling it like it is —  No two markets are the same, but if you look the whole country as one huge market, some things stand out.

Toyota Invests $383M in US Production
 
 
Toyota is increasing investments in four manufacturing plants in the US. New investments support the production of four-cylinder engines with hybrid-electric options in Alabama, Kentucky, Missouri, and Tennessee.
 
Commitments. The announcement follows last year's $5.1B investment into American production, driving the company toward electrification. The company remains committed to producing vehicles where they intend to sell them.
 
Telling it like it is —  With all these productions investments going around, it seems when the inventory shortage breaks there will be no putting it back together.

GM Patents Self-Driving Car That Can Teach Driver's Ed
 
 
A patent published last week by General Motors describes a self-driving vehicle that includes a “method for driver training.” The car's onboard processor will judge its trainee based on their actions vs. what the autonomous driving software determines is best.
 
Don't say 2030... GM says they are still years away from fully autonomous driving, which would be required for the proposed system to work. The company also made clear no plan for making the robotic back seat driver a required feature in future models.

CNN Shuts Down Streaming Service a Month After Launch
 
 
CNN's new management has decided to shut down CNN+. Customers will receive prorated refunds, and CNN+ programming will move to the television network or a single streaming platform for all Warner Bros. Discovery brands.
 
Here today, gone tomorrow. CNN+ staffers will receive benefits and pay for 90 days to explore opportunities elsewhere in CNN or the Warner Bros. Discovery family. The company gave a few reasons for the abrupt change of course, with simplifying consumer experience playing a significant role.
 
Telling it like it is —  Companies try and end things all the time. Just usually not companies this big, trying things this public, and ending them this quick.
