Rise & Shine With the Women of Walser
 
 
Last October, the Women of Walser (WoW) hosted their first Rise & Shine Leadership Breakfast. The quarterly event invites all the women working in the Walser group to meet for encouragement and professional development. Last year there were 64 invitees and 35 attendees. A year later, the invitees grew to 75, and a mighty 50 came to share, learn, and grow!
 
WoW! GMs from the group supported the ladies, coaching one another on identifying and standing by their achievements and taking time to identify and honor the leadership qualities already represented in the group's female population!
 
The mission by the numbers. Women influence 85% of car-buying decisions. Women own over 50% of new cars. However, just 20% of the dealership workforce is female. WoW's work of bringing women into more automotive conversations is not only inviting needed perspective, but adapting to the market we are in today and the one we see on the horizon.
 
Telling it like it is - This is a fantastic opportunity for the people at Walser AND anybody who sees what they are doing and tries it in their own store. Great work, Women of Walser!

OEMs, Mexico, and High School
 
Learning is fun. Today we learned a lot about automakers' plans for the future through the lens of their investments and plans in Mexico. Let's take a look together!
 
 
Ford opened its new global technology and business center near Mexico City. The $260M campus will host the largest engineering center in Mexico, employ about 9,000, and serve as a hub for the company's global transition strategy.
 
Learning is fun - This is an excellent idea because Mexico is right there in the middle of the whole world (depending on how you hold a globe). #Geography
 
 
VW announced nearly $800M to be invested in Mexico between 2022 and 2025. The money will expand production in Puebla, home of one of Volkswagen's largest factories worldwide. While a proposed paint plant will run on wind power, the expansion will start by making ICE vehicles.
 
Learning is fun - Technically, the air is a mixture of gasses, so wind power is also gas power... #Science
 
 
Nissan will invest $700M over the next three years in its Mexican presence. The investments will boost operations through facility, equipment, and automation improvements. The company says Mexico is an essential part of the brand's strategy as a major market for sales and an export hub.
 
Learning is fun - If Nissan invests $700M over three years in its facilities, equipment, and automation, how much can we assume each welding robot's birthday party will cost? Show your work. #Mathematics
 
 
Tesla is rumored to have some Mexican aspirations planned also. The specifics of a potential investment are not public, and nobody is answering requests for comments. Nuevo Leon is home to one of Mexico's most prominent and wealthy cities, borders Texas, and recently hosted a meeting with Musk and US Ambassador to Mexico, Ken Salazar. Based in Austin, Texas, with an open lane between the US and Mexico, a Tesla expansion into Mexico makes sense.
 
Learning is fun - Elon Musk has several companies. In 500 words or less, tell us why this looks like a Tesla expansion and NOT a summer home for Elon's rocket collection. #LanguageArts
 
TL; DR - Mexico is part of North America. North America is where automakers need to build cars to get US EV Tax Credits. Oh, also, probably unrelated, a lot of automakers are pumping up their investment in Mexico-based production.
