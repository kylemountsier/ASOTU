Together with
 
 
 
“WOAHHHHHH,” yelled my kids as they ran around the house. With young kids, sometimes the best option is to let them watch the 18 houses set off fireworks surrounding our house from the second story. I was captivated by the awe and wonder they had as the dark sky was lit up with booms and brilliance!
 
Remember when you first felt that type of awe and wonder in auto!? For me, it was March 2010, just 6 months after starting in the business. The month when I was challenged and encouraged by a mentor to make this more than a job: to make it a profession. That month changed my life, my family’s life, and, now 12 years later, countless others’ lives.
 
It was then that I realized how great this industry is: how amazing it is to serve people as we sell and service cars; how great it is to impact the lives of employees around us; and, maybe most importantly, how great it is to be impacted by others in the industry.
 
Take time today to remember the awe and wonder you first had. Approach today with that feeling!
 
Keep the Wonder in Your Eyes,
-Kyle Mountsier

GM In 60(ish) Seconds
 
 
Lately, whenever we open the news, GM and CEO Mary Barra are popping off in every direction. Here is the most current news we could grab on the original member of the Big Three.
 
Dealing with it. Despite chip and logistic issues, GM outsold Toyota in Q2. Toyota surpassed GM's sales last year, the first time since 1931 that GM was outsold, but after taking the worst of ongoing supply issues, Toyota fell back out of first.
 
Sales numbers show a quarterly drop but a year-over-year gain.
 
Bottlenecks. Despite the healthy sales numbers for Q2, the company says they have nearly 100K vehicles waiting for final parts. The vehicles are expected to hit dealerships before the year's end, but Q2 profits took a noticeable hit.
 
12 down! GM reported they are only making about 12 Hummer EVs a day. The waitlist is about 80K which would take almost 18 years (whattt?) to fill at the current pace. The main source of the delay seems to be between GM and LG regarding batteries - a problem already being examined by GM's efforts to self-produce more batteries.
 
TL;DR — GM is an OEM. OEMs are navigating in a hurricane right now. GM shows no signs of sinking.

Shipping Delays Causing Inventory Backup
 
 
Labor slowdowns and strikes at German and Netherland ports are leading to multi-month delays on shipments carrying inventory bound for the US. In addition to enough home decor to stock an Ikea, the delayed containers are playing temporary home for lithium batteries, fully assembled vehicles, and a wide variety of auto parts, including chassis. Mercedes, BMW, and Ford were among the OEMs looking for available vessels.
 
Gridlock. A product director of Crane Worldwide Logistics, Andreas Braun, said that the strikes are playing a role, but even if they ended tomorrow, there are still months of chaotic backup to address. Some transports cannot drop off products at docks which pushes back their next pick-up, leading to a series of delays adding up to a significant delay. The delay has impacted every container's step from manufacturing to delivery in the US.
 
The sixth round of negotiations begins today to resolve the strike. 
 
Telling it like it is —  Dock worker strikes in Germany are preventing auto parts manufactured in neighboring countries from arriving in the US on schedule. Each day the delay drags on it becomes disproportionately more time-consuming to resolve.

TROUBLEMAKER TUESDAY
Crazy Problems Need Crazy Solutions
 
 
The ASOTU team started reading The Innovation Stack. Some of us for the first time and others for the 9th, but we are enjoying it for the first time in a whole team context. Jim McKelvey's insights into entrepreneurship, business, and journeying into the unknown immediately apply to anyone, especially someone who knows they have more to give but are unsure how.
 
Businesspeople vs. Entrepreneurs. An early takeaway from the book and more of McKelvey's content is a distinction between competing in existing contexts (business) vs. answering a question that has never been answered before (entrepreneur).
 
He challenges people to find the "Perfect Problem" they are the solution to and see it to the end.
