NADA Donated $25K to Vegas Food Bank
 
 
Continuing a long tradition of building up the communities dealers call home, NADA announced a $25K donation to the Las Vegas-based Three Square Feed Bank. Brian Burton, CEO of Three Square, said 1 in 4 children live in food-insecure households, and with the help of partners like NADA, they hope to bring that number to 0. 

Three Square is Southern Nevada's only food bank and the area's largest hunger-relief organization.

AUTOMOTIVE
Wholesale Used-Car Prices Down in February
 
 
Used-car prices are still high, but they are currently down 2.1% after hitting an all-time high in January. With circumstances pushing back new car production stabilization by an additional few months, automakers prioritize their supplies more cautiously. 

Down, but still quite up. Compact cars and van prices were still up nearly 50% in February 2022 vs. the previous year. Pickup trucks are only up 24.8% due to Ford, GM, and Stellantis focusing on their highly profitable trucks. 

Efforts by chipmakers such as Intel and TSMC seek to match the need by producing more. However, automakers are forming direct partnerships to resolve their own supply needs before the late 2022 expected improvement.

2023 Mazda CX-60 Leaked
 
 
Mazda's plug-in hybrid midsize SUV leaked hours before the official reveal. The images give a complete picture of the inside and outside of the vehicle and some expected performance details. The CX-60 is the first of 4 SUVs Mazda has planned, but executives have said not to assume it is an end for existing models.

Pop the hood.
  • New Rear/All-Wheel drive platform
  • Four-cylinder plug-in hybrid and 48V mild-hybrid inline-six petrol and diesel engines
  • LED daytime running lights
  • Large dashboard digital instrument cluster
  • The interior will mix wood, leather, chrome, and Japanese textiles.
The leak came by way of Instagram blogger and photographer, Wilco Blok.

CULTURE
Airbnb Becomes Unexpected Support Channel
 
 
Alternatives to traditional relief, such as cryptocurrency, are common in the Russia-Ukraine conflict. Since the invasion of Ukraine began, people have booked 61,000 nights in Ukrainian homes through Airbnb. The funds collected become available to hosts despite guests never staying in the room. 

Monday, Airbnb announced it would offer short-term housing to 100,000 refugees fleeing Ukraine. In the past, Airbnb provided similar relief to more than 54,000 refugees and started a refugee fund to continue to support the initiative.

TECH
Tech Start-Up Sues McDonald's for False Claims
 
 
California-based tech start-up Kytch has filed a $900M lawsuit against Mcdonald's. The suit comes after McDonald's emailed franchise owners advising them not to use Kytch's product. The product in question is remote monitoring software that informs ice cream machine owners of potential outages before they occur.
 
McDonald's, often playing the part of the place with the broken ice cream machine, claimed the software posed safety risks and could violate the machine's warranties. A spokesperson for the fast-food giant has called the suit "meritless."

Biden to Order Crypto Regulations This Week
 
 
The Biden administration has taken an interest in crypto directing agencies across the government to develop a regulation strategy. Regulation timetables have shifted due to the ongoing Ukrainian crisis as Russia attempts to use crypto to dodge sanctions. People close to the process expect an executive order on cryptocurrency this week. 

Can I take your order? The order will describe the role of government agencies in crypto regulation moving forward. The order may include the information about a US-backed digital currency after a January paper explored the risks and benefits.
