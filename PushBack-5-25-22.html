Google To Enforce Advertisement Accuracy With More Scrutiny
 
 
Starting in June, Google will tighten enforcement of advertisement accuracy. The company will ramp up a strike-based system targeting misleading ads, clickbait, personal loans, and potentially ill-intending paid content.

US Vehicles Hit Record Age
 
Hesitation to buy amid high prices has driven the average age of vehicles on US roads to a record 12.2 years old. The data makes five straight years of increased average vehicle age in the US.
 
Back to normal. While miles driven have bounced back to pre-pandemic levels, auto production has not. Rising costs caused by supply shortages have customers holding onto cars longer, which has driven up the average age, despite 15M new vehicle sales last year.
 
OEMs be like —  “Ok, but have you heard about my 2030 plan??”

Mercedes Moves Toward European Direct Sales
 
 
Mercedes announced it would cut 15 to 20% of its German dealerships. Internationally, the company will cut 10% of dealerships in favor of direct sales. The plan targets 80% of European sales through the “Agency” model by 2025, with 25% of total sales made online.
 
The company said they have no changes planned in the US.
 
Telling it like it is —  It is kinda hard to read they have no changes planned in the US without mentally adding a “yet.”

What is Wi-Fi 7?
 
Only recently, Wi-Fi 6 and 6E provided a significant improvement in latency, capacity, stability, and efficiency, but Wi-Fi 7 could quadruple known wireless speeds. The technology is still a minimum of a year away but promises an exponential jump in performance. Web3 technology like AR and VR that require high throughput and low latency stand to benefit substantially from the reduced congestion and interference.
 
Telling it like it is —  Finally, Wi-Fi fast enough to buy snacks in the metaverse you can actually taste!
