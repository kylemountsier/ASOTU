Lithia and Tesla Q3
 
 
Tesla's Q3 earnings call left investors feeling a bit uneasy. The company missed projected estimates, and Musk's performance on the call "didn't sit well."
 
The numbers. Tesla reported $21.45B in revenue, missing the $21.96 expectation. Earings per share, however, beat expectations at $1.05 per share vs. $0.99.
 
The Vibe. CEO Musk's answers to questions were described as curt and almost dismissive. Musk seemed focused more on making bold claims about the company's future and capabilities.
 
 
Lithia's earning call was a bit more optimistic. Continued expansion, increased number of sales, and steadily rising prices per new and used vehicle really add up to a positive earning call experience.
 
The numbers. Net income increased 6.9% to $330.0M, with revenue jumping 18% to $7.3B.
 
More numbers. New vehicle sales increased 4.3% to nearly 70K. Used saw a bigger bump of 6.4% to over 81K.
 
A few more numbers. Profits per new vehicle rose 11% to $5,789, while Used profits dropped 19% to $2,478.
 
TL; DR - Elon Musk is Elon Musk. Lithia’s expansive dealership network shows mostly good things, but may be hinting at used car value dipping.

Arrival Hops The Pond
 
 
EV startup, Arrival announced it will wind down in the UK in favor of US operations.
 
The refocusing comes as the company's stocks are at a low, and the company has just $330M cash on hand.
 
Moving operations to the US will place the company within reach of the Inflation Reduction Act's EV Tax Credit Rules.
