Market Research Finds Americans Prefer Dealerships
 
 
A survey by market research company Escalent found only 20% of non-Tesla owning consumers prefer a direct-to-consumer sales model. The majority of those surveyed prefer dealerships for financing, delivery, and fixed-ops, with nearly half valuing the dealership staff for vehicle educational needs.
 
Room for improvement. The survey also found customers want a hybrid of online and in-person shopping options.
 
Telling it like it is —  Consumers value the experience dealers offer and aren’t going anywhere, which means dealers are free to focus on the experience they are offering. #TheCircleOfLife or something.
AUTOMOTIVE
Mask Mandates Return to Many Detroit Auto Plants
 
 
Ford, GM, and Stellantis reinstated mask mandates in Michigan counties that the CDC has labeled "high-risk." The CDC recommended sixteen counties, each with multiple assembly plants and part manufacturers affected, across the brands. Ford has not announced an expected timeline, while Stellantis will start with a two-week mandate.
 
Telling it like it is —  If it’s good enough for air travel…er, scratch that. We just work here.
AUTOMOTIVE
Hyundai And Kia Climb The EV Ranks
 
 
Hyundai has sights on becoming a global EV leader by 2030. Q1 2022 saw the South Korean automaker take the number 2 spot for US EV registrations, leaving longtime rivals behind in switching from ICE to EV.
 
A distinct advantage. While it is still early in the EV market share race, Hyundai's 2nd generation EVs and the development of a unique battery architecture are capturing optimistic attention. 
 
Telling it like it is — So you’re saying the company who has been selling EVs the 2nd longest is the 2nd best at it? Somebody write that down, it seems important.

JetBlue Goes Hostile
 
 
An ongoing effort by JetBlue to acquire Spirit has taken a turn. Spirit has rejected takeover offers in favor of a merger with Frontier, but JetBlue is now appealing to shareholders. JetBlue has made an all-cash offer of $30 per share if shareholders vote down the Frontier merger.
 
Telling it like it is —  You may be having a rough day, but you’ll probably never have “an airplane company is mad at me” level rough day. So that’s nice.

Disney's Schedule For The MCU Leaked
 
 
A leaked schedule for all upcoming Disney Marvel content has revealed the next 18 months of movies and shows. Some titles have already made rounds, like Thor: Love and Thunder, but others are exciting peeks into the future.
 
Among returning favorites are Black Panther, Ant-Man and the Wasp, and the Guardians of the Galaxy with movies between now and May 2023. Some new-to-the-MCU faces include The Marvels, Fantastic Four, She-Hulk, and Ms. Marvel, among many others.
