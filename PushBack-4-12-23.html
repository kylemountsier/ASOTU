Hendrick Auto Helps Fight Hunger

Hendrick's Auto Group

Since 2014, Hendrick Automotive Group has been supporting the Second Harvest Food Bank of Metrolina in Charlotte, NC to help feed those facing food insecurity within their community. 

Last week, 60 team members from their Northlake Auto Mall rolled up their sleeves and spent the day packing over 3,000 boxes of food for their neighbors in need.

Hendrick's Auto Group

Their team also raised $385K to donate to the cause, which goes toward helping the organization in three key ways: a mobile pantry, a weekend food backpack program, and their bi-annual Hendrick Hunger Heroes Drive. 

Donations gathered from the Hendrick Hunger Heroes Drive  contribute to things like expanding Summer feeding initiatives and helping those particularly struggling with the impacts of the pandemic.

Telling it Like it Is - Thanks for all you do to tackle food insecurity, Hendrick’s! Filling the stomachs of the hungry fills our hearts with joy.

Hyundai’s Q1, GM’s Rivalry, Rivian’s Upgrade

Giphy/Nickelodeon

With a 16% increase, Hyundai set a new Q1 record for total sales. March alone had a 27% increase in total sales, marking five months in a row that they set a record for total sales. 🎉

🐭 Hidden Mickey? Hyundai announced a collaboration with Disney to produce a limited edition custom design IONIQ 5 EV. The platinum with purple accent design is a reference to the color scheme of Disney's 100 Years of Wonder celebration.

Hyundai/Disney

Other than a few references to the historic entertainment company - Disney start up animation, firework-inspired headlights, hidden Mickey wheel design, etc. - it mostly looks like a car. We were expecting more... Lightning McQueen vibes. Maybe our vision will be realized when Pixar turns 100.

Giphy

🥈 GM has surpassed longtime rival Ford on the road to EV dominance but still trails pack leader Tesla by... a lot.

Ford reported just under 11K EV sales in Q1 this year, while GM sold nearly 21K in the same period. Tesla sold over 400K .

How did those buyouts go? Layoffs loom over most industries lately, but GM CEO Mary Barra's voluntary buyout plan avoided the worst of it. 5K employees opted to accept the buyout, which will cost the automaker $1B, but tighten the budget heading into its big electrified plans. 💰

Robo-taxi drama. GM's driverless vehicle subsidiary Cruise is recalling 300 robo-taxis following an accident in San Francisco. A software error caused a minor collision with the back of a city bus, resulting in a damaged robot. Luckily nobody was hurt in the accident (because robots don't have feelings). 🤖

Giphy/Adult Swim

🔓 Rivian has unlocked a new Dual-Motor Performance AWD option for eligible order holders to select in the configurator.

The 700-horsepower option takes a second off the 0-60 time for an extra $5K. For context, the standard Dual Motor is 600 hp with a 0-60 of 4.5 seconds. 🐴

🚨 Back up... The EV startup is recalling some 5K of its R1S SUVs to replace inadequate reverse lights. Due to low visibility from certain angles, the recall covers every R1S made between November 19, 2012 and March 21, 2023. 

TL; DR - Hyundai works with The Mouse, GM takes 2nd, and Rivian lets people in on 100 extra horsepower.

AUTOMOTIVE

EPA Announces New US Goals

Giphy/Adult Swim

📈The EPA has upgraded the already ambitious electrification plans of the White House.

Wednesday, the US Environmental Protection Agency is set to announce a goal of reaching a 60% electric vehicle market share by 2030 and 67% by 2032.

The updated goal also exceeds many automaker plans, leaving a portion of EV sales open to whoever finds a way to claim them. 🦛

Giphy/Fox

🥊 "Everybody has a plan, till they get punched in the mouth." -Mike Tyson.

So where are automakers on their path to some sort of electric future? The answer is "various places, on various paths, with various end goals."

  • Honda says it will go zero emissions by 2040 with a 40% checkpoint by 2030 and an 80% goal by 2035. 
  • BMW has more than 10 new EVs coming by 2025
  • Ford says it will produce over 2M EVs per year by 2026 and electrify 50% of global sales by 2030. 
  • GM is going all-electric by 2035, but some of its brands have closer goals. 
  • Hyundai says it will be carbon neutral by 2045. 
  • Mercedes will be electric-only beginning in 2025. 
  • Nissan says it will be carbon neutral across the life cycle of all its vehicles by 2050. 
  • Stellantis aims to join the EV market in 2025, but be all-electric by 2028.

Plans announced over the last few years may be upended by the new US guidance, and we will let you know as they change. 📣

Apple's Q1

Giphy

🍎🍎🍎 Since the pandemic's start, personal computing companies have seen a dip in sales. Previously feeling the least pressure, Apple had a 40% drop in shipments for Q1 this year, the worst of the five largest computer makers. 

🍎 Year over year, Apple’s PC market share dipped from 8.6% to 7.2%, accounting for 2.8M fewer devices delivered.

In Q4 2022, the company had a 28.66% drop in revenue, so Apple leadership says the Q1 declines are not surprising. 🍎🍎

Pizza may help? Domino's has enabled ordering from Apple's CarPlay app. So you can order a pizza as easily as turning off a commercial for pizza that your favorite radio station is playing.🍎🍕🍎
