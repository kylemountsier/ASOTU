Ford Confirmed Battery Fire (Singular)

Giphy/Regal

Ford confirmed its F-150 Lightning production/delivery pause is due to potential battery fires.

During a pre-delivery quality inspection, one vehicle "displayed a battery issue" (translation: caught fire?). No fires have been reported by customers.

A spokesperson for the brand says they believe they have identified the root cause, but a full investigation could take a few weeks. 

Already reeling from a down quarter, the effects of the pause may linger over Ford for some time. 

Why do lithium-ion batteries catch fire? Smartphones and EVs alike have been named as the cause of spontaneous battery combustion in stories before. After reading a paragraph of technical information several times, we feel confident saying this: a battery catches fire when a stressor (internal short, excessive heat, overcharging) causes it to release additional oxygen, which provides food to a fire waiting to happen in the chemical, energy-rich environment of a Li-Ion battery.

Telling it like it is - Ways to prevent possible battery fires include not overstimulating, spending time in the shade, and plenty of rest between activities. Oh wait, that is the rules for taking care of a new puppy…

What’s the Deal with Cold Plunges?

Giphy/NatGeo

Getting ready to jump into the weekend? How about going for a cold plunge? DCE (deliberate cold exposure) seems to be all the rage these days, but is it really beneficial ? Or is Big Ice just trying to push their agenda? We decided to look into it.

🧘‍♂️ Potential Advantages:
-Pain relief. 
-Stress reduction.
-Boosted immune system.

🥶 Potential Risks:
-Cold shock.
-Physical incapacitation.
-Hypothermia.

Some studies suggest that there may be advantages of CWI (cold water immersion), especially when regularly repeated. Unfortunately, those findings are largely anecdotal, and there is yet to be any definitive scientific data to verify the claims. However, there are some very real cons that, especially when combined with water, can create potentially dangerous situations.

🌟 A few best practices:
-Never cold plunge alone.
-Clear it with a doctor if you have existing medical conditions.
-Studies say 50-59°F is the optimal temperature.
-Beginners should aim for no more than 15 mins. 
-Maybe don't do it at all?

Telling it like it is - We're still split. Are you team "DCE is the truth" or team "No thank you, please"?
