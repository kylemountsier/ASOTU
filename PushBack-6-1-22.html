From Our Readers
 
 
We released an article recapping the points made in an "All Things Used Cars" Clubhouse discussion on Monday. The conversation centered around the impact of new car markups on brand loyalty. We are grateful for your responses and want to ensure the whole story has room to breathe by bringing additional insights to the light.

David Saenz replied to the Push Back email to share his thoughts.

"I caught the recorded version of the discussion on over MSRP pricing. The one unintended consequence that no one brought up is the arrogance in both sales managers and salespeople with the take it or leave it attitude. I agree with you that there is no one right answer. We have been strong with our message of not charging over MSRP since the beginning, and it has served us well. I truly believe that the bad habits created will decimate some dealers when inventory returns at some level. I also understand if you were selling 500 new cars and now are getting 50 you need to pay the bills and pay the staff. I do a lot of mystery shopping because I have questioned myself if I am doing the right thing by leaving millions on the table. Many managers are behaving in a fashion that the DP is unaware of and just keep cruising on the yacht.

Just my two cents. Love the show and appreciate getting the point and counterpoint."

Telling it like it is —  We love hearing from you! What do you think about the article or David's observation?

Canadian Dealer Groups Are Buying US Stores
 
 
Seeking better growth prospects, Canadian dealership groups have started buying stores in the US. The expansion into the US reflects the uncertain changes that may accompany electrification and digital retailing in Canada. Canadians see the dealership investment protection in the US as an opportunity to return more on investments than they could in Canada.
 
Pun intended —  Come on down to America where dealerships have agency… no not that kind.
