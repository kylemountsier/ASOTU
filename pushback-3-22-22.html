Auto Service Delay Woes
 
 
Fixed ops departments are humming with traffic again. Mandates are lifting and people are growing more comfortable leaving home. Mileage accumulation is back at pre-pandemic levels, but ongoing supply chain issues and worker shortages show a ready supply does not always greet an increasing demand.

Data don't lie, yall. According to J.D. Power's 2022 US Customer Service Index(CSI) Study, wait times to book service for one to three-year-old cars have increased by nearly one full day. 

But the wait isn't the only factor driving the customer experience. Satisfaction levels soared 19 points higher for customers who used a valet system. Overall satisfaction year over year was also up in 2022. 

Don’t call us, we’ll call you. Chris Sutton, VP of Automotive Retail at J.D. Power suggested proactive communication for keeping the customer experience positive despite delays mainly being out of dealers' hands.

Ford F-150 Lightning Beats Target Battery Range
 
 
Ford has been generating a lot of buzz around its EV business. The CEO announced a split between EV and ICE after denying a division was in the company's cards just weeks before. Early previews of the company's first EV pickup were positive, with customers snatching up all F-150 Lightning reservations within eight months of being announced. 

Everybody's looking, now what? Ford announced the three-and-a-half-ton pickup would have a battery range of 230 miles with an extended-range battery option increasing it to 300 miles. A recent test found the extended battery reaches 320 miles instead, which is a feat, considering the truck's weight and significant drag area. 

Rounding out discoveries is the price. With the extended battery and Ford's BlueCruise hands-free driver assistance system, the Lightning will cost nearly $78K.

VW Secures Material Deal
 
 
Automakers are engaged in a scavenger hunt. They seek the next raw material source or supply chain collaboration that will set them on solid soil for a little longer until some global relief comes through.
 
Squad goals. Volkswagen has formed deals with two Chinese companies that may provide something more than temporary relief from the pinch. The $33B push will build a network of battery cell factories and secure direct access to cobalt and nickel. In the final stage of the expansion, the factories will source and produce 160 gigawatt-hours of EV batteries. The cooperation will reduce the battery cost by 30-50% by controlling the process from material sourcing to battery construction.
 
Squad grows. VW also announced a $7.1B plan to invest in EV production in North America through 2027. The company will introduce 25 new BEVs across the group's brands. They will also move to localize significant design and engineering responsibilities, meaning VW may produce EVs unique to the US, as consumer desires lead them.
