Rivian Rolls Back Price Hike

AUTOMOTIVE
Ad Impressions Shifts in Favor of Asian Brands
 
 
With football season and the Olympics over, automakers have adjusted TV advertising strategies. Toyota, Nissan, and Subaru topped the iSpot.tv list of most-viewed automotive commercials for the week of Feb. 21. US brands turned their TV ad focus on news, dramas, comedies, and basketball. In contrast, Asian brands found the most success during reruns of the TV show "Friends" and the CBS Morning News. 

Who wins and why? Toyota took the top two spots with ads ranking above average in "likeability" and "watchability." The data is inconclusive as to the cause, with ad placement and ad quality playing separate but essential parts.

Gran Turismo 7 Wows on PS5
 
 
Honoring its roots while taking a fresh leap into the future, Gran Turismo 7 is a welcomed return to a certain purity and joy the series was missing for years. 

What went wrong? A rigid focus on the technical jump from PS2 to PS3 led to well-made and visually advanced games that lacked the charm of the PS2 era. With PS4 bringing a tech rebuild that pared back the traditional GT experience. 

Better now? The jump to PS5 has given fans the best visuals, audio, and gameplay in the genre to date and brought back all the classic Gran Turismo play styles they fell in love with back on PS2.

Fitbit Recalls 2 Million Smartwatches
 
 
Fitbit will recall nearly 2M Ionic smartwatches due to battery overheating reports. The recall will affect 1M watches sold in the US, with another 693,000 sold internationally between September 2017 and December 2021.

Fitbit urged customers to stop using the device and contact the company to receive a prepaid package to return the device. Each customer will receive a refund of $299.
