Canadian Dealership Gets Creative With Giving
 
 
Every dealership we know gives back, but some do so outside the box, or in Toyota On The Park's case, "outside the fence."
 
Meet Lou. One such creative community support project has been the adoption of Lewis, Sweet Lou to his friends, 1 of 20 horses at Arva, Ontario's Sari Therapeutic Riding. The group offers horseback riding to people with special needs to grow physically and socially. No, the good folks at Toyota On The Park did not have to build a stable next to the used cars - The adoption is a yearly commitment to cover the care costs for the horse. Courtney Toms, the dealership's marketing manager, says they will continue to renew their support of Lewis for the foreseeable future.
 
This is just one of the out-of-the-box ways the dealership has partnered with other groups to create a brighter, more inclusive, and empowering environment for the people who call their neighborhood home. 
 
Telling it like it is —  Hats off to the people at Toyota On The Park! Normally we mean a flat bill hat, but for y’all we will make an exception🤠

Ford All Over The News
 
 
🔍 🔬Longer range and lower cost is the name of the game in EV battery production. Ford will extend battery range and cut costs up to 15% by adding phosphate cells to its cobalt manganese battery packs in Mustang Mach-E and F-150 Lightning models. The change will begin next year for the Mach-E and in 2024 for the Lightning.
 

💯👍600,000 vehicles per year by 2023. Ford assured its investors it has 100% of the battery supplies needed to reach the massive undertaking by the end of 2023. A combination of batteries from a Chinese supplier and efforts to lower the costs and material needed in production have the automaker on track to hit their 2 million EVs per year by 2026, according to CEO Jim Farley.
 


🕵 📉Anonymous sources have given Bloomberg reason to believe and report that Ford, the 2nd largest US automaker, with around 31,000 salaried employees,  plans to cut 8,000 US jobs. The cuts will begin this summer and focus on gas-fueled vehicle production workers. The anonymous tip follows Ford CEO's comments about reducing staff to fund EV production.

UK Plans Hydrogen Component Factory
 
 
The London-listed sustainable energy firm Johnson Matthey will invest $95m in a polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cell production plant. In case that sounds like gibberish to anybody else -the alternative energy source pulls hydrogen and oxygen from the air to produce electricity.
 
The factory will begin operations in the first half of 2024 with an annual production of 3 gigawatts.

Self-Driving: Sink Or Swim Edition
 
 
GM and Ford have asked US regulators for permission to release a small number of vehicles with a few missing features, namely the gas pedal, brake, and steering wheel. The companies want to release 2,500 vehicles yearly for ride-sharing and delivery services using only their branded self-driving technology.
 
The NHTSA admin Steve Cliff said the agency will carefully examine each petition to ensure safety is prioritized. The agency will also consider access for people with disabilities, equity, and the environment in their approval process.
 
Telling it like it is —  Riding down the highway with no steering wheel sounds like a stress dream to us. The NHTSA admin’s last name being “Cliff,” moves the situation more into short story foreshadowing territory than we like to go.

Stray Is Helping Real World Cats
 
 
Stray is a new game that puts players in control of a cat and drops them into a dystopian future. Since its release on July 19, it has become a hit among players and Twitch viewers. Several animal rescues and kitten-specific organizations have taken to the live streaming platform to stream their experience in the game while fundraising for their nonprofits.
 
Even the cats are digging it. In addition to gameplay, people have shared footage of their cats watching them play the game with fixated attention. Finally, humans and cats have something in common other than our love of lasagna.
