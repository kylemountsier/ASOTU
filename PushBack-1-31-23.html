Focus on the Road
 
 
"To teach is to learn twice." -Joseph Joubert
 
The Suburban Collection's 6th Annual "Focus on the Road" competition tasks students with creating videos to raise awareness about distracted driving.
 
One school received 2 special honors from the dealer group's contest. Aiden Coffer of Romeo High School took 2nd place and a $1,500 prize, while the school won $1,000 to the charity of its choice for having the most student submissions in the contest.
 
Michelle Nartey of Troy Athens High School won the $2,500 first place price, and Plymouth-Canton Schools' Eli Iacovoni claimed 3rd and $1,000.
 
Regional VP for Lithia Motors and the Suburban Collection, Mike Cavanaugh, said it's more important than ever to remind drivers of the danger of distracted driving.
 
Telling it like it is - Creative education drives lessons deeper than potential consequences. Great work to Mike and his team for giving student’s an experience that will hang with them next time they think a text can’t wait.
AUTOMOTIVE
Ford Makes Several Changes
 
 
Just a few months after price hikes up to $8,000, Ford is cutting the price of its flagship EV, the Mustang Mach-E, by ~$4,500. Ford says that they are staying competitive in the changing market. We imagine the market change most responsible is Tesla's recent price cuts. They also upped the Mach-E production target from 78K to 130K across North America and Europe.
 
What else is up with y'all? Ford is considering significant changes to its dealer EV program. Responding to claims the tiered certificate program breaks franchise laws, the company may alter three specific things so that both tiers match: 
  • Offering round-the-clock charging.
  • Marketing benefits for each tier. 
  • Allocation - doing away with the 25-unit cap for lower-tier dealers. 

Is it just us, or, from a certain point of view, does Ford seem more interested in beating Tesla than in serving its loyal customers/dealers?

Toyota Keeps Top Global Spot, GM Reclaims Top US Spot
 
 
Toyota was the top automaker by sales in 2022 for the third year. Including its smaller brands, the group sold 10.5M vehicles in 2022. Total global sales dipped 0.1% despite a 9.6% drop at home in Japan.
 
At 8.3M units sold, VW was 2nd for the year, despite its lowest sales in over a decade, due to the lasting effects of covid and supply issues stemming from the invasion of Ukraine.
 
Win some, lose some. While Toyota maintained its global title, GM reclaimed the top US sales spot.
 
Telling it like it is - Feeling optimistic about production cuts is easy when you outsell everybody. It’s like the best seat in the nurse’s office.

TikTok: To Ban Or Not To Ban
 
 
Tiktok CEO, Shou Zi Chew, will testify before Congress in the US over security concerns. A company spokesperson said they welcome the opportunity to set the record straight about TikTok and its owner ByteDance.

Before the March 23 committee testimony, a house vote may block the use of the app in the US. One issue facing the possible ban is the Berman Amendments, which protect the flow of ideas from hostile foreign nations. The Cold War era amendment was used to block the 2020 attempt to ban the app, and some members of congress still see it as the biggest hurdle.
