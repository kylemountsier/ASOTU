The Power Of One in Marysville
 
 
When Jim Colon and Petty Watson III wanted to open Marysville Toyota, they started by learning all they could about people's barriers and perceptions of car shopping.
 
They found people would rather not negotiate on price or be dragged off to the finance office to complete their deal. Finally, they want the whole process to move faster. Time is money, but more importantly - time is time.
 
The Power Of One. By leaning into remedying these findings, Marysville Toyota has grabbed the top Google score in the Metro Seattle area, a record market share, including repeat and referral business, and a No. 1 rating for Toyota Loyalty Engagement.
 
A less aggressive, more friendly environment appeals to buyers and employees alike. Which has helped the dealership build an open, professional, and women-centric vibe.
 
Telling it like it is -  It turns out people actually don't enjoy being pressured into buying a car. Also, a free idea - hire more women.

Tesla On A Hiring Spree
 
 
Tesla is stepping up hiring efforts in the US as recession talk looms.
 
Nearly every category of the company's workforce has job openings despite ongoing hiring. Currently, about 6,900 jobs are listed, down from the 7,400 high in May, but 50% more than in June this year.

Shipping Congestion Gets Some Breathing Space
 
 
Shipping congestion is finally starting to ease. Along with a reduction in disruptions comes a drop in shipping prices.
 
Chinese exports have slowed due to less interest from western importers. With one previously high-traffic location now slowing, container shortages and port congestion have the breathing space needed to catch up.
 
Shipping a container will cost nearly $3,700, down from the pandemic record high of $10K, but still 160% higher than the pre-covid rate of below $1,500.
 
Telling it like it is - Cheaper shipping seems like an effective way to combat inflations. *shoots a weird and confused look at interest rate hikes*
