DEALER STORIES
Liza Borches On Aged Inventory
 
 
Eliminating aged inventory is a hot topic right now.
 
Used car prices are dipping, so the price we paid last week for used vehicles loses profit margins daily.
 
David Long and the All Things Used Cars community talked about strategies for handling the moment we are in, and we took some notes from what the brilliant Liza Borches shared.
 
Expect diversity. Every store, every team, and every market is going to require a nuanced strategy. However, no matter the nuances, some fundamentals exist.
 
The right end user exists for every car. Moving inventory between your group's stores or out of your network could be vital to selling inventory while retaining more value.
 
Repricing with wisdom. Before changing the price, call every relevant lead to gauge interest. You may be able to sell it with a smaller price drop.
 
"You were right. We have adjusted the price and wanted to give you dibs."
 
30-day supply. Buying and selling within the same market will allow for greater control (and profits) as the market shifts.
 
More from Liza and the whole community is available on the show's full replay on Clubhouse.

Volvo Sees Electric And Hybrid Business Grow
 
 
Volvo's sales rose 12% in November. The company says that its electric and hybrid options are in high demand, with persistent supply issues throttling customer access.
 
Numbers:
  • Recharge models accounted for 42% of November sales, up from 32% in October. 
  • Of that, about half, 20% of total sales, were all-electric models. 
  • The 12% growth in November builds on the 9% growth in October. 

The company says they are learning to live with supply issues and will plan to make decisions by the week to keep up.

In the US. Of the 59K cars sold, 9K were in the US. Up 20% year-over-year, with nearly 30% of sales in the hybrid or electric models.

Welcome back! Anderson Bell once left Volvo for Tesla but is now leaving his post as senior director of engineering in charge of vehicle interior (wow, imagine the size of his business card) to rejoin Volvo. He will return next month and lead a team dedicated to computing, electrification, connectivity, and autonomous driving (the business card grows). This means he will facilitate realizing the OEM's decision to forego developing in-car software and begin working with Android Auto.

Telling it like it is - Why invest in your own in-car software if people are already waiting to buy your cars? Sometimes wisdom is knowing which projects to pass on for now. 🤔

AUTOMOTIVE
Toyota Captures Funding For Hydrogen Prototype
 
 
A hydrogen version of Toyota's Hilux pickup is coming to the UK if things go according to plan.
 
Toyota secured the funds needed to develop the fuel cell tech and will have a prototype built in a plant in Burnaston, England, during 2023.
 
Almost $14m has been raised between industrial funding and the UK government for the project.
 
Are they splitting hydrogen atoms to make a car move? No, splitting atoms is for atomic weapons. Hydrogen power captures electricity by adding hydrogen to oxygen, so the only "emissions" are water and warm air.
 
Telling it like it is - In some ways electric car power grew to a viable option under the legacy OEM’s noses, now several are looking to be the one to crack the hydrogen case first. It’s like watching people decide which grocery store checkout line they want to get in…
TECHNOLOGY
Trucks, Tractors, Trailers
 
 
Canoo is reporting for duty! The EV startup has delivered its first electric Light Tactical Vehicle to the US Army for testing. The US awarded the contract to Canoo back in July, and on paper, they nailed the prompt.
 
 
The LTV is extreme environment ready with stealth configurations and carbon Kevlar-based protection keeping the passengers and battery safe.
 
Monarch Tractor is serving back on the home front! The California-based startup is delivering AI tractors. The vehicles use NVIDIA chips to run real-time AI applications to perform farm tasks with no driver.
 
 
Two 3D and six standard cameras give the tractor location awareness in areas not covered by GPS or wireless connectivity- you know, like the middle of a hundred-acre field.
 
Polydrops will help travel between battlefield and farm even easier! Their P19 Shorty camper trailer could extend the Hyundai Ioniq 5's battery range by over 20 miles.
 
 
 
A short 28.5-mile trip showed enough energy use reduction that, if applied to the full battery charge, adds up to a nice aerodynamics-based battery boost. Of course, it requires strict adherence to a 55MPH speed, and that's after you strap the $10K thing to your ride.
 
TL; DR - EV Trucks go to battle, AI tractors stay home, and a wedge-shaped camper is making it easier to travel between the two.
