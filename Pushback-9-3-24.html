Q2 Haig Report Released

giphy

The latest Haig Report dives into the nuts and bolts of the auto market, giving dealers a clear picture of what‚Äôs going on in the field country-wide.

With some brands surging and others struggling, plus shrinking profit margins and a noticeable dip in dealership earnings, this report is packed with the kind of insights that can help dealers make smarter decisions in a market that‚Äôs anything but predictable.
 

Haig Partners / Automotive News

 
Car brands are seeing mixed results in year-to-year sales. While some are gaining traction, others are losing ground.

  • Volkswagen: Surged 31% in Q2 2024, bouncing back after a tough period.

  • Jaguar Land Rover: Up 20.8%, driven by strong demand for Defender and Range Rover models.

  • Mercedes-Benz: Dropped 11.8%, as its EV strategy struggles.

  • Audi: Down 12.4%, marking a significant decline among luxury brands.
     

Haig Partners / SEC Filings

 

Haig Partners / SEC Filings

 
Margins are shrinking, especially for new vehicles, while used vehicles show modest gains.

  • New Vehicle Gross Profit: Fell 26.9% from last year, averaging $3,447 per vehicle.

  • Used Vehicle Gross Profit: Increased 1.9% from Q1 2024, but still down 23% year-over-year, at $1,628 per vehicle.
     

Haig Partners & SEC Filings

 
Dealership earnings continue to decline from their 2022 highs, but still above pre-pandemic levels.

  • Q2 2024 Earnings: Averaged $1.0M in pre-tax income per dealership, a 35% drop from Q2 2023.

  • Annual Earnings: Over the past year, dealerships earned an average of $4.5M, down 27% from 2023. If current trends persist, 2024 earnings could fall to $4.0M, a 25.5% decrease.
