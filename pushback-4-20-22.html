Classic Chevrolet: Fun Runs, Golf, Wine, And Good Times.
 
 
Classic Chevrolet in Grapevine, TX, has hosted events and supported local charities for the last ten years. Funds raised from golf tournaments, wine tastings, and fun runs go directly to partner charities and are matched dollar for dollar by Tom and Susan Durant, the owners of Classic Chevrolet.
 
And counting… Since the first annual golf tournaments, the dealership raised a combined $3.6M with no sign of stopping.
 
In addition to their usual activities, this year, they have offered MSRP pricing on the highly anticipated Corvette Z06 to any customer who makes a $50K donation to one of Classic's charity partners. Eric Hall with the store told us they have already had 27 customers pledging contributions, with the resulting $1.3M distributed to the company's 13 charity partners. All that before the first golf ball leaves the tee.
 
Telling it like it is —  We want to make a joke about things being “bigger in Texas” but we are too busy nodding in approval. #GOAT

Ford Starting Early Delivery of EVs This Month
 
 
Despite an endless stream of delays and shortages, Ford will deliver the first of its F-150 Lightning EVs on April 26th. The company initially announced deliveries for the following month. Quick and enthusiastic reservation numbers drove the company to prioritize the truck and ramp up production beyond the original 150K expectation for the year.
 
It's a party, and you're invited. Ford will host a live-streamed party to commemorate the first trucks leaving the line. Viewers can see the event on April 25th at 1:30 PM EST on Ford's YouTube channel.

EV Sales Remain Strong Despite Battery Costs
 
 
Customers are still seeking EVs, despite steep price increases driven by international supply factors. Battery Cells cost an estimated $160 per KWH vs. $105 a year ago. The best-selling EV in China has increased costs by 30% in the same time frame.
 
Now and later. Q1 of 2022 saw EV sales increase by 120%. Experts project elevated battery prices for another year but expect EV demand to only increase in the meantime. 
 
Telling it like it is —  Evs are popular but there is also an unprecedented inventory situation at play here resulting in people paying more for vehicles across the market.

Federal Judge Struck Down CDC's Airline Mask Mandate
 
 
A Florida judge wrote Monday that the CDC exceeded its statutory authority by renewing mask mandates for air travel. The judge wrote that the word "sanitation" used in the law limits the CDC to cleaning measures and that a "mask cleans nothing."
 
Reaction. Administration officials say the judge's ruling means the TSA should stop enforcing mask mandates. Some seek to appeal the ruling, but many expect confusion in the short term, no matter the eventual outcome.
