 Together with 
 
 
 
Let’s go back…to the beginning of ASOTU:
 
“Market forces and companies are aggressively working to dismantle, siphon from, and smear the dealership model. That's why we are empowering dealers to push back through ‘tell it like we see it’ programming and resources that inspire dealers to band together so they can continue their important work supporting local communities and providing economic stability for generations to come.”
 
Following last week, we are even more committed to this mission and vision. If you found ASOTU back in 2020, at the height of the pandemic, you would have heard and read these words often. What we have come to realize, is that they have been the truth in our industry for more than just 2 years. Their power and intention is none more true right now as we face challenges in a whole new way. Challenges we got a chance to discuss firsthand last week.
 
We got the opportunity to hang out with some of the most incredible dealers and industry leaders at the VADA Convention in Asheville, NC. The takeaways for us have provided the energy we believe this community (you) needs to make a massive impact on the franchise retail automotive industry.
 
First, perspective. This is what happens when you are in the room with other voices. Whether it be a 20 Group, live stream, conference, clubhouse room, LinkedIn comments, or anywhere else you aren’t found in an echo chamber or silo, gaining perspective from others is important. The perspectives we gained last week have us being intentional about 2 words/ideas as we continue to bring together this amazing community: innovation and legislation.
 
Innovation: No doubt this is where we default in our conversations. Technology, people, process, and other forms of innovation as retailers is necessary as we seek to solve the customer and employee experience. We need to be communicating about the EV future, building brands that last, and creating raving fans in building business with the best possible plan for future proofing. The ASOTU community is always both looking over the horizon and keenly focused on the task at hand. Innovation is crucial to the survival and thriving of any business, especially retail automotive.
 
Legislation: Say WHAT!? You heard us (and maybe for the first time). The work that NADA and state conventions are doing to not just protect what is already in place, but also ensure a strong customer experience is incredible. Our conversation with Mike Stanton, President/CEO of NADA, and Liza Borches, President/CEO of Carter Myers Automotive, highlights some of the ways they are actively pursuing legislation that will allow dealers to ‘lead from the front’ when it comes to selling and servicing vehicles in the best way possible for consumers.
 
The challenge we came away with and want to encourage you with is to lean in and learn. Learn new ways to challenge the status quo. Understand better the work being done on multiple fronts. Continue to keep your eyes up and understand what is coming. Stay steadfast in your pursuit of the day-to-day workings of your role and business.
 
We are holding ourselves accountable to this mission. Will you?
 
Keep Pushing Back,
-Paul J Daly, Kyle Mountsier

Toyota and Suzuki Announce Hybrids
 
 
Toyota and Suzuki have announced regional production of hybrids in India. The companies say producing hybrids and not EVs in that area makes the most sense considering charging infrastructure. The Toyota plant in question will produce two vehicles with different power train options, one designed by Suzuki and a stronger one by Toyota.
 
The plan is the newest move in a partnership formed by the Japanese automakers in 2017.
 
Telling it like it is —   We would draw a map of all the global OEM/Policymaker/consumer desire energy going on but it would just look like a chaotic game of Risk where nobody knows the rules. (aka, the best kind of Risk)

Retailers Don’t Want Your Stuf
 
 
Watch out summer birthday friends, the new socks your mom still gets you may have to find a home in your home. In recent earnings calls, some of the largest retailers, including Target, Walmart, Gap, and American Eagle Outfitters, have reported an overabundance of stock that is costing them more money than their crypto wallets lost so far this year (ok, maybe a stretch).
 
Just keep it. These retailers are considering an unprecedented move to keep return stock away. Soon you may seek to return something only to have to keep it and get your money back at the same time. Online retailers like Amazon already employ these practices on low-cost and perishable items, but the stock issue at brick and mortar has never led to this type of policy.
 
Telling it like it is - Auto dealers be like: “yo, and you think you have inventory problems!?”
