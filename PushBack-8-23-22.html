Gates Toyota Meet & Greet Surprise
 
 
Last Saturday, the folks at Gates Toyota in South Bend, IN, hosted a Meet & Greet starring college football's Jack Kiser of Notre Dame. The event allowed people from Gates Toyota's community to meet and have their picture taken with the linebacker, grab a free poster, get autographs, and challenge #24 to games (not Football, of course, that would be unfair).
 
Surprise. While a meet and greet is a wonderful way for dealers to invite the community into their space, sometimes the most important meetings are not planned.
 
February 2022, Jack Kiser had the entire Notre Dame football team sign and sent a football to a member of his hometown who was preparing for an operation on his brain. Saturday at the meet and greet, the now recovered fan pointed to the scar across his head, explained who he was, and took a picture with Jack.
 
Telling it like it is —  Thanks for trusting us with your story, Jordan, and everyone at Gates Toyota!

A First, A Concept, and A Power House
 
 
👶 🚙 Acura's new ZDX is the brand's first full-electric model and will hit the market in 2024. The vehicle features the high-tech sporty look and feel of Acura's 2024 EV lineup. The performance SUV comes from a partnership between Honda and GM.
 
 
🤵 🚗 Ford revealed its vision for the future of its Lincoln brand. The Model L100 pulls inspiration from the automaker's first luxury vehicle, 1922's Model L. The company has no intention of producing or selling the model, just a concept that combines the brand's history with its vision of the electric future and beyond.
 


🏆 🏎 Lucid's new Air Sapphire Edition is a top-of-the-line sedan with a 0-60mph time of under two seconds. The car covers a quarter-mile in just nine seconds. As the new front runner in EV sedan power, the Sapphire has claimed the record for EV power from the Tesla Model S Plaid for Lucid.

TL;DR: Acura drops its first EV, Ford ponders the future of luxury, and Lucid leaves other EVs in the dust.

Another Day, Another Strike
 
 
More than 1,900 British port workers have started an eight-day strike. The strike is the latest effort by British workers seeking higher wages amid a cost-of-living crisis.
 
Import/Export. The affected port, Felixstowe, is the country's biggest port. 2021 traffic accounts for:
  • $44.2b or about 48% of all container imports. 
  • At $10.8b or about 25% of the UK's exports

The eight-day strike could disrupt more than $800m in clothing, electronics, and components, which will be routed to other ports if possible.

Telling it like it is —  This port’s main imports are toys, electronics, and furniture. Its main exports are scrap metal and medications. None of that directly impacts the automotive supply chain, but with these ships scrambling to find other ports, everybody will feel some of the disruption. 
WORLD NEWS
China Makin' It Rain
 
 
We recently wrote about factory stoppages in China caused by the hottest and driest summer since the country started keeping track 60 years ago. While the country considers closing factories for another week or more, others are watching the sky.
 
👽Watching the sky? Is this story about aliens? A record drought threatens the autumn grain harvest in the country. To protect the harvest, which accounts for three-quarters of their annual total, scientists plan to seed clouds with chemicals to increase rain. A second chemical is used on the crops to limit evaporation.
 
🥼But there will be lots of tests and such first, right? The plan is expected to take place soon, even if details are not being made public. Experts say the next ten days are crucial for resisting damage to the crop.
 
Telling it like it is —  Oh, look, another intervention in the natural cycle of things that will definitely not have any repercussions down the road. ⛈
