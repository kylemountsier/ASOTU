DEALERS
Happy Retirement, Mike Fahey!
 
 
Most dealer stories are easy to write. They have one subject and one event. This one is more challenging because there are two things to celebrate disguised as one big party.
 
Along with the rest of the Gates Automotive Group team, Michael Fahey is celebrating retirement after 41 years with the group.
 
In his time with Gates, it became common for clients to tell Mike what they wanted and leave the rest in his trusted hands. In response to his upcoming retirement, the group honored him with a party called Fahey Fest. Over 200 people, including associates, customers, and friends, came to pay tribute to Fahey's years of dedication and steadfastness.
 
It is Mike's retirement to celebrate, but Gates had the joy of seeing him off with gratitude.
 
Telling it like it is - Maybe we’re wrong, but it seems you don’t make it 41 years on the job without a strong character AND a good work environment. This is a win in both categories, to say the least!
AUTOMOTIVE
OEM’s Competitive Advantage
 
 
Tesla just had its best week since May 2013. A strong Q4 and 2M vehicle production target bounced shares 33%, reclaiming some of the losses in the last six months.
 
Other OEMs are taking note of Tesla's success and applying what they've learned. 
  • Many, like Honda, are moving toward at least partial online sales.
  • Some, like Ford, offer dealers an opt-in investment to be "EV-Certified," or wait until a later opportunity to buy in.
  • While others, like General Motors, tell brand dealers to buy in or move on. 

Why change so much so quickly? The growing interest in EVs has accelerated the pace at which dealers and OEMs will need new training, in-store tech, and strategies.

NADA Chairmen Geoffrey Pohanka said in his opening address to the industry that OEM - Dealer success is intertwined now more than ever. We don't always agree on how to do that," said Pohanka. Adding, "OEMs often don't understand how complex it is to sell a car…. Sometimes we have to remind our legacy OEMs that dealers are their competitive advantage."

Hit Reply - A relationship built on common goals > A relationship maintained by dealer laws alone? Tell us what you think the right path forward is for OEMs and Dealers.

AUTOMOTIVE
UK, Germany, Saudi Arabia
 
 
⛽️🔋
The UK. Despite a 30-year low in overall car sales, the UK broke its previous record for EV sales in 2022. 17% of new cars in the UK were EVs, more than diesel for the first time. Two-thirds are business and fleet sales, and Tesla holds the first and third spots on the list of highest EV sales volume in the market.
🛠️😬
Germany. Ford needs to make structural changes to increase EV production. Proposed changes to the brand's German plants have people fearing job cuts. Ford said they are willing to talk, and a German union official said the company would decide by mid-February how many jobs to cut in Europe.
📈💰
Saudi Arabia. Luxury EV maker Lucid saw an 88% jump in market value Friday due to speculation that Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which already owns 65% of the company, may buy the remaining stake in the startup. Shares ended the day up 43% after several pauses due to volatility.
 
TL: DR - EV sales surpass diesel in the UK, Ford’s ambition surpasses German workers’ comfort zone, and Saudi interest in Lucid surpasses their 65% stake in the company.
TECHNOLOGY
Mercedes-Benz Offers Self-driving And Tetris
 
 
Mercedes-Benz is bringing level 3 self-driving to the US.
 
The Fine Print. The Drive Pilot system is approved for use in Nevada but only up to 40 mph.
 
Nevada's statement makes it clear the state is down to clown. "Nevada law allows all automation levels to operate on public streets," a spokesperson for the state said.
 
Wide awake. The system will allow a driver to do other things (like play Tetris on the infotainment system) while independently operating, but if the driver's face is not visible, the system will disengage.
 
Telling it like it is - Technology comes and goes… Tetris is forever.
