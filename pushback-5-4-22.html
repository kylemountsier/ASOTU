Toyota EV Tax Credit Nears End
 
 
Toyota is the next manufacturer in line to use up its federal EV tax credits. The tax credit grants full or partially electric vehicle buyers a $7,500 tax credit, but only for the first 200,000 for each automaker. Toyota ended 2021 with 183,000 tax credits issued. 
 
Expanding instead of expending? Toyota, Tesla, and GM are lobbying for an extension while opposing an additional credit for unionized manufacturers.
 
Take away for Auto Dealers — It’s good to pay attention to the air war, but you will always win or lose based on the ground game YOU control.

Vehicle Deliveries For April Drop
 
 
Several OEMs report double-digit drops in deliveries for April. Each company pointed to ongoing supply chain issues pulling down day supply, leading to lower delivery volumes year-over-year. 
  • Kia dropped 16%
  • Hyundai dropped 20%
  • Toyota dropped 23%
  • Honda dropped 40%

Experts watching the situation hold to a return to normal inventory levels in 2023, but admit the balance between inventory and customer-facing inflation is delicate and can lead to unpredictable blips in the business.

Telling it like it is —  “[OEMs] have not failed, they just found another way to not [adapt to supply chain disruptions.]” - Thomas A. Edison (Modern interpretation)

India's Hydrogen Policy Draws Companies
 
 
India's Modi government has announced a national green hydrogen policy. The policy seeks to produce 5M tons of green hydrogen per year by 2030 and offers tax breaks and land to companies seeking to join the push. Those close to the technology claim hydrogen cars are the alternative to BEVs supply chain issues have revealed we need.
 
Green hydrogen. A process that separates hydrogen from oxygen in water creates an energy source that does not produce carbon. India has a massive coast and plenty of sun for solar power, so it is an ideal location to become the global capital of this still-emerging energy technology.
 
Telling it like it is —  Rocks Vs. Sticks, DVDs Vs. Blu Ray, Hydrogen VS Lithium, etc

Account-To-Account Fintech Startup Raises $65M
 
 
Lithuanian fintech startup Kevin raised $65M in a Series A fundraising round. The company aims to offer payment solutions that remove unnecessary intermediaries in the payment process.
 
Square, Venmo, PayPal, etc. While payment services are plentiful, Kevin has drawn attention for its ability to use the existing POS terminal infrastructure and widely used NFV contactless payment tech.
 
Telling it like it is —  You’ve got crypto trying to decentralize finance, centralized finance trying to adapt to crypto, and now Fintech startups trying to decentralize centralized currency. Wild world.

Facebook Shutting Down Podcast Offerings
 
 
Did you know you could listen to a podcast on Facebook? At least until June 3rd, you can.
 
Just one year after launching the service, Meta has confirmed they are winding down podcasting, soundbites, and its audio hub. Meta says the move focuses on the most meaningful experiences for its users.
 
Telling it like it is —  Finally, Facebook is going to focus on what really matters like staying in touch with grandmothers, liking pictures of people’s dinner, and some third thing we are definitely sure exists.

Toyota Teams Up To Protect Pollinators
 
 
About one-third of the food humans consume globally would not exist without pollinators such as bees, butterflies, moths, flower flies, beetles, and bats. Toyota is donating $400K to the National Environmental Education Foundation (NEEF) and Pollinator Partnership (P2) to enhance 26K acres of pollinator habitat across North America over the next five years.
