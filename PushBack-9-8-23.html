LEAP for Learning

Giphy/SoulPancake

The Ciocca Dealerships are always busy serving their community, but this month they have something exceptional to celebrate: the largest contribution ever received by La Salle College for LEAP (La Salle’s Early Achievement Program). 🏫

Ciocca Dealerships cranked up the generosity-o-meter with a $200K donation, affording high-school students the opportunity to participate in dual enrollment and earn credits free of charge before beginning college full-time. 📚

The CEO and engine behind the auto group, Gregg Ciocca said, “ Growing up in Northeast Philadelphia, the importance of community was ingrained in me from an early age. It's a value that's been at the heart of Ciocca Dealerships. When Mr. John Fries introduced me to the LEAP program at LaSalle, I was immediately struck by the impact it could have on young lives. It resonated deeply with our family's belief in education as a cornerstone for individual and community growth.” 🌱

Ciocca's commitment to the LEAP program is an investment in the untapped potential of knowledge-hungry minds. It proves that no dream is too big, no goal is too distant, and no hurdle is insurmountable when a community comes together to support the next generation. 💪

 
DATA & INSIGHT

UAW Strike Looms as Detroit Negotiations Intensify

Giphy/ASOTU

🌋  The Landscape 🌋

🐢 Detroit faces an industry-altering showdown.

👩‍🏭 The United Auto Workers (UAW) , representing 146,000 workers across significant automakers, is on the cusp of a strike against the Detroit Three: General Motors, Stellantis, and Ford .

🥩  The Stakes 🥩

🇺🇸   President Biden's 2024 reelection strategy is intertwined with the UAW's decisions.

🇨🇦 A simultaneous strike with its Canadian counterpart, Unifor , could mean irreparable inventory losses this year.

📉 A day-long strike might halt 25,000 units of production.

🤑  Potential losses for carmakers, suppliers, and workers could surpass $5 billion , with consumer car prices rising.

🪧  UAW's Firm Stance 🪧

🙅‍♂️ President Shawn Fain of the UAW remains determined .

🙋   97% of UAW members support a strike if no agreement is reached by September 14 .

📈 Significant wage hikes and other benefits are among UAW's core demands .

🥸 Charges have been filed against GM and Stellantis for allegedly not bargaining sincerely.

🌥 Glimpses of Hope 🌥

⚖️ Fain showcases flexibility, hinting at potential compromises.

🤝 Current negotiations indicate possible breakthroughs.

As the deadline looms, all eyes are on Detroit. The outcome could reshape the auto industry's trajectory for the foreseeable future. 🔮

Cutting-Edge Electric Concept Cars

Multiple European automakers revealed their all-new, all-electric concept models during the 2023 International Motor Show in Munich this week. Here are some of the highlights:

Mercedes-Benz

If you often find yourself forgetting what brand of car you drive, Mercedes-Benz has developed just the solution for you. By slathering the Concept CLA Class in every size, shape, tone, and texture of the MB logo, it will be impossible for you (or anyone else) to forget! 🙌

BMW

BMW unveiled the Vision Neue Klasse that’s touting a sophisticated integration of real and virtual worlds with advanced iDrive technology (which we assume means the ability to play Pokemon Go as you drive). Intended to encapsulate a “futuristic and friendly persona,” the interior boasts a cozy yellow and an etched lower side fender that reads: the “future is bright.” ☀️

Giphy/CNBC

Rolling back time at Munich's IAA show: the iconic " bubble cars " have gotten an electric facelift! Micro's Microlino and XEV's Yoyo are buzzing through European streets.

While it does look like something Austin Powers would drive, it feels like something a toddler could propel Fred Flintstone-style.

Volkswagen

Leading the charge as part of Volkswagen’s lofty goal to launch a variety of all-electric models by 2027 is this newbie, the ID. GTI. Not only does it pack a minimum of 223hp, it’s also coming in hot as the car with the most boring name in the bunch. 🥱
