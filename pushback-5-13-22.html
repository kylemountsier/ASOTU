Rivian's Q1 Up and Down
 
 
EV startup Rivian has been in the news a lot lately. Most recently, they announced their Q1 numbers. A loss of nearly $1.6B hit the company despite 10K additional orders in the same quarter. The company has reaffirmed it will produce 25,000 vehicles this year.
 
Leaking dollars? The company says the financial losses are due mostly to producing low volume on a high-volume line. The factory is designed for 150K annual output, and building slower than that raises the costs of each truck.
 
Telling it like it is —  Rivian is sitting on 90K preorders and working on a massive new factory in Georgia. What are billions of dollars in losses when you got a plan?

Volkswagen Eyes 10% of US Market Share
 
 
VW CEO Herbert Diess has announced plans to bring back the VW Scout brand as an EV. The resurrection of the SUV after 40 years is part of VW's effort to claim 10% of the US automotive market share.
 
They expect the EV Scout to go on sale in North America by 2026.
 
Telling it like it is —  Ever putting on a master class in brand development, VW moving a model out on its own as a brand definitely feels like a “Shots Fired” moment!

Google Drops Headphone Port, Apple Switches To USB-C
 
 
After joining the backlash against Apple for removing the headphone jack, Google will remove it from the new Pixel 6A. Google is not the only smartphone manufacturer to follow Apple's lead on removing the 3.5mm jack from the phone, but they were the loudest to protest its removal.
 
On the Apple side. Apple has also announced a change to their phone hardware by exchanging the Lightning port for a universal USB-C port. This change follows a few years of consumer and policymaker pressure.
 
Telling it like it is —  Google learned what customers will tolerate. Apple learned what customers want.
