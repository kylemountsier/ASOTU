Dealers Supporting Breast Cancer Awareness
 
It is ASOTU's honor to highlight the community support and partnership we see from dealers. This month is dedicated to Breast Cancer Awareness. We’ve been inspired by the compassion in your stores, on your social media, and in your communities. We wanted to pass some facts and the dealer pushback we see.
 
Fact: 1 in 8 women in the US will be diagnosed with cancer in their lifetime. While this statistic alone is difficult to conceptualize, the reality is that each diagnosis is a human being who may not have the support they need.
 
 
PushBack: Through the Gladiators Wear Pink foundation, Tubbs Brothers INC is donating to Michigan Faith in the Fight. The Michigan group provides women with financial, mental, spiritual, and physical support while they face cancer.
 
Fact: Detecting Breast Cancer while still at a localized stage (Cancer has not spread beyond the breast) increases survivability to 99% within 5 years. About 65% of cases are caught at this stage, but that number can be increased with education and regular screening.
 
PushBack: Both Speck Hyundai of Tri-Cities and Bird Kultgen Ford spent October providing access to these much-needed screenings. Speck Hyundai donated a mammogram for every car sold. Bird Kultgen Ford donated 86 free screenings to celebrate their 86 years in business.
 
Fact: Breast Cancer is the most common cancer for American women, except for skin cancer. Over 330K women and 3K men will be diagnosed in 2022. 45K of these people's lives will be taken by the uncurable condition. Through investing in awareness, more people survive.
 
 
PushBack: Ciocca Corvette will donate $10K from selling its one-of-a-kind pink Corvette. The donation to will fuel efforts to increase awareness and fund research toward a cure.
 
Telling it like it is - This is just a glimpse of what has happened this month in the dealer community. Send us your story and we will share it!

Hyundai Optimism, South Korean Frustration
 
 
🌎🍑 Hyundai's Global COO says their new $5.54B EV plant in Georgia could begin production in 2024.
 
🗓🏭 The original plan was for the factory to begin a year later and hit about 300K vehicles yearly.
 
📈👩‍🏭 Now estimates say that the Savannah-based battery and EV plant will eventually produce up to 500K annually if demand exists.
 
🇰🇷👨‍💼 News of the Korean automaker’s optimism comes as South Korean and US officials are working throught the implications of the Inflations Reduction Act.
 
💼🤫 A Korean ambassador said they are working to adjust the restrictive regulations, but did not share any of the several possible options being discussed.
 
Theory - If a strong used car business is an investment in future-proofing your relationship with customers (and it is), then preparing for EVs is how a dealer can future-proof their relationship with OEMs. Please let us know what you think? 🤔

Hydrogen Power At The Paris Auto Show
 
 
Hydrogen-powered vehicles captured attention at the Paris Auto Show.
 
The innovation of two startups have some taking another look at hydrogen.
 
 
Hopium showed off the performance achievable by hydrogen. Founded by a Le Mans 24 hour winner, they showed off a stylish 500 hp, 621-mile range race car with a max speed of 143 mph called the Hopium Machina.
 
 
NAMX SUV is the world's first vehicle powered by removable tanks used in the hydrogen power system. The Paris-based startup's SUV has a main fixed tank that provides a 311-mile range and up to 6 removable capsules, each adding 31 miles of range.
 
Telling it like it is - Who could have guessed water would come back as a viable power source all these years after the water wheel fell out of vogue.
