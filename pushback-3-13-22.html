Women of Color Automotive Network Wins Lighthouse Award
 
 
The AWA awards have recognized innovative individuals and groups in the auto industry for the last decade. The awards cover dealer services providers such as digital marketing, fixed ops, and business intelligence tools. But in addition to celebrating the vendors who enable dealers to do what they love, The Lighthouse Award recognizes leaders who demonstrate outstanding commitment to education, leadership, and innovation. 
 
This year's winners are Patrice Banks, Amanda Gordon, Erikka Wells, and Kerri Wise of WOCAN. They are four powerful voices in the automotive world seeking to attract, support, connect with, and empower women of color in the auto industry. The automotive industry is full of opportunity and stands to only gain as more women of color add their ideas and actions.
 
Telling it like it is - “You educate a man; you educate a man. You educate a woman; you educate a generation.” -Brigham Young

Ford Announces No New Allocations Until May
 
 

Ford had some bad news Saturday. Due to supply chain disruptions with 37 of their suppliers, they will have no new vehicles for dealers until May. The news comes just before an expected bump in sales as spring and tax time bring some health back to the market. 

Ford lost 100,000 units of production and expected to lose 12% of its yearly sales, which means about $1.7M. Andrew Frick, GM for sales, distribution, and trucks for Ford Blue, said they are trying all they can and still feel confident about their production flow to dealers.
