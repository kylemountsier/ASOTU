Online Car Shopping Not Slowing
 
Online shopping has grown more popular. In 2019 only 1% of all auto sales were digital. Driven by the pandemic in 2020, the number climbed to 10%. While covid has loosened its grip on people's options, many consumers still opt for the convenience of online shopping. 67% of customers surveyed said they would buy their next vehicle online if they could.
 
Telling it like it is —  OEMs like Ford are working on direct-to-consumer digital retail options. Dealers have an opportunity now to get a solid headstart on meeting the customers where they are.
AUTOMOTIVE
GM and Ford's Old Rivalry Goes Electric
 
 
What started as a cold war with Tesla has become a skirmish between GM and Ford. The race to dominate the US EV market has the historic rivalry spilling into plans and conversations around the two companies' electrification efforts.
 
Deep and wide. GM's CEO Barra has the company focused on a wide array of EVs that will be affordable with rapid production. Ford's CEO Farley has the company leaning into the EV pickup head start they claimed with the F-150 Lightning. Each company seeks to replace Tesla in the US market, but their diverse strategies are sure to produce equally diverse results.
 
Telling it like it is —  From a consumer's point of view, this has to look like a "Who can hug mom best?" contest in their honor.
AUTOMOTIVE
European Road Trip
 
 
🔎 👮Nissan and Renault are facing legal action in France over engine problems. Over 1,000 people are represented in a group lawsuit regarding issues with vehicles made between 2012 and 2016. Renault says they will examine paying compensation to affected customers but not engage in collective negotiation.
 
🤝 📈Polestar and Gore Guggenheim expect to close their Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC) deal this week. The merger will open the Swedish automaker to public trading in the US. Once approved, Polestar will start trading on Nasdaq on June 24.
 
⛽🤨German lawmakers have rejected the EU's plans to ban new ICE vehicle sales by 2035. Finance Minister Christian Linder says the company plans to be a leader in the EV market, but a niche for ICE vehicles will continue, so the ban is wrong. 
 
TL;DR — Renault may be paying a fine, you can buy Polestar stocks later this week, and German OEMs may never stop making ICE vehicles.

A Digital Place To Rest
 
 
Web3 often feels more like a buzzword than an actual concept with a real impact on our lives. Predictions say a majority of business and socializing could be based in the metaverse within a decade, but some people are finding a different use for virtual reality technology - meditation.

WHAT THE KIDS ARE INTO
LEGO Con 2022
 
 
In 90 years, LEGO has produced some iconic sets and rich imaginations. This year the LEGO Con showed off some returning classics and brand new products.
 
Returning favorites. The classic castle and space sets are coming back. Originally produced in the 70s and 80s, the Lion Knight's castle and Galaxy Explorer sets will go on sale in August.
 
Crossovers. Minecraft, Mario, Star Wars, Avatar, and Dr. Strange are getting LEGO-ified. If pairing with popular culture isn't enough, they are also branching into home decor. The LEGO Floran Art set is the newest in the company's exploration of single-stud DIY wall art.
