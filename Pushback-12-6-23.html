Charging

New Wireless Charging Roadway in Detroit:

  • Location:  A quarter-mile segment of 14th Street, west of downtown Detroit.

  • Technology:  Copper inductive charging coils installed beneath the street.

  • Function:   Charges EVs while driving , idling, or parking above the coils.

  • Development:  Part of Michigan Central Innovation District's goal is to advance mobility technologies.

  • Future Plans:  Aiming for public availability within a few years.

GM and Pilot Flying J's Nationwide Charging Network:

  • Current Progress:   17 charging stations  operational across 13 states.

  • Expansion Plans:  25 more stations by year-end, targeting 2,000 stations in the next few years.

  • Features:  Plug and Charge compatibility, pull-through stalls, amenities like Wi-Fi, and food services.

  • Accessibility:  Locations available via GM's apps, Pilot's myRewards Plus, EVgo app, and others.

  • Partnerships:  Collaboration with EVgo and special offers for Hertz rental car customers.

Ford and Xcel Energy's Charging Port Initiative:

  • Goal:   Install 30,000 EV charging ports  across the US by 2030.

  • Collaboration:  Part of Xcel Energy's Electric Vehicle Supply Infrastructure program.

  • Coverage:  Will expand in states including Michigan, Minnesota, and Texas.

  • Benefits:  Infrastructure support for business customers, with most upfront costs covered by Xcel.

All these automakers are investing in tubes to move electricity, but not locations to capture electricity. It's like everybody opening a coffee shop, but nobody is planting any of those sweet, sweet beans.

International

Global Car Sales Surge in November:

  • DesRosiers Report:  November car sales  jumped 20.7%  compared to last year.

  • Total Sales:  138,747 new vehicles sold, with Mazda, Acura, and Toyota seeing significant gains.

  • Challenges:  Despite growth, sales are still below pre-pandemic levels of 143,315 in November 2019.

UK's Automotive Market Gains Momentum:

  • Sales Increase:  New car sales  up by 9.5%  in November, nearly reaching pre-pandemic levels.

  • Fleet Investments:  The majority of the 24,359 new BEVs sold were fleet purchases.

  • SMMT Insights:  Calls for VAT cuts on public EV charging and new BEVs to boost private demand.

China's Nio Reports Narrower Losses:

  • Stock Performance:  Nio  shares rose 4%  after reporting smaller-than-expected losses.

  • Financials:  Q3 revenue at 19.1 billion yuan, with a loss of 2.67 yuan per share.

  • Market Strategy:  Focus on efficiency and cost discipline amid intense competition in China's EV market.

  • Future Outlook:  Aims for vehicle margin between 15% and 18% in 2024.

Many of the lingering effects of 2020's problems are finally falling off the global market...just in time for 2024's problems. Y'all ready?

Big Rigs, Bigger Hearts

Giphy/Disney

The generosity of the South Dakota Trucking Community is shining bright this holiday season. With their incredible efforts, they raised a remarkable $14,500, far surpassing their initial $10K goal.

This heartwarming act of kindness was amplified by Rude Transportation, a longstanding and respected association member since 1925 known for its commitment to efficient and caring service.

South Dakota Trucking Association

The funds were put to great use at Target and Scheels, where the team shopped with zeal for the kids at Sanford Children’s Hospital and Children’s Home Society. The stores extended their support with generous discounts, adding to the festive spirit.

The joy of giving culminated at the Christmas Gala last Saturday, where the community came together to celebrate the true essence of the holiday season—spreading cheer and creating lasting memories for children in need. 🎁

 
TIE CUTTERS

2024 TIME Dealer of the Year Nominee: David Mills

David Mills, a nominee for the prestigious 2024 TIME Dealer of the Year Award, has become a community cornerstone in addition to a successful dealer.

A graduate of Moline High School and Colorado State University, Mills took the reins of Mills Chevrolet in Moline, Illinois, a dealership his father founded in 1952.

He actively supports various local causes, including Sacred Heart Catholic Church, the Handicapped Development Center, and the Davenport Challenger League. Mills also demonstrates his dedication to nurturing future talent by providing resources to auto-trade programs.

Kudos for creating a legacy of giving back, and congratulations on the well-deserved nomination, David! 👏

Robot Round-Up

Agility Robotics

In Oregon, Agility Robotics' RoboFab is getting set to mass-produce humanoid robots at their new 70,000-square-foot facility. 10K annually to be exact.

At 5’9 and weighing in at 140lbs, their bi-ped bot, Digit, is promising to revolutionize warehouse work and is already grabbing attention from retail giant, Amazon.

But, we predict they’ll be missing the days of bathroom break complaints once Digit takes hours to recharge. 🔌

Gizem Gumuskaya / Tufts University

Tiny living robots created from human cells — aptly named " anthrobots" — are the latest sensation in a Tufts and Harvard lab.

Born from adult tracheal cells, these microscopic marvels have shown to exhibit some unique healing properties and just may reshape the future of healthcare.

Lab-confined and bio-degradable, these ethically sound cells are quickly turning science fiction into science fact. 🧫

Marilyn Sargent / Berkeley Lab

In a pioneering fusion of AI and robotics, Google's A-Lab is transforming material science.

Cutting out the previous, painstakingly slow laboratory work, their autonomous system can now effortlessly synthesize new materials in a matter of days.

Meanwhile, Google DeepMind's AI has identified nearly 400K stable substances, providing a vast playground for the A-Lab's innovations.

Already being referred to as the “ChatGPT for materials discovery,” its potential use and impact are only beginning. 🤖
