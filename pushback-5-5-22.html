Cryptocurrency Use Is Becoming More Common Among Dealers
 
 
Cryptocurrency in retail automotive has been a conversation for some time. Recent deals in several US dealerships have further moved crypto for cars from theoretical into a practical option with signs of spreading further.
 
Gotta go fast! A Saudi Arabian buyer paid for a 2021 Mercedes-Benz S580 from a Fort Mitchell, KY, dealer using Ethereum crypto. The transaction came about when the dealership informed the buyer it could not hold the vehicle for the seven days the wire transfer would take, which a quick downpayment in crypto resolved.
 
List of fast things — Light, Crypto transactions, toddlers with something in their mouth they should not have.
AUTOMOTIVE
Georgia Offers Rivian Big Incentive Deal
 
 
Despite some disagreement on the state's direct-to-consumer stance, several Georgian counties have offered EV startup Rivian a $1.5B incentive package. The company's new manufacturing plant in Stanton Sprints, GA, will employ 7,500 at an average salary of $56K plus benefits through 2047.
 
That’s a pretty good deal. The incentive package is the largest in the state's history. State and county contributions include 
  • Massive tax breaks 
  • Land acquisition and development funds 
  • Interstate, highway, rail line expansions 
  • Other local incentives 
Telling it like it is —  Tax breaks and incentives don't an economy make, but they go a long way.
AUTOMOTIVE
Porsche Leads EV Battery Startup Investment
 
 
EV Battery company Group14 has raised $400M from a group of investors led by Porsche. Group14's valuation is currently above $1B and will use the raised funds to construct a 2nd battery material factory in eastern Washington State.
 
Something special. Group14's technology shifts focus from graphite to silicon-carbon. The resulting batteries match current battery ranges but cost less and reduce reliance on Chinese graphite.
 
Group14—  “How much are you willing to pay for dibs?” Porsche: “yes.”

Ongoing Cyber Attack Steals Trillions
 
 
A multi-year cyber operation has stolen trillions in intellectual property from about 30 multinational companies. Chinese state actor, APT 41, has stolen hundreds of gigabytes of blueprints, diagrams, formulas, and proprietary data, including things from fighter jets to depression medications. Many were unpatented plans.
 
The operation was started in 2019 but remained undetected until recently. At this time, authorities have not stopped the campaign.
 
Telling it like it is —  At this rate, hackers will start stealing things before the original creators even have the idea to make them.

Doctor Strange Expects $300M Global Opening
 
 
Still a day away from opening, the newest Marvel movie is set to take a strong 2nd in post-covid box office numbers. Projections show Doctor Strange and the Multiverse of Madness doing $180M domestic and $140+ overseas, putting it between Spider-Man: No Way Home ($582M) and the more recent The Batman ($251M).
 
Keep em coming. The new film, in MCU style, plays a direct sequel to 3 recent releases. To get the most from Doctor Strange 2, you should first watch WandaVision, Loki, and Spider-Man: No Way Home. It releases one day after the final episode of Moon Night on Disney +.
