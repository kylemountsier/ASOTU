Findlay Supports Student In and Out Of The Classroom
 
 
A child's work never ends. Each day they learn more about their unique contribution to their family, community, and world. The unfair truth is that some have a more challenging time answering these questions than others due to circumstances entirely out of their control.
 
Thankfully, there are people ready to partner with them for powerful and lasting success!
 
Findlay auto dealerships recently presented two groups, Neighborhood Connection, and Washington County School District Foundation, with checks for $12K!
 
The $24K will be invested entirely in the lives of the kids in and out of the classroom by focusing on essential nutrition, supplies, and school support they need!
 
Karyn Keanaaina, Marketing Director at Findlay St. George, said they have been doing charity golf tournaments for 10 years, but this year decided to spread the donations, as well as raise awareness of the 30% of students below the poverty level in the district.
 
Telling it like it is - Beyond the material needs being met by Findlay’s gift is the wonderful feeling that "somebody has your back." Kids don’t always recognize it at the moment, but when they grow up, they will remember who cared, and folks like Findlay will be on the list. 🌋

Hybrids, In-House Production, and Domestic Batteries
 
 
Toyota is showing off its two new Prius models and assuring critics there is more than one way to reach carbon neutrality. One is a pure hybrid, while the other, Prius Prime, is a plug-in hybrid.
 
 
While Toyota is not following other companies toward EVs, it does plan to be carbon neutral by 2050 - just using some other method CEO Akio Toyoda is keeping to himself for now.

Ford CEO Jim Farley says the company will return to the days of producing more of its components. EVs take about 40% less labor to produce, so the company will bring more production in-house in the interest of providing a role for everyone.
 
 
EV motors, batteries, and other components production will protect the workforce and push the company toward better margins - thus staying competitive against higher profit operations like Tesla.
 
 
GM is also eyeing in-house production as a path to greater profitability. Several deals are moving the company toward CEO Mary Barra's vision of delivering 1M EVs annually by 2025.
 
 
The deals primarily focus on domestically sourced battery materials. Securing the North American battery supply chain will protect profits and qualify GM EVs for consumer tax credits.
 
TL; DR- Toyota announces new Prius models, Ford announces new in-house production plans, and GM announces (even more) deals to keep battery production profitable.
