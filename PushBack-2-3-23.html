Chatting With Our People at NADA
 
 
We had countless chats with dealers while at NADA, and each dealer shared with us their tips, tricks, and insights for the industry. In case you missed them, here's a glimpse of what we picked up from our conversations over the weekend.
 
Friday: Patrick Abad is the GM of Beaver Toyota of Cumming. His main focus for 2023 is to maintain the physical, mental, and financial wellness of the company and its employees. To achieve this goal, Abad’s company hired a financial advisor who will offer confidential analyses and lessons to every employee.
 
Saturday: Tom Dunn is a dealer in Ohio and Kentucky. Tom and his partner have been leading used car dealers in their area for over 30 years through high-quality service and solid used car departments. This year, he spoke at NADA about acquiring and reconditioning used cars.
 
Sunday: We grabbed Rhett Ricart, owner of the Ricart Automotive Group and NADA Board member, for a quick exchange. He said this year's conference is one of the best he's been to, thanks in no small part to the lack of distractions (looking at you, Vegas). Ricart commented, "The dealers needed this," and we couldn't agree more.

AUTOMOTIVE
Kansas To Tax Public Charging?
 
 
A new bill in Kansas aims to tax public EV charging. The state has a $0.24 per gallon fuel tax for maintaining roads, so the proposed $0.03 per kWh would contribute to the same fund.
 
Calling Foul? Some say the bill targets low-income residents of the state and would essentially double tax EV owners, since the state registering an electric vehicle is more than double the cost of ICE vehicles. Revenue generated by the higher registration is also used for the road maintenance fund. The higher EV registration costs, combined with home charging not receiving the same tax, leads EV advocates to suggest this is more about deterring investment than it is about tax balance.
 
Telling it like it is - Obviously, states will need to introduce EV versions of fuel taxes to protect revenue for infrastructure care, but is this clumsy implementation, anti-competition, or a fair move?

Working W/ Rivian, Volkswagen, and General Motors
 
 
📉 Rivian announced another round of layoffs. The EV maker will cut 6% of jobs to prioritize production. The 2nd round of layoffs in 7 months, company leadership says the 840 layoffs will not affect the Illinois plant, but mostly come from the California offices. 🪖 In terms of the "price war" this is the first reported friendly fire.
 
💧 VW is taking a pass on hydrogen passenger cars. The German automaker says hydrogen isn't competitive and will instead focus on EV tech for at least the next ten years. The news follows a decline in the brand's total sales for 2022, but increased sales for its EVs. 👍 Nothing wrong with doing what works, Freunds and Freundins.
 
📺 General Motors has a bunch going on. 
  • Record pre-tax earnings for 2022. 
  • Dropping salaried max bonuses from 200% to 158%.
  • Removing a $12K cap on hourly employee profit-sharing bonuses. 
  • A new partnership with Netflix will have Will Ferrell driving a GM EV in a super bowl ad, featuring zombies and the kids from Stranger Things.
