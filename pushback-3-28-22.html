Supply Chain Issues Drive-Up Catalytic Converter Thefts
 
 
Once upon a time, a desperate thief equipped with a hacksaw and a lookout could climb under a vehicle and return with less than $100 in precious metals. Catalytic converters contain rhodium, platinum, and palladium. Ongoing supply chain issues have driven the prices of such rare metals up, and thieves have taken a renewed interest. 

Now hiring: Junkyard dogs? Previously relegated to abandoned back roads in the dead of night, these thefts have increased in quantity and scope. The number of reported thefts increased 350% from 2020 to 2021. Thieves now target dealerships and fleets with more frequency. In January of this year, offenders cut 20 converters from a fleet of school buses in Detroit.

State governments are investigating measures to prevent these thefts but urge dealerships and rental agencies to take precautions.

 
Telling it like it is - If you’re already willing to crawl under a car, maybe you should learn to fix them instead of destroy them? It's more profitable.

Hurry Up And Wait
 
 
Everybody knows buying a vehicle takes more than choosing a car, using a gigantic shopping cart to wheel it to the check-out line, and swiping your credit card. It's a ton of metal – not a new hat. 

CDK Global and NADA Academy surveyed over 1,000 customers and 303 dealers to pinpoint friction in the buying process. They found while customers are motivated by price, they report waiting at the dealership at the top of their friction points. 

The flip-side. Dealers reported customer experience was their primary focus and a lack of system integration negatively impacted every step of the process. The need to rekey information, for instance, leads to longer waits resulting in diminished customer satisfaction.
 
Telling it like it is - System integration service providers everywhere just felt a disturbance in the force…

Volkswagen Delays ID.5 Launch
 
 
Volkswagen delayed the launch of their ID.5 EV by one month. The new release will be in the first week of May. The company cited wire harness supply disruptions in Ukraine as the cause. 

Fair is fair. The Zwickau VW plant has temporarily halted production, but will resume this week. VW's decision to delay the release stems from an inability to deliver equally to all dealer partners. The company will hold ID.5s that are already in the appropriate stations for delivery.
 
Telling it like it is - OEMs know supporting dealers IS supporting customers. It's good to see them respect the dealer ecosystem.

More People Than Ever Car Shop Online
 
 
J.D. Power conducted and released a 2021 India Sales Satisfaction Study. No two markets are the same, but the data collected in the study leads to some interesting insights. 

 The data:
  • 85% of car shoppers do research online, but a strong preference for in-person showroom visits persists. 
  • 48% of shoppers used YouTube for research
  • 35% used social media ads as an online information source
  • 68% said they would prefer a completely in-person process
  • 20% prefer a completely online process
Sandeep Pande, Lead of Automotive Practice India at NielsenIQ, says the divergence of preference is critical, but all customers want to know is that dealers can anticipate their needs and fulfill them accordingly.

Telling it like it is - Customers want to buy in person, but shop online. Maybe Brian Kramer is on to something...

DeFinance Capital Founder Hacked
 
 
Everybody hates phishing. Nefarious emails, texts, or calls are so common that we regard most unexpected communication with suspicion by default. Most people have little to fear from these hackers. DeFinance Capital founder Arthur Cheong is not most people. 

It could have been worse. A malicious link in an email compromised Arthur's wallet and drained $1.7M in cryptocurrency and NFTs. A second wallet lost $720,000 of the firm's Lido tokens. The Defiance Capital team realized the hack and were able to transfer $13.3M of the firm's remaining Lido tokens out before hackers were able to do more harm. 

General rule of thumb: don’t click any link you didn’t ask for.

Uber And NYC Taxi Partner Up
 
 
After years of beef, Uber and New York City taxis have formed a partnership. Uber will add city taxis to its app. Customers will pay similar fares for cabs as an Uber X. Cab drivers will access estimated earnings and decide to accept trips. 

180° The move marks a significant change for Uber. Once seeking to disrupt the taxi industry, the rideshare company now has plans of listing every taxi in the world in its app by 2025. The inclusion of city cabs will generate revenue and combat a driver shortage for the company.

United States and European Union Strike Natural Gas Deal
 
 
Europe is breaking its dependency on Russian gas. A deal between the US and EU will offset gas imports from Russia by 15 billion cubic meters(BCM). The deal will require increased natural gas production in the US. Other nations will also contribute. President Biden added that moving away from natural gas is the long-term goal, but further isolating Russia is prioritized. 

Ten Percent. Russia exported 155 BCM to the EU in 2021, triple the amount of 2019. The US will send 50 BCM to the EU by 2030, but other countries' contributions are not public yet.
