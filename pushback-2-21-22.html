Thousands of New Cars Stranded at Sea
 
 
A fire on the Felicity Ace auto transport ship has left nearly 4000 Porches, Audis, Bentleys, and Volkswagens drive in the Atlantic. The crew was safely evacuated by other vessels more than a thousand miles off the coast of Portugal. The ship left Emden, Germany on Feb. 10th and was scheduled to arrive in Davisville, Rhode Island on Feb. 23rd to drop off some of the vehicles before continuing on to Houston, Texas.
 
The incident could have long-reaching effects on already depleted inventories for VW Group brands in the U.S. For the moment companies are focusing on informing customers and dealers who will be affected by the incident.

Investigated Over Unexpected Breaking
 
 
A formal investigation was announced Thursday into 416,000 Tesla vehicles over reports of unexpected break activations. The evaluation will begin with 2021-2022 Tesla Model 3 and Model Y vehicles because of their advanced driver assistance systems.

The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration has received 354 complaints about the issue over the last 9 months. Customers report rapid deceleration that is often repeated in a single trip. Owners say they have raised concerns over the "phantom breaking" with Tesla , which dismissed the complaints as normal breaking.

Space Tourism Demand Rises
 
 
Blue Origin CEO Bob Smith said the company needs to build more New Shepard rockets to meet the demand from the space tourism market. The company launched 3 crewed missions last year and Smith believes they can double that in 2022. The space company, owned by Amazon's Jeff Bezos, currently has 2 operational rockets and uses one for crewed flights and the other for cargo research flights.

Looking for a ticket? Other companies are also stepping up their game in the space tourism market. SpaceX and Virgin Galactic each provided crewed space flights with some tickets available now starting at $450,000. With companies taking note of the robust demand for space tourism the race is on to make these experiences available to more eager astronauts.

Remote or Resign. Is That The Question?
 
 
The American workforce has been transformed by the "Great Resignation" with job conditions and wages becoming more competitive to attract workers. As employers call remote employees back into the office many are reevaluating their priorities in the job market. As of December 2021 there were 58 unemployed workers for every 100 jobs, creating an excess of positions.

An end in sight? Economists say it is not possible to predict whether wage pressure and high job mobility will persist throughout 2022 and beyond the pandemic. Some predict the March 1st return-to-office date may accelerate the Great Resignation as employed workers negotiate for higher wages with companies offering greater flexibility.
