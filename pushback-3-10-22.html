Rental Car Agencies Begin Shift to Electric
 
 

The rental car industry is gradually transitioning from ICE to EV, causing a shift from European to Chinese-made cars. Companies are investing in longer-term savings by buying Chinese EVs, saying they are comparable in quality but with lower purchase prices.

Carpe diem. Analysts say the time is now as the pandemic's impact on public transit and air travel increased the value of the rental car industry to nearly $100B. When the pandemic dropped the demand, many rental car companies sold off their fleets. Another inventory sell-off may follow as they supplement and eventually replace their fleets with EVs.

Investors Sue Rivian Over Price Rollback
 

Recently news broke that EV start-up, Rivian, would be hiking the price of their upcoming vehicles by about 20%. Later the company rolled back the hikes for cars pre-ordered before the announcement. Now shareholders have called CEO RJ Scaringe's price rollback and apology a futile attempt at damage control and are suing the company. 

The court is in session. The case claims the company misled investors in its initial public offering. Scaringe hopes the reverse will mend the damaged trust for customers. Since the initial price hike, Rivian's stock has dropped 37%.

AUTOMOTIVE
GM Expects Nickel Supply to Last
 
 
The Russian invasion of Ukraine has the price of essential materials rising. With chip shortages already stressing auto production, many have projected the nickel shortage to add months if not years to the already long wait till normality. But GM CEO Mary Barra is confident her supply chain team has prepared well for such an event. 

Despite trends, Barra expects GM to maintain its promised EV prices, such as $30K for the EV Chevy Equinox. With gas prices up by $1.30 from a year ago, she believes EVs to be the ultimate solution for the future. 

 
Telling it like it is - Manufacturing: occasionally “right on time” is a bit too late.

More Businesses Pullout of Russia
 
 
Mcdonald's, Coca-Cola, Pepsi, and Starbucks have joined the growing list of countries, companies, and folk doing what they can to pressure Russia to end their invasion. 

A different kind of sanction. Some companies, like Mcdonald's, will end their operations. The fast-food OG will temporarily close 850 stores. While still others, such as PepsiCo, will be more selective. Ending sales of Pepsi and 7-Up, but continue to produce dairy and baby food.

Biden’s Executive Order
 
 
Wednesday, the White House published details of their anticipated executive order regarding digital assets such as cryptocurrency. The Biden Administration has called the plan the first-ever whole government approach to addressing digital asset technology.
 
The Fact Sheet. The order goes into detail regarding agencies across the US taking measures to mitigate risks to consumers, investors, businesses, and global financial stability while harnessing the power of digital assets. So far, leaders in the crypto industry are optimistic about the administration's choice to recognize the importance of being a global leader in crypto.
