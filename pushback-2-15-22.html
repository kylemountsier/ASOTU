Carvana Launches Marketplace For Dealers
 
 
Online used-vehicle retailer, Carvana, has invited 3rd party dealers to use their site to list vehicles along with Carvana's own certified vehicles. Carvana CEO, Ernie Garcia, declined to provide details about the program in response to analysts' questions, but reactions to the invitations have been mixed. Some dealerships with existing online commerce options see no need in accepting the invitation despite Carvana's well-developed digital infrastructure, while invitees like car rental company, Hertz, are taking advantage of a new way to move their used cars at a time when prices are their highest.

Analysts say the program may provide means for the retailer to pump up their inventory and give consumers access to a wider selection. Carvana has been careful with the roll-out of the program to protect its reputation while leveraging consumer trust for third-party dealers despite the risk of third-party transactions causing an issue for consumers.

First Electrified Jeep Has Dealers Wanting More
 
 
Coming off their best market share year ever, Jeep has entered the EV game with their first hybrid-electric, Jeep Wrangler 4XE. The question is still raised: will the rugged expectations of Jeep fans be satisfied by an electric option? Head of Jeep, Jim Morrison, says electrification has been well accepted by the Jeep community with the new hybrid Wrangler accounting for a quarter of all Wrangler sales.

An upgrade to electric? How does the Wrangler 4XE compare to the ICE Wranglers?

  • Offers 470 lb.-ft torque and instant torque
  • Performs many off-roading tasks in all-electric mode
  • Gives Jeep customers the "Upgraded performance" they want, despite being a hybrid.

Jeep has many pots on the stove, with updated Grand Cherokee, Wagoneer, and Grand Wagoneer also in the mix. Morrison is confident the future has what will keep existing fans and attract new customers along the way.

Can "Smart Charging" Solve Power Grid Concerns
 
 
With EV sales increasing across the world, concerns of straining the existing power infrastructure have companies and municipalities considering options. Estimates say by 2030 Europe will have 65 Million EVs and without billions of dollars in upgrades, the existing power grid will not be able to keep up with the additional demand. In the face of this reality, some companies have introduced app-controlled "Smart Charging." Chris Pateman-Jones, CEO of British EV charger company Connected Kerb, says the shift to electric will be "nigh on impossible without smart charging."

But how does it work? Using Connected Kerb's app allows you to set your charging speed, time, and the exact price at which your car will start and stop charging based on the current price per kilowatt. The solution will allow users to connect their car without immediately charging, but instead offset the strain on the power grid (and their wallet) by letting the car wait until a lower demand time in the day.

Other options are being explored, but most are currently available only as public charging station options. With time we may see Smart Charging or "bidirectional charging" (Your car selling power back to the grid during peak times) as at-home options for EV owners.

Coinbase Super Bowl Ad Briefly Crashes App
 
 
Coinbase, a cryptocurrency exchange, ran an ad during the Super Bowl on Sunday that featured a bouncing QR code logo. Viewers who scanned the code were taken to a site offering $15 in bitcoin to folks who signed up for a Coinbase account before Feb. 15. At least, that is what it would have done if the site had been able to keep up with the traffic.

Sorry, we're at capacity. Within a minute of the ad running the site had 20 million hits, 6 times higher than previous benchmarks. This sudden surge in traffic caused the site to crash for about an hour. The Apple App store saw the Coinbase app jump from 186th to 2nd

Coinbase is down in the market by 23% this year after only recently going public and along with other crypto-based companies are seeking to branch into courting sports fans.

"Encanto" Soundtrack Tops Charts For Fifth Week
 
 
The Disney animated film "Encanto" features everything you would expect from the film studio, a heartfelt story, stunning animation, and songs that will not stop replaying in your head (or home) for weeks. With music by Lin-Manuel Miranda, creator of the musical "Hamilton", the soundtrack has achieved success independent of the movie with 5 weeks at number 1, the most streamed song in the United States on Spotify, and an equivalent of 110,000 sales last week alone.

But, why? The success of the soundtrack has been propelled by the popularity of the song "We Don't Talk About Bruno" and is the first Disney soundtrack to be number 1 for five weeks since Frozen in 2014.
