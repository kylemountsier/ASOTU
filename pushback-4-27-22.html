Detroit Auto Show Set For September
 
 
Organizers say the Detroit Auto Show will return this year for the first time since January 2019. The show will take place in mid-September and feature eve, emerging technology, and indoor/outdoor test tracks.
 
Michigan lawmakers passed a spending bill including a one-time grant of $9M to aid in rebooting the show. Event planners say the show will play a pivotal role in the public adoption of emerging technology.

AUTOMOTIVE
Ford To Increase F-150 Lightning Production
 
 
Ford CEO Jim Farley announced plans to scale electric F-150 production from 40K to 150K. The company has its eyes on being first and dominant in the full-size EV pickup market.
 
King of Trucks. Farley says the new F-150 is as big a deal for the company as the Model T. Market watchers say Ford's fully electric technology will be the true test of American EV adoption.
 
Telling it like it is — Most EVs feel like leaving what is normal, the F-150 is about as normal as American vehicles get.
AUTOMOTIVE
Corvette Goes Electric
 
 
GM President Mark Reuss announced the company would produce fully electric Chevrolet Corvettes. He provided no specific timetable but said the company would offer the electric Corvette as early as next year.
 
Hybrid. GM will produce a hybrid model first, with the fully electric version later. The hybrid will be based on Corvette's current model, but the EV is unknown.
 
Theory — The new electric Corvette will slightly recharge every time somebody stops to watch it pass.

Meta Opens Retail Shop
 
 
Meta, formerly known as Facebook, is opening showrooms dedicated to inviting people from the real world into the Metaverse. The first will open on May 9th on Meta's Burlingame, CA, campus and offer VR headsets, accessories, and video chat devices.
 
Meta Store. The company said the goal of the retail push is to show people what current products offer while giving a glimpse into the future of the Metaverse. Meta has dedicated $10B over the next year to realize the Metaverse dreams of CEO Mark Zuckerberg.
 
Telling it like it is — Is Mark Z just really hoping people get headsets in time for his metaverse birthday party?
CRYPTO
Fidelity Investments To Allow Bitcoin
 
 
Fidelity plans to allow investors to put bitcoin in their 401Ks. The plan will open the 23,000 companies that use Fidelity to bitcoin options later this year.
 
Mainstream. Fidelity is the US's largest retirement-plan provider, but this move toward the mainstream for crypto will need the endorsement of employers to be complete, which remains to be seen.
