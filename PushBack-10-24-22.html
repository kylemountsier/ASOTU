Buy Centers Week 3 - AMA
 
 
David Long and the All Things Used Cars community opened the floor for a Q&A about buy centers. After the last two weeks of covering the topic through operator experiences, it was time to turn the mic to those still wondering how to get started or take their used car buying business to a more powerful place.
 
As always, the conversation started with a market rundown by Cox Automotive's John Ellis. Check out the first 10 minutes of the playback on Clubhouse for a shot of data and insights you can ruminate on all week.
 
What is the first step?
Get the right tools, put a person in an office, simplify their day to a car-buying job description, and give them at least 3 months to work out the kinks.
 
The person matters. The experience doesn't.
Your buyers will hear "no" a lot. Pick a person who can be committed to doing it.
 
Create opportunities for them to gain confidence and experience. Invest in their confidence as a buyer. Let them buy $2K cars and take them straight to the auction.
 
Are there any buy center-focused AIs?
Buy center AI exists. However, an AI must be part of a complete set of systems and processes to get the job done.
 
Aim for a net zero expense to start.
A buy center does not need to start off with a $50K budget. See what you can do with no additional expense and THEN ramp up from there.
 
Branding matters.
If you are letting people know you buy cars, be sure you are also letting them know some of how you are different from others.
 
Can you pay a customer without the title in hand?
Yes, but you don't have to. There are successful examples of buy centers paying customers within an hour with or without a title AND successful examples of buy centers not paying customers until the title is in hand. Find out how it works for your team.
 
If you wanted to boil down what the All Things Used Cars community has spent the last 3 weeks saying, it is the old expression - the best time to start a buy center was 10 years ago, and the second best time is right now.
 
The community is filled with people ready to share their experiences, so you start on the right foot and assist in getting the next step pointing in the right direction.

Toyota Swallows Its Pride
 
 
Toyota will not meet its full-year production target. The company communicated several times this year that, while monthly cutbacks were taking effect, they were confident about the full year.
 
Finally, the company reached its tipping point and confessed that a year of supply chain and semiconductor chip issues had changed its target. It did not make the new target public.
