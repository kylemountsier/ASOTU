DATA & INSIGHT

Leading From The Front

Giphy/

In Friday’s riveting episode of David Long’s All Things Used Cars, the secrets to exceptional leadership were revealed. Here’s what you’ll need in your toolkit:

🧰  Vulnerability: Leaders must embrace wins and losses openly. Transparency and resilience forge unbreakable team bonds, establishing trust and authenticity.

🧰  Communication: It's not about talking, it's about connecting. Through open, two-way dialogue, leaders can create an environment where voices are heard, friction is addressed and relationships are forged.

🧰  Accountability: Owning both victories and defeats is non-negotiable. Leaders must maintain unwavering standards, anchoring their teams with consistent responsibility.

🧰  Adaptability: Rigidity has no place in leadership. Leaders must be willing to adapt to change and welcome new ideas (even if it means abandoning your pride). Adaptation fuels progress.

🧰  Results-Driven: Exceptional leaders aren't just talk; they're action. They deliver outstanding results and push their team to succeed.

🧰  Sincerity: Leaders should epitomize integrity, ensuring promises become actions. A leader's credibility is their most valuable currency. Without it,

🧰  Emotional Intelligence (EQ): Empathy isn't a weakness; it's a superpower. Be sensitive to the struggles, needs, and motivators for each of your individual team members. Be sure you’re using the rights tools at the right time with the right person. Strong relationships built on trust and mutual understanding are the foundation of robust leadership.

🧰  Motivational: Successful leaders radiate positivity and encouragement. That energy is infectious, eradicating complacency and inspiring others to join the hype.

🧰  Decisiveness: No one wants a follow wishy-washy leader. Hesitation is the enemy of progress. Leaders must learn to make decisions swiftly and with conviction. A swift "no" trumps a lingering "maybe" every time.

BUSINESS

Industry Round-Up: Furloughs, Ferraris, and Fees

Tenor

😥🪧✋

Stellantis and Ford announced Friday that they will temporarily lay off   1,250 employees due to the impact of the UAW strike — 700 from Stellantis and 550 from Ford.

In total, Ford has furloughed 2,480 employees and Stellantis about 1,340.

 

🤑🏎️⛓️

Following requests from wealthy, tech-savvy customers, Ferrari has begun accepting cryptocurrency for its luxury sports cars in the U.S. and will likely extend the option to Europe soon.

How come? Ferrari's Chief Marketing and Commercial Officer said cryptocurrencies made sense for two reasons —

  1. It will contribute to efforts to reduce their carbon footprint through the introduction of new software and larger use of renewable sources, and

  2. They want to accommodate (mostly younger) investors who have built their fortunes around cryptocurrencies, or traditional investors who have looked to diversify their portfolios.

 

😡💰👩‍⚖️

On Friday, Tesla urged a Delaware judge to reconsider the $230M in legal fees requested by a team of shareholder attorneys who won a settlement in a dispute over director pay.

How ‘bout $64M and call it even?  Tesla's attorney argued the shareholder's attorneys exaggerated the value of the settlement — and by extension their requested fee — by pegging its value to the cost to directors (~$10,690/hour per director) rather than the benefit to the company.

Kathaleen McCormick,  Chief Judge on the court heard about two hours of arguments but declined to mention when she would ultimately be issuing her ruling.

A Total Eclipse in a Car

Rolls-Royce/Benedict Campbell

In a perfectly-timed reveal to coincide with the solar eclipse, Rolls-Royce unveiled their highly exclusive Black Badge Ghost Ékleipsis Private Collection on Friday.

This limited-edition beauty blends aesthetics with innovation to mimic the same sense of awe experienced during an eclipse, with the “ Lyrical Copper” finish perfectly capturing the ethereal glow of the transiting light.

Get ‘em while you can. Though this stunner features countless mesmerizing details, there will only be 25 of them to go around.
