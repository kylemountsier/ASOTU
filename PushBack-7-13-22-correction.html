Kicks For Kids
 
 
Mohawk Autogroup is hosting a SneakerCon as part of its Kicks for Kids campaign. The donation-based event invites people to bring name-brand shoes or hoodies to donate to foster and underprivileged kids. Former New York Giant and Cleveland Brown, Lee Rouson, appeared in a video promoting the event with Pastor Charlie Muller. They said their vision is to stack shoes to the ceiling of Mohawk Chevrolet.
 
The campaign runs from July 8th to August 12th. More information about the event and how you can contribute are on the group's event page.

AUTOMOTIVE
BMW Starts Subscription-Based Heated Seats
 
 
One small step for OEMs, one giant leap for testing what consumers will go along with. BMW is officially selling heated seat subscriptions for $18 a month. The monthly sub-model is not the only option. Customers can pay for a year ($180), 3 years ($300), or even lifetime access for $415.
 
Microtransactions. BMW has been putting features behind subscriptions since 2020, along with Audi, Cadillac, Porsche, and Tesla. The heated seat option has not been added to the US digital store. We can only wait and see if - or when - that changes.
 
Telling it like it is — Building cars with features that may never be turned on during a chip shortage seems like a really strange plan. What do you think?
AUTOMOTIVE
OEMs In The Race To The EV Top
 
 
👷 📉Rivian is considering laying off up to 700 non-manufacturing workers. Like many other companies, the EV maker is weighing streamlined operations in the face of a potential recession. The company's staff exploded to 14k people during the pandemic. No decision has been announced.
 
🛻 🔋Greely is meeting consumers where they are, which happens to be really pumped about camping. The Chinese EV maker launched the new electric RD6 pickup to provide outdoor activities to people living in Covid-stricken population centers.
 
🤵 💪Lordstown Motors is a startup with relatively few waves in the hurricane of EV competition. A flip in the CEO role may soon change that. New CEO Edward Hightower has experience as a Ford and GM engineer and will lead the company through the development and launch of its Endurance pickup.
 
TL;DR — Rivian may cut jobs, Greely gets in the EV pickup war, and Lordstown Motors eyes a competitive next move.

WORLD NEWS
Thousands Of Uber's Documents Leak
 
 
Between 2013 and 2017, Uber helped people accept that ride-sharing is part of life now. Amid backlash from taxi companies and lawmakers, the company managed to withstand efforts to stop its spread almost as if by magic. A recent leak of thousands of documents suggests less "magic" and more "cutthroat semi-shady opportunism" could be the cause.
 
Nitty Gritty. The Guardian obtained the original documents and shared them among a consortium of outlets. Among the findings are secret tech used to throw off police investigations, back scratch deals with high-ranking lawmakers, and positive chatter about violent clashes between Uber and Taxi drivers.
 
Whistleblower. Uber's top Europe lobbyist from 2014 to 2016, Mark MacGann, came forward as the whistleblower after the 124k documents were made public. MacGann said he regrets being part of a group that massaged facts to earn driver and political trust by selling a lie.
 
Bygones? Uber says they understand Mark's regret, but he is in no position to speak credibly about Uber today. Uber also claims the leak came after a half-million-dollar settlement was paid to MacGann, which he disputes.
 
Telling it like it is —  Hulu made a show called “Super Pumped” about how cutthroat Uber’s founder was. So this leak is basically just free promotional material at this point.
