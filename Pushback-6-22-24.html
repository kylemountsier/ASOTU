Electric Super Duper Car

Giphy/YouTube

Contradicting the idea that "big things" come in small packages, the pint-sized McMurtry Spéirling EV is a small thing in a small package, completing courses in amazingly small amounts of time.

The EV supercar has shattered records at Hockenheim, outpacing the Mercedes-AMG One by a remarkable 14.1 seconds.

Clocking in at 1 minute and 24.4 seconds, this track-only marvel has become the fastest closed-wheel car on the iconic German circuit.

Despite being smaller than a Miata and weighing under 2,200 pounds, the Spéirling packs a whopping 1,000 horsepower, making it a formidable contender.

Former F1 driver Max Chilton piloted the car during its record-setting lap on June 8, with a price tag of £895,000 and limited to just 100 units, this tiny but mighty EV is set to deliver unparalleled performance to a lucky few starting in 2025.
