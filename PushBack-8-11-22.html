Adventure Subaru Awarded
 
 
Out of 636 American Subaru dealers, Adventure Subaru in Fayetteville, AR, has been named the 2022 Love Promise Retailer of the Year. Over the last ten years, owner Don Nelms and the team have donated over $3.2m to 70 local organizations and causes. One such organization is the Single Parent Scholarship Fund of Northwest Arkansas. The dealership's work helped provide at least 57 scholarships to single parents who went on to earn college degrees. And that is only ONE of the many groups Adventure Subaru supports.
 
Don's commitment to community education is rooted in overcoming his childhood learning disability. Now he and his team help other kids and adults find the support they need to reach the potential the Adventure Subaru team knows they have.
 
Telling it like it is —  More educated parents = more opportunities for children = a more powerful community today and tomorrow. Subaru made the right call on this award for sure!

California DMV Accused Tesla
 
 
Be it Twitter or Tesla; robots are giving Elon Musk trouble. Two filings with the California Office of Administrative Hearings say Tesla's marketing suggests Autopilot can operate autonomously, but it isn't. Tesla has fifteen days to respond to the accusations in court or potentially lose its license to operate as a manufacturer and dealer in CA.

How Ya Doin' Japan?
 
 
🏭 🚙 Toyota has had several production projection reductions lately (oof. say that five times fast). However, the company stands by its global 9.7m forecast for the year. Plans to have a strong September and increased numbers in November hope to wash away shortages and stoppages as recent as this week.
 
 
💹 🤑 Honda is a lot like pizza. Even people who prefer other brands agree they deserve the love they get, and none of us talk to the people who say otherwise. The company raised its yearly profit outlook by 20b yen, putting it at 830b yen ($6.15b) for the fiscal year ending March 31st, 2023. While operating profits are expected to increase, Honda warns against over-optimism as the same list of setbacks we've discussed for months still loom over everything we do.
 
 

🦹‍♀️ 💾 Hyundai and Kia are facing legal issues in at least seven US states stemming from ignitions system issues leading to theft. St. Louis police say the brands account for two-thirds of every car stolen in July. Like hackers in 90s movies, people have compromised the ignition system with any old USB cable. Coupled with no window-breaking immobilizing technology, many Hyundais and Kias have become sitting ducks.

TL:DR — Toyota says production is going to be fine. Honda says profits are going to be a bit better than fine. Hyundai and Kia say your car is going to be fine, just, like, keep a good eye out, huh?

Microsoft Emoji Go Open Source
 
 
Microsoft will add more than 1,500 3D emojis to Figma and GitHub as free, open-source code. Creators will be allowed to create custom emojis using the code to encourage creativity and inclusivity in the medium.
 
The company says they spent a lot of time on inclusive designs in the original 1,500 emojis, but just one team knows a broader community will make the idea globally relevant in unique ways.
