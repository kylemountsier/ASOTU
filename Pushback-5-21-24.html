Moving Cars Around

Thanks to imports, a new car may travel more miles before you drive it than in the first few months of ownership. 

BMW Imports Banned Material

A recent Senate report revealed that  BMW  imported 8,000 Mini Cooper vehicles into the U.S. with components from a banned Chinese supplier. The legislation, passed in 2021, aims to prevent goods produced with forced labor from China's Xinjiang region. BMW has continued using these parts until at least April 2024.

BYD Production Hits Mexico

Meanwhile,  BYD  unveiled its new Shark PHEV truck in Mexico. This hybrid truck, starting at $53,440, boasts an impressive range of over 500 miles and is set to compete with established models like the Ford Ranger. BYD plans to expand production to Mexico, emphasizing the importance of the region in its growth strategy.

Italy Arrests Cars

In Italy, police seized 134  Fiat  Topolino cars for displaying the Italian flag despite being manufactured in Morocco. This violation of a law against misleading indications of origin forced Fiat to remove the flag to release the cars.

 

Bama Benz Ballot Ballet

Mercedes-Benz workers in Alabama  voted against  joining the United Auto Workers (UAW) union, with 56% rejecting the proposal. This outcome deals a setback to UAW's organizing efforts, especially after their recent success at a Volkswagen plant in Tennessee. 

Despite the loss, UAW President Shawn Fain expressed pride in the workers' efforts and emphasized the union's commitment to ongoing organizing and addressing unfair labor practice charges against Mercedes-Benz and other automakers.

 

Volkswagen Draws New Lines

Volkswagen is  shifting  from an all-electric vehicle strategy to include more plug-in hybrids due to slowing EV sales.

  • The company has faced setbacks, including delayed model releases and falling behind in the Chinese market.

  • VW has also paused plans for a €2 billion EV factory in Germany and is seeking leniency from European regulators on emissions.

  • Despite the changes, VW is partnering with Xpeng Inc. and planning new EV models to regain market share.

 

Electric Caterpillar. Electric Butterfly? 

Cat

Caterpillar and Vale are  partnering  to test a 240-ton electric haul truck and other decarbonization technologies at Vale's Brazilian mines. This initiative aims to reduce Vale's carbon emissions by 33% by 2030 and achieve net-zero emissions by 2050. The tests include battery-electric trucks, V2G/V2x systems, and dual-fuel solutions.
