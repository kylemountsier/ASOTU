Ford to ban F-150 Lightning customers from reselling trucks
 
 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Ford is offering an option provision attached to the new F-150 Lightning which mandates that buyers must keep their electric trucks for one full year before trying to resell them, according to a new "No-Sale" provision.

 

Telling it like it is — An optional clause where dealers are the ones that get to choose whether or not they are the bad guy. This should go well.

 
Amazon and Stellantis Deal Creates Unknowns for Rivian's Future
 
 

In addition to the existing 100,000 vehicles by 2030 deal Amazon has with the American electric auto manufacturer Rivian, this week Stellantis announced Amazon will be their In-Car software supplier as well as the first commercial customer for their upcoming Ram ProMaster. The News had a negative impact on Rivian's market value and saw a small bump to Stellantis. Amazon has announced by email that things between the company and Rivian are still on track and “We always knew that our ambitious sustainability goals in our last mile operations would require multiple electric delivery van providers".

 

Telling it like it is — Just think, in 10 years' time, Amazon may be the reason your car can plot against you with your cell phone.

CES Wows In-Person & Online Attendees
 
 

Robots, Tvs, Smart glasses, 5G, personal devices, and of course EVs were spotlighted over the course of the CES show this week in Las Vegas.
