DEALERS
Eliminating Aged Inventory Pt. 2
 
 
Each week All Things Used Cars brings together top operators and thinkers to reimagine the retail auto industry's many aspects. Then ASOTU tries to turn that hour of power into a short easy-to-skim piece of this email.
 
This week we are in for a special treat because the founder, facilitator, and host of All Things Used Cars, David Long, shared his notes with us and we will share them with you.
 
In addition to the below notes, you can listen to a full replay of "Top 10 Ways To Eliminate Aged Inventory, pt. 2" on Clubhouse or plan on joining the community for the next talk every Friday at 9 am EST/6 am PST. 
  1. Benchmark your process, create your strategy, and review it often.
  2. A solid appraisal process so you can get the prices right on day one.
  3. Increase speed to retail. 
  4. Think of a 30-day supply as a two-week supply X2 
  5. Base prices on the market day supply of the car
  6. Focus on your 10 most expensive vehicles. 
  7. Use your tools.
    1. Use Google Analytics trends combined with Polk data. 
    2. vAuto heat sheet report
  8. Color code used cars. Yellow- Orange- Red. When a car is orange, drive the car home.
  9. Leverage all partnerships. 
  10. Establish and protect a daily trade walk. Act on every thing you notice - tires, lights, oil changes, updated pictures, weird smell, etc. 

Telling it like it is - Sometimes process improvement tips are a bit too big to bite off. We suggest starting with any that make you feel called out.

AUTOMOTIVE
Humble Suggestions From OEMs
 
 
Lexus heard you want a manual transmission and would like to suggest an alternative - make-believe! The "virtual" manual transmission will put a realistic feeling clutch and stick shift in EVs by Toyota's high-performance brand.
 
 
You may think some manual control over the vehicle's acceleration will be directly tied to the feature... but no. It is literally just so drivers feel like they are driving a stick shift.
 
Lucid would also like to suggest a different plan - not canceling your order. Delivery delays, staffing issues, and an unhopeful production goal outlook have customers bailing on their orders.
 
 
Lucid has a fourteen-call plan to save the orders, but we cannot think of a better way to drive customers further away. A case-owner, manager, and regional manager, follow-up call plan adds up to 14 calls over two weeks before the order is finally canceled.
 
BMW's suggestion is to take the bumpy road next time. They filed a patent for tech that would turn bumps into electricity for EVs. The suspension system would capture energy from the up-and-down movement to recharge the battery while driving.
 
 
While patents get filed and are not used all the time, this one, combined with our persistent presence of potholes, could eventually add up to something neat. (Like, for real, tho, why are we even paying taxes if the roads look like swiss cheese?)
 
TL; DR - Lexus suggests trying a pretend gear shift, Lucid suggestions not leaving them, and BMW suggests potholes are as bad as they seem.
