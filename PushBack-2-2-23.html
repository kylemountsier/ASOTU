Nissan And Renault Work Things Out
 
 
Nissan and Renault's 24-year alliance is safe...for now. The deal took 4 months of intense talks, but both companies feel that they have reached a necessary balance. 
  • Renault's stake in Nissan will drop from 43% to 15%.
  • The excess stake of 28% will be placed in a French trust to sell for around $4.1B.
  • With 15% cross-shareholding, Nissan gained a vote on alliance matters and a free hand for a long-term strategy in the US, China, and other markets.
  • Part of the deal requires both parties to pool more resources into Latin American, Indian, and European projects.
  • Nissan will invest in Renault's BEV unit.
  • Renault will partner with Chinese groups, such as EV maker Geely and semiconductor giant Qualcomm. 

Both Renault and Nissan experienced a bump in share value following the announcement. Renault saw a 25% jump, while Nissan rose just 3%.

Telling it like it is - Rome was not built in a day, but 4 months to reach a “we should both get an equal vote” agreement in a partnership is a little surprising. What changes do you see heading for dealers based on Nissan’s newfound freedom?

China Check-In
 
 
China. The Chinese market likes EVs and has demonstrated that when cheap options are available, they sell. EV maker Nezha doubled its sales in 2022 to surpass the more established and more pricey NIO (despite NIO increasing sales by 34%). Both companies and their SUV or Sedan options were outsold by the budget EV, Hongguang Mini.
 
By 2050, China will have such cheap EV options you'll have to bring your own 9-volt to start it.
 
Shanghai. Following a surge in orders, Tesla says it will ramp up production at its gigafactory to 20K units a week. The previous 1-month production high was over 80K in September, but declined following reduced demand (which Musk contests). Tesla's recent price cuts have driven demand up in its most profitable market.
 
If the price going down makes sales go up, imagine how many sales you'd get if your cars were FREE! No, wait... that's not how this works.
