Touch Screen Nostalgia
 

 

As early as 1992, touch screen navigation systems were available in vehicles. Hard to believe, eh? In fact, Oldsmobile, that super forward thinking brand, was the first one to retrofit 100 vehicles with the technology called TravTek. The unit had a trunk mounted piece that looked like a chopped off elephant tusk and only was able to select pre-loaded addresses.

Reroute. Instead of that familiar voice coming from the system and “rerouting” when you took a wrong turn, there was simply a help button that called AAA for you. Also, you didn’t dare and drive outside the 1200 square mile radius and hope to find your way!

Telling it like it is - This history lesson brought to you by the touch screen at the hotel gym that still to this day can’t figure out how to work when you need it most.

Mo Money, Mo People Staying Around
 
 
According to the NADA Annual Dealership Workforce study, turnover in US dealerships saw a substantial drop in employee turnover as it dropped to its lowest level since the study was first introduced in 2011.
 
Average dealership employees rose to 74, which is still below pre-pandemic numbers, contributing to both efficiency and higher earnings per employee.
 
Telling it like it is - You mean better cultures, more wages, less stressful negotiation-free workplaces, and caring for customers is something people WANT to keep doing!? Ya don’t say…

Self Driving at a Stand Still
 
 
Ford announced that it will be pulling all support and investments from self-driving tech startup, Argo AI. John Lawler, Ford CFO, stated that they feel their investments would be better spent in a business that will have a large return in a shorter period of time. Ford has since expressed more interest in hands-free technology rather than fully autonomous driving technology.
 
Telling it like it is - This definitely has nothing to do with Ford’s latest financial stumbles, such as their gross underestimation of production costs for Q4 or their stock dropping to its lowest point in 11 years. Nope. Def not.

California May Pass EV Buck to the Rich
 
 
A proposed ballot measure in California would raise taxes by 1.75% for any residents making over $2 million per year. The additional income from the taxes would go toward charging stations for electric vehicles, as well as wildfire prevention. 

The majority of decided voters (49%) are in favor of the ballot, but Gavin Newsom, California governor, is strongly opposing the raise in taxes to support EVs, regardless of his goal to fully ban gas-powered vehicles.

Poll: Would you rather…. 1) Have only EVs only by 2035; 2) Get taxed more; 3) Both. Trick question, eh?

Driving Through Toward Web3
 
 
Since NFTs became popular in early 2021, several fast food chains have begun dabbling in Web3.
 
🍔 McDonalds released 188 “Big Mac Rubik's Cube NFTs” in China last year.
 
👑 Burger King put QR codes on their Keep It Real Meals, which allowed customers to collect one of three NFT game pieces. All 3 game pieces unlocked a fourth NFT that included special prizes all the way up to free Whoppers for a year.
 
🌮 Taco Bell launched a 25-piece NFT collection of its menu staples creatively illustrated that customers could purchase. Some of the NFTs came with electronic gift cards.
 
☕️ Starbucks announced this month that it would incorporate NFTs into its reward program. It will be one of the first major loyalty programs to launch a web3 campaign at scale.
 
🍗 In a completely different camp, Popeyes has shown no interest in dropping an NFT. Early this year on Twitter, they posted “WE’RE NOT ON THAT WAVE”.
 
Telling it like it is - We’ve said it before: NFTs can’t just be a money grab. They need to be built on community and interest… and fast food restaurants have those? 🤔
