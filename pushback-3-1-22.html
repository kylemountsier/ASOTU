Rooftops Are up From 2021
 
 
Urban Science's 2021 Automotive Franchise Activity Report shows slight but stable growth in the industry despite far-reaching inventory shortages. While the number of franchises decreased slightly from 31,959 to 31,646, 73 new rooftops opened in the country during the same period. 85% of the areas measured remained stable with no net change for the better or worse. 

The majority of this rooftop growth was in the highly populated states of California, Texas, and New York, but the average sales by dealers across the study show a 19 unit increase.

 
Telling it like it is - More rooftops means more sales but also more points of acquiring inventory. The snowball is rolling, friends!

Suspected Cyber Attack Halts Toyota's Japanese Production
 
 
Toyota is suspending domestic factory operations on Tuesday, losing around 13,000 cars. No information was immediately available, but a possible cyberattack on a plastic parts and electronic components supplier was made public. The attack came after Japan joined the West in clamping down on Russia after it invaded Ukraine. It is not clear that the cyberattack is related to the Russia-Ukraine conflict. 

Ongoing supply chain issues already strain the company that employs "Just in time" manufacturing to use parts as they arrive to avoid stockpiling. The company does not know if the halt at its 14 Japanese plants, which account for about a third of its global production, will last more than a day.

 
Telling it like it is - One more thing for the industry to overcome, which of course we will.

New VW Golf R Comes to the US
 
 
While popular in Europe, the VW Golf never achieved widespread appeal in the States. VW of America even dropped many of the lesser models in the line leaving only 2, the Golf GTI and the more powerful sibling, the 2022 Golf R. 

It's like a family car that runs on Redbull. The vehicle looks like any other four-door hatchback but comes with all-wheel drive, a 315hp turbocharged four-cylinder EA888 engine, and a slew of other performance upgrades from cam phasing to thermal management with an interior that remains as spartan as ever.
 
Telling it like it is - Don't tell anybody, but the “R” stands for Street Racing.

Starlink Made Available in Ukraine
 
 
A tweet by Ukrainian politician Mykhailo Fedorov captured the attention of many on Saturday, including Elon Musk. The tweet began, "@elonmusk, while you try to colonize Mars -- Russia try to occupy Ukraine!" The tweet requested the multi-company tech CEO to provide Starlink, a satellite internet provider, to Ukraine as they attempt to "Address sane Russians to stand." 

Coming right up. Within 10 hours, Musk replied, "Starlink service is now active in Ukraine. More Terminals en route." With reports of many service disruptions across the country's existing network, access such as this could be a critical tool in the nation's defense.
 
Telling it like it is - From a business point of view: Is there any better “stress test” a service can be put through?

The 28th Screen Actors Guild Awards
 
 
This year, the SAG awards were in person and sporting a list of covid precautions as long as the list of awards. Will Smith's "CODA," a drama about a deaf family, took top honors in an unexpected win, with "Squid Game" and "Ted Lasso" also winning big. 

It's a small world, after all. Several vital moments highlighted the value these writers and actors provide beyond entertainment.

  • Squid Game is the first non-English language show to be nominated and won multiple awards. 
  • CODA stars the only deaf Oscar winner and tells a story of "Children of deaf adults" with humor and care. 
  • Star of "Succession" Brian Cox used his time to urge people to remember Ukraine's president was a comic. It is time for artists of all kinds to make a shift in the world.
 
Telling it like it is - Maybe with the help of technology, folks are learning to live in the post-covid world with broader horizons than before?

EV Maker NIO Seeks Additional Listings
 
 
Chinese Tesla rival NIO plans to list shares in Hong Kong. The Shanghai-based company broke ground in 2014 among those vying for the Chinese EV market, but unlike other similar China-based competitors, they have not entered the exchange in Hong Kong. They are among other Chinese businesses looking into additional listings outside New York in the face of potentially being forced off the American exchange. 

NIO delivered a record 25,034 vehicles in Q4 2021, up 44% from the same period in 2020. Investors favored NIO shares earlier in the pandemic, but the stocks are under pressure amid a broader pullback in EV-related stocks. NIO is also seeking to join Singapore's stock market.

 
Telling it like it is - Is this one of those “Act like you’re leaving to see if anybody begs you to stay” things?
