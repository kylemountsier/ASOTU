Inventory Dents and Dips

Giphy

Carfax has released its first Used Car Index , and customers are using it to find a unique flavor of future transportation - banged-up-a-tad. ü§ï

Like pre-ripped jeans, these vehicles with minor damage are hip with a specific crowd, and unlike pre-ripped jeans, they are MORE affordable than their undented or even repaired peers (seriously, ripped jeans should be earned, not bought). üëñ

Giphy

While used inventory is taking a "beating," new day supply is asymmetrically mending. Data collected on publicly traded dealer groups shows many brands restoring a 30-day supply while others struggle to keep up with demand. üìà

One example is Asbury Auto Group, with a 30-day supply for Q1 2023, an increase from a 10-day supply one year ago and a 26-day supply in Q4 2022.

When broken down into domestic and imported segments, the numbers fork dramatically with a 63-day supply of domestic vehicles and only 18 days for imports.

Telling it like it is - New inventory is returning, but the impact of supply issues, Covid, and the Inflation Reduction Act may yet be unrealized in terms of importing inventory. Used cars, on the other hand, are around and consumers are following affordability to some previously less popular places.
