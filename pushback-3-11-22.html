AUTOMOTIVE
California Receives Air-Pollution Waiver
 
 
Per a previously filed petition, California has received a waiver allowing the setting of custom air-pollution limits. The desired limits are stricter than the EPA's emissions standards for passenger cars and trucks. The waiver expired under the previous administration, but has been renewed under Biden. 

Picking sides. With the support of the waiver primarily coming from Democratic administrations, automakers are also voicing divided opinions. Ford, Honda, BMW, Volvo, and Volkswagen have supported the waiver, with GM and Nissan siding against the stricter standards. 

 
Telling it like it is - With so many states following California’s lead, it seems the EPA should be asking them for a waiver.
AUTOMOTIVE
Chinese EVs Move Into Europe
 
 
Chinese EV company Xpeng has started taking pre-orders for a version of its P5 electric sedan in Europe. The company hopes to rival Tesla by aggressively expanding international sales into Denmark, Norway, Sweden, and the Netherlands, including a physical retail store. 

Unique P5? The EV offered in Europe will differ from the original with a less-sophisticated version of the Xpilot semi-autonomous driving system and no Lidar.
 
Telling it like it is - Does somebody need to tell Xpeng we are having a supply chain crisis? They seem to have forgotten.
AUTOMOTIVE
Tesla Hikes Price
 
 
Wednesday, EV maker Tesla raised the prices of its Model Y and Model 3 vehicles. The increase is $1K each in the US and almost $1.6K in China. The hike sets back Musk's dream of releasing more affordable EVs. 

A trend or a tragedy? It is easy to draw a line between the hike and the surging raw material prices caused by the Russian invasion of Ukraine. But since January 2021, the cost of the Model Y SUV has increased by 20%, and the Long Range Sedan Model 3 has increased 10.6%. With Rivian also announcing a recent price hike, the cost of widespread adoption of EV tech may still be in motion.

TECH
Twitter goes “Dark”
 

Restrictions stemming from what some have called the "Cyber Iron Curtain" have cut many Russian citizens off from the outside world. With access to only state-owned news sources, some do not know their country is invading Ukraine or what is happening across their border. Tech and social media companies, such as Twitter, have taken to the "dark web" to provide access. 


@PrivacyPlease. Although the dark web is often associated with illicit activities, privacy and censorship bypass are more common. A Tor browser is required to access such privacy-protected aka ".onion" sites.

 
Telling it like it is - Time to find out if the pen, oh sorry, if the Tweet is mightier than the sword.

SoftBank to Launch NFT Marketplace
 
 
SoftBank's tech division, Z Holdings, plans to launch an NFT marketplace this year to meet its revenue targets. The NFT mall could launch as early as spring and be available in 180 countries. Co-CEO Kentaro Kawabe says the company cannot miss out on the opportunity web3 provides. 

More more more.  The announcement is not SoftBank's first step into the web3 space. In November, they led a $93M funding round for The Sandbox, a blockchain gaming platform, while also investing #150M in a South Korean metaverse platform called Zepeto.

MARKET
Amazon Board Approves Stock Split
 
 
A month after Google's parent company, Alphabet, Amazon has announced plans for its first split since September 1999. The split will divide each share into 20 pieces of equal value. The resulting 20 shares' value will equal one current share. 

At the time of the news, a single share of Amazon Inc had a value of $2,785.58. After the split, each of the twenty new shares would be worth $139.28. Amazon hopes the change will make their stock more accessible to investors and offer employees more flexibility in managing their equity.

 
Telling it like it is - Everybody knows once you break a 20 it's as good as gone!
