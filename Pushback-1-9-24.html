Telling the News

Giphy

2023 numbers are coming in, and everybody has something fun to talk about. Several sites specifically mark the increase in EV and Hybrid sales, with Toyota, of course, playing a critical part in any story from the global auto market. 

Toyota's  performance in 2023  exemplified  industry trends, with a  30% increase  in sales of electrified vehicles, signaling a robust consumer shift toward hybrids and electric vehicles (EVs). The remarkable  459% increase  in all-electric car sales underscores the accelerating transition towards sustainable mobility solutions.

So, legacy automaker EVs are getting into the hands of people who want and can afford them. Some startups are beginning to embrace the existing dealer network to get their cars to their customers. 

VinFast's   partnership  with US dealerships to expand its all-electric vehicle offerings demonstrates the potential for collaboration between innovative startups and established sales networks. This approach not only facilitates market entry but also enhances the availability of diverse EV options for consumers.

VinFast isn't the only company looking to break off more of the US market. While it isn't the biggest, the market is important across the business world. The procession of companies looking to get in or gain access to EV tax credits continues. 

Honda's  consideration of a  $14 billion   investment  in an EV and battery plant in  Canada  reflects a strategic response to changing US EV tax regulations. This move highlights the importance of understanding and adapting to policy shifts and market incentives in the North American market.

Northvolt's   securing  of substantial EU funding to establish an EV battery plant in  Germany  indicates a growing commitment within Europe to meet the rising demand for EV infrastructure. The planned  60 GWh annual capacity  marks a significant step towards addressing future needs and ensuring a stable supply chain for EV manufacturers.

Across the world, in the largest auto market, companies are constantly unveiling new things that could one day spell a heavyweight bout between long-standing US favorites and well-established Chinese brands. 

Xpeng's   initiatives , including its entry into the  MPV segment  and ambitious plans for advanced technologies, highlight the intense competition and rapid innovation within the Chinese auto market. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for US auto dealers looking to anticipate global trends and potential competitive pressures.

Back to the Moon

First US Commercial Moon Launch: Astrobotic Peregrine Mission 1 (Official NASA Broadcast)

In a historic event, the United States launched its first lunar landing mission  since 1972 , marking a significant milestone in space exploration. The mission, led by a private company, was launched aboard the Vulcan Centaur rocket, a new model developed by United Launch Alliance. This monumental launch from Cape Canaveral carried the Peregrine lander, designed by Astrobotic Technology under a NASA contract. The mission's uniqueness lies in its commercial nature and diverse payload, which includes human remains, stirring controversy and raising ethical questions.

The Peregrine lander, expected to land on the moon on February 23, carries 20 payloads, of which five are NASA science instruments. These include radiation environment monitors and lunar soil analyzers, advancing our understanding of the moon's environment. However, the inclusion of human remains by space burial companies Elysium Space and Celestis has sparked opposition, particularly from the  Navajo Nation . They regard the moon as sacred, and the idea of it becoming a resting place for human remains is deeply disturbing to them. The Navajo Nation's president, Buu Nygren, has urged NASA and other authorities to consider these cultural implications. However, NASA has limited oversight over this private-sector mission.

The launch of the Vulcan Centaur rocket is also noteworthy. The rocket represents a significant step forward for United Launch Alliance, competing in a market dominated by companies like SpaceX. Featuring US-made engines by Blue Origin, Vulcan Centaur symbolizes a shift from reliance on Russian-made engines, a change driven by political factors.

This mission, part of NASA's Commercial Lunar Payload Services program, is a testament to the evolving nature of space exploration, where private companies play a significant role. While it opens new opportunities for scientific discovery and commercial ventures, it also highlights the need for sensitivity toward cultural and ethical concerns in space exploration.
