FTC And You
 
 
News about the FTC's new dealership rules has traveled fast and far. We will touch on the details to be sure we are all up to date and invite you to be heard.
 
The FTC has proposed new rules that target abusive practices like junk fees and bait-and-switch advertising.
 
A request to extend the period for public comments past Sept 12, 2022, has been rejected.
 
As it stands, the new rules will result in a lower quality customer experience and higher prices for new and used vehicles.
 
Efforts to represent the majority of dealers (who are honest and, let’s remember, ALSO hate abusive techniques) in the conversation have been met with some pretty ugly ridicule.
 
Comments are still open until the 12th, and ASOTU is working to be sure the retail automotive community's voice is heard. Leave your comment now.

Automotive Pop Quiz
 
 
✄ 🏭 Honda will cut September output by up to 40% in Japan. Because:

A. To boost the value by limiting the supply
B. Supply Chain Disruption
C. Alien Invasion
 
Yes, it is the supply chain again. B is correct.
 
💶 🏃Ford has backed off its planned production investment in Spain. The company says it will continue work at the Valencia plant, but the application for 106m euros of pandemic relief funds has been withdrawn, while Ford:
 
A. Focus on creating a custom engine sound for the Mustang Mach-E
B. Revise their outlook for Europe
C. Expands into soccer team ownership
 
Yeah, it's B. After recent layoffs, the company is still working to determine the best way to adapt to EV production.
 
🇺🇸 ⚡️ Mercedes started production of its first US-made electric crossover. The new Alabama EV plant is one of 7 spread across three continents and will produce 100,000 EVs next year. Which is:

A. 100% of the company's EV production
B. 33% of the company's EV production
C. 4% of the company's EV production
 
That's right, at about 305k, 2023's expected EVs will be one-third produced in the Alabama plant. It's B. Are you Surprised?

Washington Follows California’s Lead
 
 
Washington Gov. Jay Inslee says the state will adopt California's regulations by the end of the year. CA often leads state-level decisions about emissions, and their recent decision to ban gas-powered vehicles is expected to have a similar impact.
 
Transportation makes up half of WA's emissions. State leadership is working on a place to ensure the required charging infrastructure is in place for citizens to adapt to a more electrified future easily.
 
Telling it like it is — Banning gas-powered cars five years after most companies are switching to electric cars is a really unoffensive way to put your foot down about something.
