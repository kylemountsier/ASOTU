Ukrainian Determination on Display
 
 
When was the last time you had a fire drill at work? Everyone has to drop what they are working on, shuffle outside, avoid the cloud following the smokers, make your way back inside, and realize you've forgotten what you were doing. Now imagine adding one little thing: rockets.

Trading a break room for a bunker. Workers at the Leoni wire harness plants in Ukraine are made of special stuff. Under Russian rocket attacks, they have continued to produce wire harnesses. OEMs like VW and Ford use the parts made at the plants to construct vehicles.

If simply showing up to work in an active war zone was not impressive enough, they recently announced adding a night shift. The additional shift will increase their production to 70% of pre-invasion capacity.
 
Telling it like it is - What does it say about a nation's spirit when its factory workers start working overtime in warzone conditions? #respect

Auto Sales Fall in March
 
 
Every conversation that starts around high dealer profits inevitably drifts into reminders that the hot market will cool. The war in Ukraine has postponed forecasted supply chain relief a bit longer, leading to the number of vehicles in inventory dropping below 900,000. 

Give it to us straight, doc? Projections show US retail sales dropping 27.8% in March year over year. New-vehicle sales could tumble by 5.1 million units compared to 2021.
 
Telling it like it is - Dealers have always known the market cooling is a question of “when” and not “if.” Time to prove that banding together and pushing back makes the mountains feel like molehills.
AUTOMOTIVE
Batteries, Batteries, and Batteries
 
 
Companies are pressing forward through raw material shortages. Individual companies, partnerships, and Joint ventures are breaking ground on battery plants looking toward the production capacity needed for an eventual transition away from ICE.
 
Roman holiday. Jeep maker Stellantis and Mercedes are partnering to construct a battery plant in Italy. Rome pledged $407M in public funds to the venture.
 
Back across the pond. Stellantis has also struck a deal with LG to plan a battery plant in Canada with eyes on an additional site in the US.
 
Running with the bulls. VW announced this week a battery plant in Spain. The plant will produce 40 gigawatt-hours a year, employ 3,000 workers, and begin operations in 2026.
 
Telling it like it is - OEMs are investing in a post-supply-chain-crunch world. Are dealers ready to succeed in the world they are crafting? We know they are.

Gas Price Relief Comes From Unexpected Source
 
 
For many, gas prices have carved a path from "high" to "maybe a horse isn't that expensive after all?" Efforts by state and federal lawmakers may see some relief coming soon. 

Can I claim my car as a dependent? States are suspending their gas taxes. Examples include Maryland cutting $0.39/gal and Georgia at $0.29/gal. Other state lawmakers are considering the move, and many are pressuring the Biden admin to pause federal fuel taxes for additional relief.
 
Telling it like it is - If things keep going this way, Paul McCartney will have to update his feelings about the taxman.

CRYPTO
Ethereum Vs. Environmental Impact
 
 
About five minutes after a person learns what cryptocurrency is another person tells them about the negative effects many digital mining operations have on the environment. Blockchain mining devours energy, so mining operations often set up shop where energy is cheap and non-renewable. Despite many years of industry domination and pressure to do so, even Bitcoin has not reduced its energy consumption. 

Player 2 has joined the game. Ether has played second place to Bitcoin for some time, but some think that trajectory may soon change. The company is shifting its network from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake oriented. Energy consumption will drop by 99% following the adjustment. No exact date has been made public, but the company expects the change to complete by June.
 
Telling it like it is - With the difference between Ether and Bitcoin being close to 10X, maybe it's time to give Grant Cardone a call!
TECH
Walmart Files Lawsuit Claiming Stolen Self-Checkout Tech
 
 
Cutting-edge technology can make or break a retail company's digital presence. A long loading screen, unresponsive link, or hard-to-read color scheme can turn customers off, leading them to a competitor or deciding they didn't want that CCR poster after all. So having an easy-to-use app or site has become paramount as retailers try to lock down customers. 

Please don't touch my stuff ever again. Walmart has accused rival BJ's Wholesale Club of stealing their self-checkout technology. The Scan & Go technology used by Walmart and its big-box subsidiary, Sam's Club, gained popularity when social distancing was adopted by shoppers. Walmart claims the self-checkout feature on BJ's Wholesale Club's app is identical to its own and that the rival company took innovations without permissions.
 
Telling it like it is - Owning a feature like self-checkout seems impossible, owning code however...
