US Dealers Are Here To Stay, But Change Is Coming
 
 
A recent report released by Axios says existing franchise laws protecting the US dealer model are here to stay. While online retail and changing OEM standards are putting pressure on dealers to keep up or bow out, the role held by dealers as local knowledge and service hubs is too valuable to the rolling adoption of EVs by the public for OEMs to abandon.
 
🤓 The report used Apple’s Genius Bar as a parallel to the high tech brand centers dealers may become where first time EV buyers can have all their questions answered before buying a new or used EV.
 
📁 The report suggests a direct line between the record profits of the last few years and the needed investments for getting ahead of the EV curve.
 
Telling it like it is -  Axios is owned by Cox Enterprises, who cannot see the future, but has been a trusted data backed opinion for over 100 years. Whatever it looks like, the future is coming… Let's be ready!

Toyota In The News
 
 
As of September 30, Toyota lowered its October production target by 6.3%, to about 750K. While chips have pestered its monthly production all year, the company maintains its nearly 10M vehicle projection for its fiscal year ending March 31.
 

President Akio Toyoda says there is nothing wrong with Toyota's EV strategy - and as the guy who came up with the strategy, we assumed he would say so. They will invest about $70B in electric vehicles, including hybrids, with about $35B going directly toward all-electric battery tech over the next 9 years.
 

Zero emissions by 2035, on the other hand... While Toyoda is confident the company will serve the diverse demand of customers, they say meeting California's 2035 goals will be "difficult." He compares the hard shift to BEVs to the "fully autonomous cars we were all supposed to be driving by now." Which we think translates loosely to "2035 is WAY over there. Y’all really think you can guess what it's going to be like when it gets here?!"

TL; DR -  Toyota is still downshifting to deal with chip shortages but confident about finishing their personal race for this year and EV adoption… less so about staying relevant in zero-emission states.

If It Ain’t Broke…
 
 
You know what they say?

"If it ain't broke, don't fix it" (just automate it)
 
And that's what CarOffer's PremiumXchange has done. They are already the largest, non-affiliated online pre-owned vehicle exchange - AKA the perfect solution for connecting underwater inventory with dealers in more appropriate markets..
 
How do you improve on something like that? Good question! Easy answer: Make it automatic!
 
Use the 12K+ strong network of dealers to simply share vehicles you need to get rid of, be seen by a dealer looking for those vehicles, and let PremiumXchange make automatic matches based on dealer preferences.
 
Simple, powerful, automatic. There really isn't much more to ask for!

The Rise Of Microfactories?
 
 
Who is Arrival? What is a Microfactory? Is southern England a real place? Lets find out together.
 
Arrival is one of man electric vehicle start-ups looking to capture part of the market before the big OEMs get their ships turned. Recently, the company completed its first electric van and started the process of raising funds to break into the US market.
 
Microfactories are what make "completing one vehicle" a bit of a newsworthy event. Arrival uses in-house tech and processes to create small-footprint, heavily automated factories. These microfactories will bring mass production anywhere big enough to fit a house when perfected.
 
South England is a real place. It is where Arrival currently operates.
 
Free idea -  1. Get this process down to a few hours. 2. Put it in a big transparent tube. 3. Make it like build-a-bear, but for electric cars.  4. Profit.
