Beaver Toyota Knows How To Party
 
 
People sometimes ask us when we will stop talking about Beaver Toyota of Cumming, and our answer is always the same - when they stop doing things that need to be shared.
 
Austin is a 9-year-old boy from Georgia on the autism spectrum. Austin's aunt shared part of his story in a state-wide call for birthday wishes, including a streak of birthdays where invitees did not attend his party.
 
When the request for birthday cards managed to find the lady leadership team at Beaver Toyota things started to change.
 
Just 4 hours after hearing the story, the Beaver team transformed their store into a dream birthday party. Gifts, balloons, candy, well wishes from every department in the store, and other children from a local school were waiting to celebrate Austin the way he deserved.
 
Telling it like it is — There are many things to say here, but ultimately they all boil down to uncontainable gratitude for Austin and each person who made this happen for him. #WhoDealersAre 🥳

Investments and Announcements
 
 
GM is always busy! Sometimes they invest in new things, other times they spend money on new ideas, and occasionally they drop huge loads of cash on future projects. They announced a $491M investment at its plant in Marion, Indiana, to prepare the facility to stamp steel and aluminum parts for future EVs.
 
Seriously though, in the last few months, we have covered a lot of GM investments, and it is beginning to feel like a jack-in-the-box about to pop. Any second. Any. Second. Now. 
 

Ford likes to keep everybody guessing. Questions like "Are you going to cut dealers out of the EV business?" and "How all-electric is your all-electric future?" for instance. A recently announced gas-powered 2024 Mustang is possibly the last ICE vehicle by the company.
 
While Ford's muscle rivals go electric, it will be interesting to see if their brand or power method captures more loyalty in the coming years.

Lincoln Prepares To Pivot
 
 
Lincoln is preparing to pivot its dealers to EVs but has no plan of offering buyouts like its peers. The company's president expects to end 2022 with around 85 fewer dealers than its current network of nearly 700.
 
The company says network consolidation will continue, but only voluntarily.
 
Telling it like it is — “Yeah, you can volunteer to meet our EV standards, or you can volunteer to sell some other brand.”

WORLD NEWS
Europe’s Not A Fan 👎
 
 
💢The EU Trade Commissioner has "Concerns" over e-car tax incentives in the US. Now the fun question is, if we take the political jargon and journalese out of this statement, what on earth does it mean?
 
👮The European Trade Commission says the US's new EV tax credit rules could violate World Trade Organization rules.
 
⛔The announcement comes after Tesla halted plans to produce EV batteries in Germany to comply with the new US standards.
 
🔍Valdis Dombruvskis, the EU Trade Commissioner, says his teams are going more in-depth to determine if and what rules have been broken.
 
Why it matters — The rapid OEM moves we've seen since the Inflation Reduction Act became law could be coming up to a new series of twists and turns. We will be sure to report on how they may continue to change the trajectory for Dealers here in the US.
MONEY AND MARKETS
Patagonia Goes Non-Profit
 
 
"Earth is now our only shareholder." said the letter sent to Patagonia associates to inform them the founder was transferring ownership to a non-profit. The nearly 50-year-old outdoor clothing company will pour all future earnings into organizations that combat climate change and protect the environment.
 
Speaking of Earth - The company is worth $3B and expects to invest $100M a year in climate protection…which is pretty smart since you need to have an outdoors to sell outdoors clothing. 😎
