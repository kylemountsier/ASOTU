Nissan Shared Slump 5%
 
 
French automaker Renault is considering lowering its stake in Nissan. News of the possible shake-up in the two-decade-old alliance led to Nissan shares dropping 5%, their most significant single-day losses since early March.
 
Nevermind? In January, the alliance announced plans to work closely together on EV developments. A $26B investment plan was made public that was indented for the next five years.
 
Telling it like it is —  Nissan said they would buy some of whatever Renault sells off, so now they will do so for a lower cost.

Robotaxis Come To Chinese Cities
 
 
Self-driving start-up Pony.ai says it has received the first city robotaxi license in China. The license allows pony.ai to operate 100 self-driving cars as taxis. Last year, the Toyota-backed start-up received permission for commercial operations in suburban districts, but received approval for city operations over the weekend. The company has tests ongoing in two other Chinese cities and California, with plans to expand in the next year.
 
Testing 1, 2, 3. The approval process for city operations required 24 months of liability traffic accident-free testing, among other factors.
 
Telling it like it is —  How many robotaxis does it take to shrink the biggest auto market in the world? Asking for a friend.

Rare NFT Sells For Over $1M
 
 
The Moonbirds NFT collection launched on April 16th and has clocked a total sales volume of nearly $360M. A recent member of the collection, Moonbird #2642, sold for 350 ETH, which translates to over $1M.
 
What makes these owls so special? Moonbirds garner special attention due to their relationship with Proof Collective. The collective backs the NFT series and includes members like Gary Vaynerchuk and NFT artist Beeple. Owners of a Moonbird gain access to the private PROOF discord and additional benefits from the collective as the token remains in their possession.
 
Telling it like it is —  Sometimes NFTs are fun pictures on the internet and sometimes they are the admission fee for joining an elite collective of movers and shakers. Just like baseball cards.

Musk To Buy Twitter
 
 
After weeks of back and forth, Twitter has agreed to be purchased by Elon Musk in a $44B deal. Shareholders will receive $54.20 per share. Twitter says they carefully considered the offer and concluded it was in the best interest of stockholders. The deal is expected to close this year.
