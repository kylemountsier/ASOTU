Ford Debuts New Ranger Raptor
 
 
Ford's high-performance Raptor nameplate will be added to an upcoming midsize Ranger pickup. Like the F-150 and Bronco Raptors before, the Ranger promises to offer excellent off-road performance.

Built Ford tough? Specifics have not yet been made public, but a press release shared that "the next-gen Ranger Raptor has been engineered with smarter technology controlling tougher hardware to create the most advanced Ranger ever." While Ford has not officially announced the Ranger Raptor will come to the U.S., some rumors have circulated that a stateside model with a 2.7-liter, twin-turbo V6 pumping out around 325 horsepower and 400 pound-feet of torque could go on sale in America.

Automakers Warn Dealers About Markups
 
 
Hyundai and Genesis have issued a warning to U.S. dealers against aggressive pricing strategies. They claim the practices such as markups that push the prices "way above" sticker are damaging the brand image. While acknowledging that retailers can set their own prices, the automakers warn of potential actions they may take against offending dealers such as reduction in future allocations.

The letter mentioned an eventual return to supply and demand equilibrium. A return to normal will allow customers to remember what brands did during the inventory shortages as a gauge for choosing where to spend money in the future.

 
Telling it like it is - If Hyundai knows the number that is fair and keeps the doors open we are all ears…

Ford to Transition Fleet Business to EV
 
 
The e-Transit design seeks to make the transition to EVs as pain-free as possible for long standing fleet customers. With a fully electric drivetrain, the high-volume Transit full-size van line was designed from the start to support existing fleets and the special equipment they have already customized their vans to support. Much like the upcoming F-150 Lightning, the Transit has left the interior completely unchanged.

Will it look like a spaceship? The body is of the same stampings as the gasoline and diesel versions, but it rides on a different chassis to accommodate a liquid-cooled battery pack (the same battery pack as the Mustang Mach-E). The smoother and quieter EV model will still pack 266-hp, maximum torque of 317 lb.-ft, and a gross weight of 9,500lbs.

Virgin Galactic Reports 2 Years of Losses
 
 
Space tourism company Virgin Galactic released financial records for Q4 2021 and results were grim. The company has yet to begin commercial service and in the last 2 years has lost $1 billion.

Calling it quits? Company officials have chosen to look on the bright side of the financial data reporting "We remain on track...We achieved many important milestones in 2021 that laid an essential foundation towards becoming a scaled, commercial operation." Issues do not seem to stem from lack of demand, but lack of capacity. The company is still in a test phase but expects to begin commercial operations late in 2022.

S&P 500 Confirms a Correction
 
 
The S&P 500 index is facing a correction for the first time since 2020. The correction is a term for marking a decline of 10% or more from a recent high mark. The most recent high was January 3rd and as of Tuesday's closing, the index was down 10.25%.

Cause or cure? A contributing factor to the drop could be attributed to rising interest rates. High interest rates tend to weigh on shares of high-growth companies by eroding the value of future earnings more than the value of earnings made in the short term.

Chinese Crypto Exchange to Re-enter the U.S.
 
 
Chinese cryptocurrency exchange Huobi is planning to re-enter the U.S. market more than two years after it ceased operations. The company left the U.S. market to comply with regulations and has spent several years under the tighter crypto regulations in China. The return to the U.S. market could put Huobi in competition with large exchanges such as Coinbase.
 
Huobi first launched a crypto exchange in the U.S. in 2018, but withdrew from the market the following year due to a lack of strong commitment to the market and no U.S.-based management team. They expect asset management to be a larger portion of their business than the exchange, which echoes the traditional market.
