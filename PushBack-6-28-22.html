If you are on day one of your automotive journey or one day away from retirement, the truth is somebody has inspired you, and chances are they have no idea.
 
A few quick rules to keep in your tool belt:
-Don't wait to express gratitude.
-Don't rush to express disappointment.
 
The retail automotive community is full of mentors, leaders, and those patient enough to invest in people for the long haul. Taking time to recognize those who have invested in us prepares us to continue the worthwhile discipline of building up others instead of tearing them down.
 
So today, take some time to say thank you… and when you’re done, let somebody know you got their back.

Policy, Tech, and Customer Desires
 
 
⛽ 🤝Japan released a drafted proposal seeking the removal of "zero-emission" goals from G7 plans moving forward. If adopted, removing the zero-emission language could have a widespread impact on policy and OEM actions moving forward. The political forum represents Germany, Italy, Japan, the UK, Canada, France, and the US's combined efforts.
 
🛻 💰American car shoppers remain undeterred. Gas prices are high, and EV adoption has seen an uptick. However, unlike the previous record high fuel prices in 2008, consumer habits show no sign of SUV and pickup abandonment. Data shows part of the persistence is fuel economy upgrades across brands and models, and some is attributed to consumers taking what they can get in tight supply times.
 
🚙 🤖Chinese internet giant Tencent has launched an all-in-one cloud product for domestic and overseas automakers in China. The software provides driver access to social media and map apps while collecting data for future autonomous driving systems. According to Liu Shuquan, VP of Tencent Intelligent Mobility, the company is already working with BWM and some US automakers. Liu says 40 brands and 120 vehicle models are already included in the partnership.
 
Tl;dr — Japan wants to keep making combustion engines for now, Americans are a little bit desensitized to expensive fuel, and a single Chinese tech company MAY monopolize in-car software (at least in China).

TROUBLEMAKER TUESDAY
Dale Pollak Inducted To Ring Of Honor
 
 
Troublemaker, author, innovator, Cox Automotive EVP, and Founder of vAuto, Dale Pollak, is the 33rd inductee to NIADA's Ring of Honor. The highest award for a non-dealer, the Ring of Honor, recognizes allied industry leaders who have contributed significantly to the auto industry.
 
Dale's work has improved and expanded the industry's view of inventory. Bringing an investment mindset and developing an extensive philosophy of used car retail, Dale has changed the face of the industry for the better.
 
The award was not only a sign to those watching but to Dale. He said the award addressed a longstanding insecurity, sharing that he was never a "car guy" like his dad. Now the work he has done from the time he was 16 working in his dad's dealership to now the award signifies to him that the real car guys have accepted him as a "true car guy."
 
Telling it like it is —  We have nothing but respect and appreciation for Dale and his work. A truer Troublemaker would be hard to find.
