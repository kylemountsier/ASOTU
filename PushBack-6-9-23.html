AUTOMOTIVE

Motor Morsels: Tesla, Hyundai, China, Lucid

Giphy/PBS

Musk-Golia. Elon Musk is adding globe-trotting to his resume. Rumors say a $4.5B investment may bring a Tesla factory to Spain , while in Mongolia , Musk is pointing at battery minerals and asking, "y'all using these?" to gauge interest in a battery plant. Of course, it isn't all Tesla. SpaceX's Starlink could be a win-win addition to the Musk-Golia (Musk and Mongolia) partnership.

Giphy/Rooster Teeth

Not Kiaboyz. Hyundai Motor America donated $50,000 to two Savannah organizations as part of its community investment strategy in Georgia. Savannah State University will receive $25K for STEM scholarships. Another $25K will aid child passenger safety and driver education programs at the Dwaine & Cynthia Willett Children's Hospital and Safe Kids Savannah.

Giphy/Emmys

More-More. Chinese leaders think the only thing better than more is more-more ! Following a 7.3% passenger vehicle sales increase in May, the country launched a nationwide campaign to stimulate automobile purchases and bolster demand in the world's largest auto market. The initiative will encourage lending for auto purchases and promote replacing gasoline cars with new energy vehicles. New EV incentives could lead to a wave of investments similar to the US after the Inflation Reduction Act or devastate the local bicycle market. It is anybody's guess.

Giphy/StickerGiant

Speaking of China... US luxury electric vehicle maker, Lucid Group , is preparing to enter the Chinese market, confirmed by the company's head of China operations, Zhu Jiang. While initially selling imported cars, Lucid is also considering local production in the country. This move comes as Lucid is set to raise about $3 billion through a stock offering.

TL; DR - Tesla may expand production to Spain and Mongolia, Hyundai is spreading education in Georgia, China incentivizes new-energy auto sales, and Lucid prepares to join the largest auto market in the world.

From Solar Power to Waspinator

The largest floating solar farm in North America, owned by NJR Clean Energy Ventures, is now online in New Jersey. The 8.9 MW array, covering 17 acres, powers 1,400 homes and 95% of a local water treatment plant's needs. The water-cooled panels increase efficiency and reduce evaporation so people can enjoy energy and save water.

You know who else likes water? Crocodiles! Like one nameless new mother in a Costa Rican zoo who, after 16 years in isolation, "gave birth" to a fully formed baby croc. It isn't the first " virgin birth " recorded in a similar situation. In 2018 an alligator in the US laid a similarly "populated" egg. Unfortunately, none of these fatherless hatchlings survived, but they have given scientists a lot to think about.
