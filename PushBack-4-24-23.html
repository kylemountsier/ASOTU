AUTOMOTIVE

Service Activity, Pre-Owned Sales, and Inventory up in Q1

Giphy/Reddit

🌱✂️

Unlike my neighbor Dave, data don't lie. Data also doesn't borrow your mower, then bring it back broken and insist "it was already like that." The industry had a March that, at the very least, has folks optimistic about Q2.

📈🔧📉

In March, service lane activity was up 15.9% month-over-month but down 4.3% year-over-year. However, in terms of revenue, the movement is reversed, with a 5.6% jump in revenue from March 2022 but down 0.5% from February to March.

🚗🦘

Despite a projected 6% decline, pre-owned sales jumped 4.2% in March 2023 vs. 2022 and 11% compared to February. Cost per repair order was up 10% vs. Q1 2022. 

Giphy/US National Archives

🏭👩‍🏭

Through Q1, most automakers had an increase in sales. Averaged-out sales increased about 7.5%, with only Toyota and Stellantis declining. Inventory, of course, has played a significant role in the ups and downs of recent years. Still, as the semiconductor shortage finally eases, inventory is on the mend. GM, for instance, has a 50% inventory rise in Q1 2023, marking a potential return to normal. 

Telling it like it is - Inventory is coming back, but it won’t undo the need to keep your service and pre-owned business strong.

Quick Bites: GM, VW, Ford, MINI and Tesla

Giphy/Cartoon Network

🍴 GM is adding a new trim to their Hummer EV line-up. The 3X is a powerful range upgrade for both the SUV and pickup models and offers the option to include an Extreme Off-Road Package (which we assume means you can opt-in for speedboat and/or personal plane capabilities). 🛩️

🍴 Volkswagen and Canada have committed to a $20B  investment for a massive battery gigafactory in Ontario, marking the single biggest investment ever in the country's EV supply chain. Groundbreaking is slated to begin in 2024. 🔋

🍴 Last week, Tesla suddenly decided to increase the cost of its older, slower-selling Model S and Model X vehicles by up to 2.9% following four US price cuts earlier this year. The Coolest Concept Cars from SAS 2023

Giphy/Columbia Pictures

Last week’s Shanghai Auto Show not only put the spotlight on many new production vehicles, it also featured several intriguing concept cars. Here are a few faves:

CFOTO/Future Publishing via Getty Images

BMW i Vision Dee Concept

This electric marvel features a slew of futuristic features like graphic windshield displays, a front grille that can mimic facial expressions, and BMW’s patented “E ink" technology that allows you to change the car's exterior color with the push of a button.

DiDi Neuron Concept

Chinese ride-sharing company DiDi revealed an autonomous-taxi prototype that features tons of interior space and seats facing each other. The prototype is also equipped with robotic limbs that can assist passengers with things like loading luggage and picking up items from the floor.
