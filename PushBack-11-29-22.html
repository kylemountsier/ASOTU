At one point in my life, I didn’t drink coffee. It was literally spit-worthy in my book. Then I started hanging out with a friend named Brian at our local coffee shop. He would explain to me the history, taste profiles, and tea-like qualities of the coffee put out by our local roaster. Brian had a passion for coffee that I had never met before. Nothing like the ‘Double Shot No Whip Venti Caramel Macchiato' style of passion I had known most of my life (though I love you for it if that is you).
 
Long story short, I am now just as, if not more, passionate about coffee. I literally do research before I go to any city to find the best local roaster with the highest quality beans and brew style available.
 
Lesson: people follow other passionate people. What say you auto industry? Got something to share about what we do every day that may just impact the community around you?
 
Keep Telling Your Story,
-Kyle Mountsier

Energy Innovation In France
 
 
Carbon neutrality means a bit more than switching away from gas-powered cars.
 
Renault wants to be carbon neutral in Europe by 2040 and globally by 2050. The company is exploring new power options at an old plant in Douai, France - geothermal!
 
Oh, cool, so they are building a volcano 🌋? No, that's more Bond villain-y than the reality of geothermal power. By drilling down about 13K feet, the factory will draw energy from the hot water below the Earth's crust.
 
Drilling will begin in 2023, with carbon-free heating and electricity production starting after 2025.
 
Telling it like it is - Manufacturing is a great way to use geothermal energy, but imagine drinking coffee brewed using the heat at the earth's core. ☕️☕️☕️

Tesla’s Semi Gets Up To Speed (And Range And Weight Capacity)
 
 
Tesla's Semi has completed its first full trip.
 
What is a full trip? The 500-mile, 81,000-lb load trip is a critical milestone for the adoption of all-electric semi technology because it aligns the mandatory driver rest period with the truck's recharge period. 
🚛🤠💤 
Deliveries of the Tesla Semis are set to begin on December 1. They are expected to significantly impact the trucking industry because of the lower operating costs.
 
Telling it like it is - And if the driver turns the radio down it may even make it 501 miles!
