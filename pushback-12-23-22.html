December 23, 2022
 
We are continually enamored by the dealer community. Check it out.
 
Earlier this week at the Year End Extravaganza, we heard a theme of excitement and anticipation for 2023. Whether dealer or industry partner, it seems this industry continually approaches it with a ‘chin up’ mentality, focusing on opportunity rather than despair.
 
We know that 2022 threw some unique challenges at dealers and there was immense thriving throughout the industry. If you watch the news (which...we do), you could be led to believe that things are doom and gloom. Our theory, the auto business is going to find ways to make sure we continually make lemonade out of lemons. 
 
There have also been more and more stories being shared about the community work dealers around the country are doing. The holidays are a special time for that and we have highlighted a few recent ones below. We would always love to hear what your stores or dealers you know are doing in their communities!
 
Lastly, for the first time in a year, our team will take a pause on Monday from delivering an email to your inbox. We want the team to take time this weekend to be with family and not have to peruse the news. We hope you share in that time and can’t wait to wake up Tuesday morning to all the stories of how many cars everyone sold on the 26th!
 
We love this ASOTU fam and are grateful for you this holiday season for being willing to be a part of the shift in culture and perception of this great industry! Have a Merry Christmas!
 
Keep Pushing Back,
-Paul J Daly, Kyle Mountsier, and Chris Reeves
DEALERS
The Holiday Spirit
 

Apple Autos celebrated the season of giving by joining the effort to give 12 vehicles to 12 single mothers in their community. White Bear Mitsubishi, The Beat Lot, Friendly Chevrolet, Gilleland Chevrolet, Miller Auto Plaza & Volkswagen of Inver Grove joined to give the cars through the Wheels for Women program at Newgate Automotive Training School.

Fully loaded! Each vehicle was delivered in time for Christmas and packed with home goods, toys, NBA tickets, gift cards, photoshoots, service, and whatever else the dealers could pack in to bless the families.

We never use bad language in this email, but just this one time, we will say it - wow-wee, that's freakin' awesome as heck!


Ourisman Automotive Group hosted holiday luncheons for associates and raffled gifts. Then turned their attention and generosity outward to give back. Ninety Eight $25 gift cards were delivered to the Montgomery County Coalition for the Homeless. The donations will help the nonprofits care for their residents.

Is there a better time of the year to remind people down on their luck that they are not alone? 


Toyota of Cool Springs delivered its first pile of collected food to Lewisburg, TN's HOPEtown. The nonprofit cares for people's specific need: Food, clothing, housing, programs for children, etc. As Frank Sullivan put it, "Whatever people are in need of, that's what we're interested in."

The folks at Toyota of Cool Springs will continue to collect food and home goods through December 31 for the community group. 

In a perfect world, we would not need people willing to stand "in the gap" and serve whatever needs they encounter. In our imperfect world, at least we get to see and join these beautiful expressions of charity from time to time. 


Group 1 Automotive went all hands on deck to wrap gifts for their adopted family. Through the group Kid's Meals, Group 1 Automotive participated in the Adopt-A-Family program to bless their community with enough gifts to give Santa and at least 4 of his reindeer back problems.

Every year I am reminded that wrapping isn't some effortless ending to the gift, but the most challenging part of the experience. These people are saints. -Chris


Beaver Toyota Of Cumming let us look in as they blessed their 3rd Christmas Wish family. Over $2,500 in gift cards and support for groceries, bills, preschool tuition, and a pile of gifts for each child. At the end of a challenging year, a mother and her five children were given a Christmas worthy of the important and wonderful people they are. 

It is hard to imagine a more perfect gift to give a parent than to help her give a better year to her children. Amazing.
CULTURE
A Smorgasbord of Stuff
 
Before the long weekend, we wanted to give a quick overview of a few corners of the news we keep in mind.
 

Crypto may be more alive than almost everybody previously thought. While FTX founders are pleading guilty to fraud, investors say Bitcoin is primed to enter a new season of volatility. A period of calm for bitcoin amid macroeconomic movement signals traders approaching turbulence - which doesn't mean up or down, just big and moving. 


Metaverse is still Meta's focus despite finding it "Harder than we expected." The company will keep the project at around 20% of its total investments. Meta is not the only Metaverse game in town. Other companies and creators are still exploring it as the future of artistic expression, education, social media, and even dating.


Ukraine's President visited Washington DC to deliver a speech just as Russian losses in the conflict reached a staggering 100K. Putin called the visit to the US a mistake by Zelensky. Meanwhile, the US has sent more money and equipment to Ukraine for defense efforts and a pledge to support them "as long as it takes."

Telling it like it is - We didn’t forget these topics we just figured everybody was as fatigued by them as we are. Here is a snapshot- now try to ignore the news until Monday, friends.
