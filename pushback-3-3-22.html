Ford is Betting on Dealers
 
 
Ford CEO Jim Farley is dividing up company operations. The shift aims to make the 118-year-old company more competitive against new EV competitors in the industry such as Tesla. In addition to the new approach to the business, Farley shared Ford is "Committed to the dealer model" and that Dealers will play an essential role in beating Tesla in customer experience. 

 Divide and conquer?
  • Under CEO Ted Cannis, Ford Pro will maintain the existing commercial arm: distributing, selling, hardware, and software solutions for dealers.
  • Under Kumar Golhotra, Ford Blue will handle the traditional ICE business. 
  • Under Jim Farley, Ford Model e will deal with EVs.

Farley went on to say that the company is not simply keeping dealers "in the mix for the moment," Ford is "betting on dealers for the long term."

Telling it like it is - A bet on dealers ain't even a gamble, it's an investment. 
AUTOMOTIVE
TradePending Acquires AutoAPR
 
 
Dealership vehicle valuation and merchandising software provider TradePending announced they had acquired AutoAPR, a website engagement tool provider. Hundreds of dealers around the country already use AutoAPR's website tools. Existing employees of AutoAPR will join the TradePending team, including CEO Dan Mayer and CTO Daniel Congrove. 

TradePending CEO Brice Englert called the acquisition the company's first step toward a fast track innovation in the automotive software industry. Both companies are excited to work together and foresee significantly increasing their value to dealers.

 
Telling it like it is - If 2 companies with names with no spaces come together, we figure the collaboration prospects must be just as in sync as their names suggest.
AUTOMOTIVE
Rivian Increases EV Truck Price by 20%
 
 
Rivian announced they are raising the price of their R1T Truck and R1S SUV. The 17-20% increase brings prices to $79,000 for the R1T and $84,500 for the R1S. Pre-orders that have begun production will not increase, but all other pre-orders will.  

Options? The company will refund the $1,000 deposit or allow the customer to choose a downgraded model with a standard battery. The standard battery has a 260-mile range and lowers the price by $12,000. However, neither the standard nor the newly priced options will be available until 2024. 

Rivian has blamed inflation for the steep price hike, but many customers feel tricked and outraged.

MARKETS
Amazon Says Bye to Bookstores
 
 
Over the past few years, many have pointed to Amazon's return to brick and mortar book stores as a herald to their belief in the concept. Until yesterday. Amazon confirmed they are moving toward closing all 68 retail Amazon Books stores.
 
The Good Side. The retailer has stated they will be working hard to find jobs within the organization for all impacted employees. They also stated they are strengthening their resolve on ensuring the success of their Whole Foods, Amazon Fresh, Amazon Style, and Amazon Go locations.

CRYPTO
NFTs Step in as Modern War Bonds
 
 
Crypto-art pioneers and long-time activists are using NFTs and digital tools to create and sell art with the explicit purpose of supporting Ukraine, and people are happy to have a way to contribute to the cause. The Ukrainian government recently started accepting cryptocurrency donations to fund ongoing efforts to repeal the Russian invasion. In the five days since contributions began, folks raised over $22M, including $3M through NFTs. 

Better than cash? Aleksandra Artamonovskaja of the organization RELI3F explained, "The power of Web3 allows money to be distributed in the most effective and transparent way possible," Making Crypto and NTFs a powerful way for individuals to offer financial support collectively that bypasses traditional costs of moving money. One company, PartyBid, even allows users to collectively bid on NFT art, receiving a piece of the ownership by adding to a more significant financial contribution they otherwise would not have been able to make.
 
Telling it like it is - Why are we bypassing existing financial institutes during war? It's transparent and efficient. Oh, does that mean it would be better during peace too? No comment…
UKRAINE
Ukraine Isn’t Giving up
 
 
Ukraine's second-largest city, Kharkiv, is holding firm after the third day of heavy bombardment by Russian invaders. The frontline, just 30 miles from the Russian border, remains under firm Ukrainian control by the great effort of Ukrainian defenders. While Moscow says it is not targeting civilians, damage to civilian infrastructure is widespread. 

"We have understood their tactic: they can't enter the city because every time they try, we hit them in the teeth," Kharkiv's deputy governor, Roman Semenukha, said in a TV interview. As the fighting continues more nations, companies, and people are finding ways to drive the best resolution.
