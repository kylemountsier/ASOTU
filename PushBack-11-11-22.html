Power and Parking Lots
 
 
We all remember a few months ago when California announced a gas-powered vehicle ban as of 2035. Then, as if written by a comedy team, it asked EV owners not to charge their cars during a heatwave. The reality is California, other states, the US, and many other nations are still preparing to power their vision of a carbon-neutral future.
 
 
POWER! The US generated and consumed about 4.12 trillion kWh of energy in 2021, a 500% increase from the 1960s. 2022 is on track to be a record year for energy consumption due to economic growth and rising temperatures. Experts suggest a shift to electric vehicles will increase the national need for power by 30%, or 1.25T kWh a year.
 
 
Updated, New, and Renewable. Plans to meet this rising energy need are expected to come from renewable sources like wind and solar. Part of the effort to cover the rising energy need is in the Inflation Reduction Act. In addition to an $11.7B set aside to issue new loans for energy infrastructure upgrades, the act will cover 30% of residential solar systems between 2022 and 2032.
 
 
Fun fact. As of May, Honolulu, Las Vegas, San Diego, Albuquerque, and San Jose were the top 5 solar energy producers in the US. Very sunny places. With the Build Back Better Act passing in August, more money will be invested in clean energy, which could shake up this top 5 with places like... we don't know... anywhere on the East Coast or Gulf?
 
 
One idea. French President Macron plans to double wind and 10x solar power production. This week the French Senate approved legislation requiring all parking lots with space for at least 80 vehicles to be covered by solar panels. Carparks will have between 3-5 years, depending on size, to comply with the new solar requirement.
 
So why tell all this to people in a dealership? Innovation is trying something new. Knowing more about the cultural, political, and economic waters we swim means those innovative new ideas more often propel us in the direction of our customers.
 
Telling it like it is - Great idea France! Next we should cover up speed walking tracks. Nobody wants to watch that anyway.
