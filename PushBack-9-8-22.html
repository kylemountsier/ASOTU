Brazil, Software, and Rumors
 
 
Mercedes-Benz will lay off 3,600 employees in restructuring its truck and bus chassis plant in Sao Paulo, Brazil. The company hopes to outsource some of the plant's operations to address rising costs and the changing industry. 

Stellantis has appointed Chris Taylor as its new Chief Digital Information Officer. Never heard of that position? Well, that is because it was created to help the No. 4 carmaker transform into a mobility tech company. The company is aiming for nearly $20B in annual software-related revenue by 2030.

Ford says the next-gen Mustang will NOT be a hybrid. Following rumors that the seventh-generation S650 Mustang will have a hybrid drivetrain, three anonymous sources told Automotive News it will not be the case.

TL: DR — Mercedes is restructuring production, Stallantis is restructuring leadership, and Ford is restructuring people talking about things they don’t know.

French Fries to the Rescue
 

The Nullarbor plain is an expansive, mostly treeless, region along the coast of southern Australia. As a chunk of Outback between two EV charging networks, until recently, there was no way for an EV to cross its nearly 450 miles. 🌵🦘

You said something about French fries 🍟? Some locations along the networkless stretch have begun offering deep fryer oil-powered charging stations.

Problem solved! Well, maybe, but reviews say the cost of charging is a little high, a little slow, and subject to failure in the cold ⛄. So while spreading the ever-growing need for energy across more sources is good, reliability in remote regions is also pretty good to have.

Telling it like it is — Turning waste into energy is like sustainable+.
