Cox See Used Car Prices Declining Further
 
 
🚗🚙 Measured from December to December, used car prices dropped 14.9% in 2022.
 
🚕🚓 Cox Automotive expects prices to continue down (another 4.3% by year-end) amid rising interest rates and improved new inventory levels.
 
🏎️🛻 However, the drops are not enough to offset the 88% rise in index pricing between April 2020 and January 2022.
 
Telling it like it is - Great news for hesitant buyers and the dealers they buy from, but a potential threat to profits since many of these cars are losing value in the dealer's inventory. It is always time to take care of that used car day supply, but it seems like now it is time to take care of that used car day supply.

BMW Announces New Subscription Features
 
 
BMW has announced some more subscription-based features. A few follow the trend of locking prominent features behind paywalls, while others offer a new take on existing concepts. 
  • Remote Engine Start has been around for a while across brands. Now you can start yours remotely for a fee. Or just ask your kids to go start the car and accomplish the same thing. 
  • Drive Recorder does exactly that, records your drive with onboard cameras. Add a soundtrack, and *POW* you're a filmmaker. 
  • Traffic Camera warns drivers about fixed or mobile speed cameras, which doesn't play into any BMW owner stereotypes. 
  • Driving Assistance Plus with Stop&Go keeps you shuffling ahead in traffic without fender benders in the front or angry honks in the back. Precisely like a mullet. 
  • Parking Assistance Professional parks the car for you, but unlike its non-professional counterparts, it will park the car hood first. A problem we didn't even know existed or needed a solution!

Most features have monthly, yearly, 3-year, and outright ownership prices except Traffic Camera, which must be paid for by the $25 month.



Telling it like it is - Honestly, if this worked backward, it would do a lot for affordability issues. “How much can I save if I don’t want windows? Ok, cool… and what about passenger seats?”

OEMs and Skyrim
 
 
Sometimes we read the news and follow our imagination, as LeVar Burton taught us on Reading Rainbow. Today, for some reason, our imagination took us to 2011’s Skyrim. We are not complaining.
 
 
Tevva has moved its 7.5-ton electric delivery truck from concept to mass production. Its 140-mile range and 5ish-hour recharge time interest last-mile and urban delivery providers. The company has a hydrogen-electric truck planned for later this year—the hydrogen feature extends the range to 354 miles and takes about 10 minutes to refill.
 
Many of us got our first taste of what mass production means at the anvil in Whiterun. So many daggers… so few bandits. 🔨
 
 
Chevy's Corvette E-Ray will have a steal mode feature. Which, as it sounds, will make the already quiet electric powertrain operate even more quietly. Just how quiet? We don't know. Full details will come later this month.
 
Sneaking is fundamental to adventuring. Until it isn’t. 🪓
 
 
Lamborghini broke its sales record in 2022, and it sold out until 2024. 9,233 Lambos were sold worldwide, a 10% increase over the previous best year. More than half were the Urus SUV.

VW: Warning and Greenpeace
 
 
Volkswagen is sounding an alarm about 2023.
 
Yesterday they reported a 6.8% drop in sales in 2022 and warned that they expect the effects of the chip shortage to persist through 2023.
 
💾 Not for lack of trying or demand. Order books are up 18% in Europe, and VW is working to deliver, but the invasion of Ukraine has added complications to complications across the continent.
 
At home in Germany, Volkswagen is facing a possible court case.
 
👩‍⚖️ Sending a message? A Greenpeace lead suit would demand the automaker tighten its emission reduction targets to include a ban on ICE cars and cut emissions by 65% from 2018 levels by 2030. A German district court said the case was admissible but unlikely to succeed.
 
 
TL: DR - VW would like you to lower your expectations for 2023, and they would like Greenpeace to lower their expectations of emission reductions.

Recycled and Electric Roads
 

Plastic roads are passing tests in 5 states. 
  • No microplastic run-off so far.
  • Reuses hard-to-recycle forms of plastic.
  • It uses plastic in an asphalt mixture, so the formula can vary. 

Some successful instances include a road in Hawaii containing about 150K water bottles worth of plastic and two quarter-mile roads in Pennsylvania that likewise use plastic equivalent to nearly 150K plastic bags.

Wait long enough, and throwing plastic trash from your moving car window won't even be littering anymore, just routine maintenance! What a strange world we live in.


Electreon, a company in Israel, has developed technology to charge EVs wirelessly while they drive.

It sounds kinda simple, actually... Copper coils installed under the road and another attached to the bottom of the vehicle create a magnetic field that charges the vehicle's battery. It's the same way wireless phone chargers work. 
  • Balingen, Germany, will begin testing the tech on a 1000-yard road frequented by electric city buses.
  • Michigan will begin testing two short stretches of the tech later this year.
  • Several other US states are considering tests of the technology within the next few years. 

It’s good to recharge while you drive, it's why we only listen to bluegrass in the car. What about you?
