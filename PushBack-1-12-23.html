Better Help With Mercedes-Benz of Easton
 
 
Back in December, Mercedes-Benz of Easton started offering access to the therapy app Better Help for all associates.
 
GM Daniel Govaer said the store's managers realized in their 1:1 meetings with associates that Covid had left a lingering stress in the team. When the managers themselves tried to help, associates set up therapy through company-provided insurance, and the stress only increased.
 
Much of the info available was outdated, some doctors listed were retired, and more challenges in the initial setup quenched the motivation and courage people needed to seek the help they needed.
 
Since handling the initial setup and covering all the costs with Better Help, 12% of the store's associates have started using it, with an average 5/5 rating when asked if they would recommend the app to a friend and when asked about their therapists.
 
Mercedes-Benz of Easton has set their people on the path to a more mentally healthy life that will, without a doubt, become a more delightful experience for their customers.
 
Telling it like it is - Seems like work/life balance isn’t really just a time thing, eh?

Tesla’s New Investment
 
 
Tesla has a $770M expansion planned for its Texas gigafactory.
 
The addition will add a battery cell testing lab, cathode and drive unit manufacturing facilities, a die shop, and something called "Cell 1" to the already massive Austin factory.
 
Some construction will begin in the next few weeks.
 
Cell 1? Yeah, we don't know, either. It is a 693,093 square foot, $368M space of undisclosed purpose.
 
Telling it like it is - If you've seen Footloose, you know sometimes a guy needs ample undisclosed space to "express" his frustration. Maybe that's where Musk gets his ideas.

Quick Looks: NIO, Polestar, BYD
 
 
🥇📦🗓️
NIO set a new delivery record in 2022. At 16K deliveries, the company's December sales increased year over year by 50.8%. With 289,556 lifetime deliveries, the company is about 10K short of projections, despite the record year. 
🤝♻️🧲
Polestar is partnering with Cyclic Materials to increase magnate recycling efforts. By creating a circular supply chain for its rare earth magnets, the company will reduce the overall environmental impact of EV production. Historically, magnet recycling has not been a priority, but it will be essential in a circular supply chain. 
🤵‍♀️🇮🇳🔋
BYD is getting fancy in India this year. The company's third passenger EV will launch in India at the end of 2023. BYD Seal will be an all-wheel drive luxury sedan with a 435-mile range. They hope to corner 40% of India's EV market by 2030, still yet to mention sales in the US.

TECHNOLOGY
Stuff From CES
 
Dishonorable mentions. A fun CNET article focused on the bad, the worst, and the ugly (sorry, Mr. Eastwood).
 
This year, Dyson's Zone headphones made CNET's list. A combo personal music listening device and sci-fi covid mask. Literally, something in the background in the movie Blade Runner. Fun!
 
 
Look who's talking. Edmund's list of top car-tech features BMW's talking car. The Digital Emotional Experience, or "DEE," is a system "Far beyond the level of voice control and driver assistance we are familiar with today."
 
The i Vision Dee also impressed people with an advanced heads-up display with virtual reality features projected on the windshield. Apparently, the car can also change colors at will thanks to 240 e-ink panels and "gets excited when it sees you," which it will express with a screen on its front grill. So it's like a cute, high-tech, terrifying, chameleon-puppy-butler, car. Neat!
 
 
A 2nd opinion. Good Housekeeping's list actually had the Dyson Zone Headphones on their list of top picks, so to each their own. Further down their list was the Ring Car Cam.
 
Ring has become a popular name for its doorbell cameras, and the new car cam is sure to capture just as many odd moments. The small device has a front and cab-facing camera, night vision, motion detection, and a two-way talk feature. So if somebody is putting a parking ticket on your car, you can at least ask them not to. Smart!
 
TL: DR - Dyson is adding vacuums to headphones and shoes, BMW is adding AI to its cars, and Ring is adding doorbell cameras to your 1992 Toyota Corolla.
