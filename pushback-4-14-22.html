Carmax Released Q4 Data
 
 
The online automotive retail company, Carmax, reported a 5.2% drop in used car sales but a 33% increase in profits during Q4 2021 due to average retail prices . The year ended with revenue up 49% but earnings down 28%.
 
BUY. EVERY. THING. Carmax reported buying 1.4M cars in 2021, a 95.5% increase year over year. The company used its online instant appraisal tool for about half of the vehicles.
 
Telling it like it is —  The data is clear that individuals are selling their cars online more than ever. As David Long would say, “How do we future-proof ourselves?”

Lucid Debuts Latest Tesla Rival
 
 
Luxury automaker Lucid has released a new edition of its Air EV. The Air Grand Touring has 1,050 HP, a 446-mile battery range, and runs just under $200K.
 
Hot off the presses. The company announced about 25,000 reservations for the new Air and expects to produce 14K vehicles in 2022. Unlike the original Air model, the Grand Touring will not have a capped reservation. 
 
Other things you could buy with $200K  —  4 Teslas, 7 horses, or 168K taco bell tacos. Choose wisely.

Chinese Car Market In Disarray
 
 
China has implemented various lockdowns in response to the region's worst covid outbreak in 2 years. As the world's largest car market and a production hub, these lockdowns have production and sales numbers dwindling.
 
Is there any end in sight? Some lockdowns are in an indefinite timeline and will be lifted as covid numbers lower. Automakers in the regions are preparing to deal with the backlog of supply production in several ways, with some inviting employees to lockdown IN the factories and others planning to open extra shifts as soon as the lockdowns lift.
 
Telling it like it is —   Some studies say lockdowns are a fast track to crushing covid numbers, let’s hold hope the people of China get stability soon. For them and the industry.

Hacker Forum Seized
 
 
The US has seized the domain and indicated the founder of Raidforums.com. The site is one of the largest hacker forums in the world and provides a marketplace for the buying and selling of hacked or stolen data.
 
Score one for the good guys! Found in the investigation were usernames and passwords for eCommerce companies, broadcasting and cable companies, and private telecommunication accounts. In addition to the US, Europol and law enforcement from several European countries worked on the case.
 
Telling it like it is —  Cybercrime is big money, these sorts of joint efforts are only going to become more common and more important.
