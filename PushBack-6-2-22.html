Dealership Values At An All Time High
 
 
The Haig Report for trends in auto retail for Q1 2022 shows dealership value is at a record high. Driven by high profits and many dealer groups looking to expand their network, the average share price of a franchise retail dealership is up 3% so far in 2022.
 
A rising tide? The increase in dealership value stands in stark contrast to the 15% drop in the S&P 500.
 
Telling it like it is —  Practicing your best habits on the good days means they are already well practiced for the challenging days. I think we all agree these are some pretty good days.
AUTOMOTIVE
Supercharged Tesla News
 
 
Tesla is the dominant name in the EV world. Many OEMs are looking to the Tesla EV footprint as the benchmark for entering the EV market successfully. For a time, people used Tesla and EV interchangeably. Staying aware of Tesla makes sense for the whole industry.
 
Self-Driving tech has driven one California man to the unthinkable- running for office. Software company engineer and CEO Dan O'Dowd is running for office to keep full self-driving software beta testing off public roads. The campaign distinguishes between Autopilot and FSD, saying the experimental FSD software gives too much control to the computer without the driver's input.
 
The world's largest casting machine is coming to Tesla to produce Cybertruck bodies. Called the Giga Press, the device upgrades upon its 2020 predecessor. The machine will fulfill a previous announcement by Tesla that the Cybertruck will be built using a cast stainless-steel exoskeleton. Production should start in 2023.
 
Everybody's favorite time machine, the DeLorean, is returning as an EV. Some say the iconic car's Texas-based resurrection is a stylistic competitor for Tesla's Model S. Others point out that the Roadster has more power. Either way, the relaunch has drawn excited comparisons to Tesla before the first DeLorean has left the plant.
AUTOMOTIVE
Britain Facing Battery Challenges
 
 
Post-Brexit trading rules and a lack of suitable "gigafactory" sites threaten Britain's push for EV adoption. Experts say the country needs six large plants to sustain a healthy car industry but currently only has one small Nissan plant. Fear of losing the entire vehicle production industry and 170K jobs in the region has lawmakers working toward a solution.
 
Solutions? About $1.2B in funds allocated by the government seek to support the supply chain. Battery Startup, Britishvolt, and Nissan are working on large battery plants. People still wonder if current planning is enough to make a timely difference.

WORLD NEWS
Israel And UAE Enter Free-Trade Agreement
 
 
Less than two years after formal diplomatic relations were established in a US-brokered deal, Israel and the United Arab Emirates have signed a free-trade agreement. The agreement is the first of its kind between Israel and an Arab country. Officials say it will take a few weeks to process, publish, and ratify the deal fully. Experts claim the deal could grow ten-fold in value within five years.
 
Implications? Many see the deal as a sign the decades of tension between Israel and its Arab neighbors is easing. The impact on regional and global trade is yet to be fully understood as more technology, equipment, medical and agricultural supplies moving freely between the nations could have unmeasurable positive results further down the road.
 
Telling it like an after-school special —  Remember kids, for better or worse, when you trade with a nation you also trade with every nation that nation trades with.
