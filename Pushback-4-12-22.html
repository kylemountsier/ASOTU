Toyota Adjusts Inventory Goals
 
 
Toyota announced they would adjust their inventory day supply target from the 40s to 30. Having adapted to low inventory, North American sales chief Bob Carter said they have learned to be more efficient and see no reason to return to the 45-day plan once the supply chain stabilizes. 

Telling it like it is —  We all learned new tricks during the pandemic. Some of us learned to make bread and others restructured the inventory strategy of international corporations.

Nissan Partners With NASA For Battery Development
 
 
Nissan and NASA are working together to develop all-solid-state batteries. The partnership aims to eliminate the need for rare and expensive metals in future energy solutions.
 
No shortage of confidence. Despite being early in the development stage, a pilot solid-state plant will open in 2024. Nissan hopes to debut the tech in passenger vehicles by 2028.
 
Telling it like it is —  “Collaboration is rocket-fuel in the engine of innovation” - Henry Ford, probably.
AUTOMOTIVE
CMA CGM To Acquire GEFCO
 
 
The French shipping company, CMA CGM, announced they would acquire the Russian automotive logistics firm GEFCO, pending competition authority approval. The European Commission expects to approve the estimated $488M deal within a few months. 
 
GEFCO.
  • 75% controlled by state-owned Russian Railways.
  • 25% owned by carmaker Stellantis.
  • Employees 11,500 people in 47 European countries. 
Telling it like it is —  This Russia state-owned asset yard sale brought to you by sanctions?

Elon Musk Teases Mining Operation
 
 
Elon Musk tweeted Friday that his company Tesla "might actually have to get into mining & refining" due to the price of lithium. The cost of the metal is 18 times higher today than in 2012. Musk points out there is no shortage of the element itself, but the process for extraction and refinement is slow.
 
MineX. The company has revealed no official plans. People have pointed out Musk's history of tweeting before going all-in on new projects and are eager to see what happens next.

Gig Economy Vs. Gas Prices
 
 
Rising gas prices have impacted businesses and individuals alike, but driving gig workers feel double-tapped. Uber, Lyft, and some delivery company workers have asked for additional fuel compensation. Uber and Lyft drivers say a recent fuel surcharge added to customer fees has not done enough to offset the price.
 
The usual suspects. Currently, an Uber ride costs 50% more than before the pandemic. Covid, supply crunches, and the Russian invasion contribute to rising costs. Debates are ongoing as to who should foot the bill.
