Customers Prefer Dealerships
 
 
A survey of over 1,200 people found consumers are counting on automakers to offer both online AND in-person buying options for EVs.
 
74% of respondents, ranging from folks looking to buy an EV to those resistant to switching from ICE, say they would prefer a dealership buying experience.
 
Why? According to the report, specific experience expectations, discussing the sale with a real person, and an existing relationship when the time comes for service all factor into respondent answers.
 
Telling it like it is -  At the end of the day, what the consumer wants is the tie-breaking vote. 100 years of trust and loyalty have customer favor on dealers. Now, what needs to be done to deserve it 100, 10, and even 1 year in the future?

GM Invests In Battery Recycling
 
 
GM wants to know if you're done with that battery now? No? That's ok. They'll wait.
 
GM has invested in a partnership with Canadian battery recycling company Lithion Recycling.
 
The undisclosed amount will finance Lithion's efforts to open its first commercial plant next year and fund joint battery processing R&D.
 
The flagship recycling plant will process 7,500 tons a year and eventually be joined by more plants in the US, Europe, and South Korea.
 
Lithion's goal of 95% recovery will lead to cheaper battery production for GM, a 75% cut in mining emissions, and a 90% drop in water consumption.
 
Telling it like it is - Sure, when GM recycles batteries it’s cool, but when we do it it’s all “Who took the batteries out of the TV remote?”

VW Secures Canadian Battery Material
 
 
VW's PowerCo has a new partnership to produce battery material for 2.2M EVs. The 100% VW-owned PowerCo was established in July and has started construction of the first of its 6 planned battery plants.
 
The partnership with Umicore has secured cathode active battery materials, the most costly components in an EV, for PowerCo to begin turning into batteries. Which will keep VW EVs in compliance with tightening US EV tax credit standards.
 
Telling it like it is - VW is closer to their “Over take Tesla by 2030” goal, yes, but they are also better friends with Canada…which is its own reward.

Walmart Enters The Metaverse
 
 
Walmart would like to welcome you, and more so your kids, to the future. (where nothing makes sense anymore)
 
Walmarts first two metaverse experiences will be in the gaming platform, Roblox. Walmart Land and Walmart's Universe of Play are among several experiments aimed at attracting shoppers to the retail giant.
 
Post-Covid. Following consumer behavior changes during the pandemic, Walmart is looking to social media, apps, and gaming as the possible future of customer engagement. Of course, based on the medium, special attention is being paid to Gen Z.
 
TL;DR - At Walmart, you can buy socks, a blender, a month's supply of groceries, and a tent that sleeps 20, but they are still switching it up to connect with Gen Z. We wonder what that means for auto retail?
